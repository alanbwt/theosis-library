Texts that have waited centuries for an English reader.

The Patrologia Graeca alone contains 161 volumes of Greek Christian writing. The Patrologia Latina runs to 221. The vast majority of these texts have never been rendered into English. They exist only in the original Greek and Latin, accessible to a diminishing number of scholars and invisible to everyone else.

Theosis Library is a sustained effort to change that. We publish new English translations of early Christian texts, presented in parallel with the original language, fully annotated, and freely available. Every translation links to the source edition so that any reader, anywhere, can check our work.

The project takes its name from the Greek concept of theosis (θέωσις): divinization, the participation of humanity in the divine nature. This idea stands at the centre of the earliest Christian debates. Is divinity the exclusive property of Christ, or does a spark of the divine reside within all beings? The Church Fathers and the Gnostic teachers they opposed both grappled with this question. Their arguments, preserved in the texts collected here, remain among the most penetrating explorations of the boundary between the human and the sacred ever committed to writing.

Published

  • The Book of Various Heresies, Chapters 29–43: The Gnostic Schools

    Filastrius of Brescia · d. c. 397 AD · Latin · First English translation
    Fifteen chapters covering every major Gnostic school known to the late fourth-century Church: the Simonians, Basilideans, Valentinians, Marcosians, Carpocratians, and the Barbelo-Gnostics. Parallel Latin and English. No previous English translation exists.

Forthcoming

  • Refutation of All Heresies, Book VI, Chapters 24–37

    Hippolytus of Rome · c. 170–235 AD · Greek
    The Valentinian theology in full: the divine Pleroma, the emanation of the aeons, the fall of Sophia, the Demiurge, and the divine spark imprisoned within humanity.

  • Against Heresies, Book I

    Irenaeus of Lyon · c. 130–202 AD · Latin
    The earliest systematic account of Gnostic theology. Irenaeus catalogues Valentinian, Marcosian, and other Gnostic systems in extraordinary detail before arguing against them.

  • Against the Valentinians

    Tertullian · c. 155–220 AD · Latin
    Tertullian at his most caustic: a satirical attack on Valentinian cosmology that preserves, in the act of mockery, a detailed portrait of beliefs the Church sought to erase.

  • Against Arius, Book I

    Marius Victorinus · c. 290–364 AD · Latin
    A Neoplatonist philosopher, converted in old age, brings the full apparatus of Platonic metaphysics to the question of Christ's divinity. Virtually unknown outside specialist circles.
