"In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was God."
John 1:1 · Codex Sinaiticus · c. 90 AD
The earliest complete NT manuscript. This verse launched 2,000 years of debate.
"I said, you are gods."
John 10:34 · Codex Sinaiticus · c. 90 AD
Jesus quotes Psalm 82. The most contested verse in the theosis debate.
"He became man that we might become God."
Athanasius · On the Incarnation · c. 335 AD
The most famous sentence in the history of theosis. Condemned by some, canonized by others.
"He sent a spark, which corrected man and made him live."
Filastrius · First English translation · c. 385 AD
Never before translated into English. The Gnostic divine spark, preserved by a Church Father.
"Let Utnapishtim become like the gods. Never again."
Epic of Gilgamesh · British Museum · c. 1200 BCE
The oldest flood narrative. One mortal gets immortality. Everyone else is refused.
"Let us make man in our image, according to our likeness."
Genesis 1:26 · Septuagint · traditional
Image vs likeness: one is given at creation, the other is achieved. The foundation of theosis theology.
"That you may become partakers of the divine nature."
2 Peter 1:4 · Codex Sinaiticus · c. 100 AD
The scriptural proof-text for theosis. "Partakers of the divine nature" is ontological, not metaphorical.
"He became what we are, that we might become what he is."
Irenaeus · Against Heresies · c. 180 AD
The earliest systematic theosis formula. More radical than Athanasius: not "God" but "what he himself is."

Why This Exists

AI can generate plausible translations of any ancient text in seconds. What AI cannot generate is a photograph of a manuscript. The scans in this library are the evidence. The original-language texts are verifiable against those scans. The translations are checkable against the originals. Every claim links to a source you can see with your own eyes.

As AI becomes more capable, the need for a verification layer becomes greater, not less. This is that layer.
