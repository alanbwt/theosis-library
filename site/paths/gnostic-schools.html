The Gnostic Schools

Who were the Gnostics? What did they actually believe? A guided tour through the major Gnostic movements as described by their opponents, from Simon Magus to the Valentinians.

  1. 1
    Filastrius ch. 29 · Simon Magus

    Simon Magus

    The fountainhead. A divine Ennoia trapped in matter, the world made by angels.

  2. 2
    Filastrius ch. 31 · Saturninus

    Saturninus and the divine spark

    The spark (scintilla) sent to animate the body made by angels. Only the spark is saved.

  3. 3
    Filastrius ch. 32 · Basilides

    Basilides

    365 heavens, emanation chains, Simon of Cyrene on the cross instead of Christ.

  4. 4
    Filastrius ch. 33 · Barbelo-Gnostics

    The Barbelo-Gnostics

    Ialdabaoth, Noria, four Aeons from darkness. The primordial cosmogony.

  5. 5
    Filastrius ch. 34 · The Cainites

    The Cainites and Judas

    Judas as salvific agent who understood Christ's passion would save humanity.

  6. 6
    Filastrius ch. 38 · Valentinus

    Valentinus

    The Pleroma: 30 Aeons, the Ogdoad, Decad, Dodecad. The most elaborate Gnostic system.
