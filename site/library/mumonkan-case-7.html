§Case 7 · Wumenguan
Manuscript
Greek

趙州因僧問、某甲新到此院、乞師指示

English

A monk said to Joshu: “I have just entered this monastery. I beg you, Master, please give me instruction.” Joshu said: “Have you eaten your rice gruel?” The monk said: “Yes, I have.” Joshu said: “Then go and wash your bowl.” At that the monk was enlightened.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Case 7: The koan’s genius is its refusal to be special. The monk seeks esoteric teaching; Joshu points to the most mundane act possible. Enlightenment is not a state beyond washing bowls but the full presence within washing bowls. This is the Zen teaching of “nothing special.”
