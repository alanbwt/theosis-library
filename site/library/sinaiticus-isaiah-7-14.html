Introduction

Isaiah 7:14 contains the “Immanuel” prophecy. The LXX’s rendering of Hebrew עלמה as παρθένος (“virgin”) became the foundation of the Christian virgin birth doctrine.

§7:10-14 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

διὰ τοῦτο δώσει κύριος αὐτὸς ὑμῖν σημεῖον ἰδοὺ ἡ παρθένος ἐν γαστρὶ ἕξει

English

Again the Lord spoke to Ahaz: “Ask a sign of the Lord your God; let it be deep as Sheol or high as heaven.” But Ahaz said, “I will not ask, and I will not put the Lord to the test.” Then he said, “Hear then, O house of David! Therefore the Lord himself will give you a sign. Behold, the virgin shall conceive and bear a son, and shall call his name Immanuel.”

§7:15-17 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

βούτυρον καὶ μέλι φάγεται

English

He shall eat curds and honey when he knows how to refuse the evil and choose the good. For before the boy knows how to refuse the evil and choose the good, the land whose two kings you dread will be deserted.

Translator's Notes

  1. §7:14: The Hebrew עלמה (almah) means “young woman”; it does not inherently imply virginity. The LXX’s choice of παρθένος introduced a specificity absent from the Hebrew. Matthew 1:23 quotes the LXX version to establish the virgin birth as prophetic fulfillment.
