Introduction

Hymns to Isis proliferate from the New Kingdom onward as her cult expanded from Egyptian funerary goddess to universal mother deity. This hymn from the Temple of Philae (c. 3rd century BCE) celebrates her powers of healing, protection, and cosmic sovereignty.

§Philae Hymn, opening · Temple of Philae, First Pylon
Manuscript
Greek

dwā tw Ās.t wr.t mw.t-nṬr nb.t p.t ḥnw.t nṬr.w. ḥm.t-nswt tp.t n.t Wsjr

English

Praise to you, Isis, the Great One, the God’s Mother, Lady of Heaven, Mistress and Queen of the Gods. You are the First Royal Spouse of Osiris. You created the sunrise. You made the Nile flood at its season.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Philae Hymn: The expansion of Isis from wife-of-Osiris to cosmic creator-goddess is one of the most remarkable developments in Egyptian religion. Her titles—Queen of Heaven, Star of the Sea, Mother of God—were later applied to the Virgin Mary, particularly at sites like Philae where Isis temples were directly converted to churches.
