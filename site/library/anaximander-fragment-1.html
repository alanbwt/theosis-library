Introduction

Anaximander of Miletus (c. 610–546 BCE) proposed the apeiron (the Boundless) as the first principle of all things. This single surviving fragment describes how things come into being and pass away according to the ordering of time.

§Fragment 1 · DK 12 B1 (Simplicius, In Phys. 24.13–21)
Manuscript
Greek

ἐξ ὧν δὲ ἡ γένεσίς ἐστι τοῖς οὖσι, καὶ τὴν φθορὰν εἰς ταῦτα γίνεσθαι κατὰ τὸ χρεών· διδόναι γὰρ αὐτὰ δίκην καὶ τίσιν ἀλλήλοις τῆς ἀδικίας κατὰ τὴν τοῦ χρόνου τάξιν

English

The things that are perish into the things from which they come to be, according to necessity; for they pay penalty and retribution to each other for their injustice, in accordance with the ordering of time.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Fragment 1: The language of “injustice” (ἀδικία) and “retribution” (τίσις) applies moral and legal categories to natural process. This is among the earliest instances of cosmic justice as a philosophical principle, anticipating the Stoic concept of fate.
