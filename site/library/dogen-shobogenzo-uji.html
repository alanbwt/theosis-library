Introduction

Ūji (“Being-Time”) is Dōgen’s radical philosophy of temporality. Time does not flow from past to future; each moment is the complete expression of all existence. Being is time; time is being.

§Uji 1-3 · Shōbōgenzō
Manuscript
Greek

有時は高峰の頂に立ち、有時は深海の底に行く

English

An ancient buddha said: For the time being, I stand on the highest mountain peak. For the time being, I walk on the bottom of the deepest ocean. The “time being” means that time, just as it is, is being, and being is all time.

§Uji 4-5 · Shōbōgenzō
Manuscript
Greek

光陰の去来とのみ学すべからず

English

Do not think of time as merely flying by. If time were simply flying away, there would be gaps between you and time. Mountains are time; oceans are time. Without time, no mountain could appear.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Uji: Dōgen’s philosophy of time anticipates Heidegger’s “Being and Time” by seven centuries. Both reject the notion of time as a container through which things pass. For Dōgen, each moment is not in time; each moment is time.
