Introduction

The Asa di Var is a collection of hymns by Guru Nanak set to the Asa raga, recited every morning in Sikh congregations. It addresses the nature of God, the illusion of ego, and the equality of all human beings before the divine.

Translation

§Pauri 1 · Guru Granth Sahib, Asa di Var, Page 462

I am a sacrifice to my Guru a hundred times a day, who without any delay made angels out of men. If one were to think about God a hundred thousand times, thinking alone would not bring knowledge. Even if one were to remain silent and constantly absorbed in His love, this inner silence would not bring peace. The hunger of the hungry is not satisfied, even if they obtain the loads of all the worlds. One may possess hundreds of thousands of clever tricks, but not even one of them will accompany him in the end.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Pauri 1: The phrase "made angels out of men" (manukh te devte kiye) is Guru Nanak's version of theosis: the Guru's teaching transforms ordinary humans into divine beings. The systematic rejection of thinking, silence, and worldly accumulation as paths to God establishes that only the Guru's grace and divine love can bridge the gap between the human and the divine.
