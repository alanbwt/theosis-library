Introduction

Sozomen’s Church History (c. 450 CE) covers roughly the same period as Socrates Scholasticus but draws on additional sources and gives more attention to monasticism and the Eastern churches. It is dedicated to Emperor Theodosius II.

§1.1.1–5 · Sozomen, Church History
Manuscript
Greek

[Greek: Ἀναγκαιότατον ηγησάμην]

English

I have thought it most necessary to narrate these events. For it seems to me that no subject can be more worthy of record than the institution and progress of the faith, which, starting from small beginnings, spread itself throughout the world, not by war or force, but solely by divine guidance and the virtue of its teachers, who willingly subjected themselves to labors and dangers and death.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1.1: Sozomen’s emphasis on the faith’s growth “not by war or force” reflects a pre-Justinian perspective when Christianity’s spread was still attributed primarily to persuasion and martyrdom rather than imperial compulsion. His attention to monastic figures (Antony, Pachomius, the Desert Fathers) makes his history valuable for understanding the ascetic movement.
