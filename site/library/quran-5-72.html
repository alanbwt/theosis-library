Quran 5:72-73: They Disbelieve Who Say God Is the Messiah

Quran (divine revelation), c. 610-632 AD

Introduction

Surah al-Ma’idah (The Table Spread), verses 72–73, contains the Quran’s most direct rejection of the Christian claim that Jesus is God. The passage names the doctrine explicitly—“God is the Messiah, son of Mary”—and declares that anyone who holds this belief has committed kufr (disbelief). It then extends the condemnation to the Trinity: “God is the third of three.” The Quran frames divine incarnation not as a theological error but as a form of shirk (associating partners with God), the one unforgivable sin in Islamic theology.

For the theosis question, these verses represent the sharpest possible rejection. If God cannot become human—if the very claim constitutes blasphemy—then the Christian logic of deification through incarnation is not merely wrong but spiritually catastrophic. Islam offers its own account of human nearness to God through taqwa (God-consciousness) and ihsan (spiritual excellence), but it draws an absolute line: the servant remains a servant, and God remains God.

§5:72 · Quran, Surah al-Ma’idah 5:72
Manuscript
Arabic

لقد كفر الذين قالوا إن الله هو المسيح ابن مريم وقال المسيح يا بني إسرائيل اعبدوا الله ربي وربكم إنه من يشرك بالله فقد حرم الله عليه الجنة ومأواه النار وما للظالمين من أنصار

English

They have certainly disbelieved who say, “God is the Messiah, son of Mary.” But the Messiah said, “O Children of Israel, worship God, my Lord and your Lord.” Indeed, whoever associates others with God—God has forbidden him Paradise, and his refuge is the Fire. And there are no helpers for the wrongdoers.

§5:73 · Quran, Surah al-Ma’idah 5:73
Manuscript
Arabic

لقد كفر الذين قالوا إن الله ثالث ثلاثة وما من إله إلا إله واحد وإن لم ينتهوا عما يقولون ليمسن الذين كفروا منهم عذاب أليم

English

They have certainly disbelieved who say, “God is the third of three.” There is no god except one God. And if they do not desist from what they are saying, there will surely afflict the disbelievers among them a painful punishment.

Translator's Notes

  1. §5:72: The Quran puts a counter-teaching in Jesus’s own mouth: “Worship God, my Lord and your Lord.” In the Islamic reading, Jesus himself rejects his own divinity. The phrase rabbi wa-rabbakum (“my Lord and your Lord”) places Jesus among the worshippers, not among the worshipped. This directly contradicts the Johannine Prologue’s claim that “the Word was God.”
  2. §5:73: The phrase “God is the third of three” (thalith thalatha) has been debated by scholars. Some argue it reflects a misunderstanding of the Trinity, since orthodox Christianity does not teach that God is “one of three” but rather three persons in one essence. Others argue the Quran is addressing actual heterodox Christian communities in Arabia that may have held cruder tritheistic views. Either way, the Quranic position is unambiguous: any multiplication of the divine is disbelief.
