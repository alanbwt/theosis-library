Chilam Balam of Chumayel: The Creation of the World

Chilam Balam of Chumayel: The Creation of the World
Chilam Balam Scribes, c. 17th-18th century AD
Princeton University Library MS

Introduction

The Books of Chilam Balam are the most important surviving texts of Yucatec Maya literature — prophetic histories, cosmological narratives, and ritual calendars transcribed in the Latin alphabet after the Spanish conquest but preserving pre-Columbian knowledge. The Chumayel version contains the most complete Maya creation account outside the Popol Vuh.

Yucatec Maya original and English translation.
§1 · CB §1
Yucatec Maya

He uah u tzolan katun, uaxac Ahau u katunil; hunac ceel u kinil.

English

This is the record of the katun. Eight Ahau was the katun when it occurred; the first day was its day.

§2 · CB §2
Yucatec Maya

Bay ix u hoppol cab yetel caan, tu lacal ba tumenel ti Dios.

English

Thus was the beginning of earth and heaven, all things by the word of God.
