Gallic War VI.13-18: The Druids and Celtic Religion

Gallic War VI.13-18: The Druids and Celtic Religion
Julius Caesar, 100-44 BCE
Codex Bongarsianus (Paris BN lat. 3860)

Introduction

Caesar's ethnographic excursus in Book VI of the Gallic War is the most detailed classical account of Celtic religion. He describes the Druids as judges, teachers, and theologians who taught the transmigration of souls — a doctrine that Greek observers compared to Pythagoreanism. Despite Caesar's political motivations, this passage remains indispensable for understanding pre-Christian Celtic belief.

Latin original and English translation.
§6.13 · BG 6.13
Latin

In omni Gallia eorum hominum qui aliquo sunt numero atque honore genera sunt duo... Druides... Equites.

English

In all of Gaul there are two classes of men of any account and dignity... the Druids and the Knights.

§6.14 · BG 6.14
Latin

In primis hoc volunt persuadere, non interire animas, sed ab aliis post mortem transire ad alios, atque hoc maxime ad virtutem excitari putant metu mortis neglecto.

English

Above all they wish to persuade people that souls do not perish but pass from one body to another after death, and they think this is the greatest incentive to courage, since the fear of death is thereby set aside.
