Introduction

§9:4-6 · Bhagavad Gita 9
Manuscript
Greek

mayā tatam idaṃ sarvaṃ jagad avyakta-mūrtinā | mat-sthāni sarva-bhūtāni na cāhaṃ teṣv avasthitaḥ

English

By me all this universe is pervaded in my unmanifest form. All beings exist in me, but I do not dwell in them. And yet beings do not exist in me—behold my divine mystery! My Self sustains all beings but does not dwell in them.

Translator's Notes

  1. §9:4-5: The consecutive assertions “all beings exist in me” and “beings do not exist in me” constitute a deliberate paradox. Rāmānuja resolves it by distinguishing God’s essence from God’s sustaining power; śaṅkara reads it as pointing beyond conceptual thought entirely.
