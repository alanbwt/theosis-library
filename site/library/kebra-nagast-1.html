Introduction

The Kebra Nagast (Glory of Kings) is the foundational text of Ethiopian civilization — a 14th-century compilation that traces the Ethiopian royal line from the union of King Solomon and the Queen of Sheba, and narrates the transfer of the Ark of the Covenant from Jerusalem to Aksum. It served as the constitutional document of the Ethiopian empire and the theological basis for Rastafarian belief.
