Introduction

The Hermetic Prayer of Thanksgiving is preserved in both the Greek magical papyri (PGM III) and the Nag Hammadi Coptic library (NHC VI.7). Its presence in both pagan and Gnostic collections demonstrates the fluidity between Hermetic, pagan, and Christian mystical traditions.

Greek original and English translation.
§NHC VI.7 / PGM III.591–609 · Nag Hammadi Codex VI.7; PGM III.591–609
Manuscript
Greek

χάριν σοι οἴδαμεν· πᾶσα ψυχὴ καὶ καρδία πρὸς σὲ ἀνατείνεται, ὦ ὄνομα ἀτάρακτον

English

We give thanks to you, every soul and heart is lifted up to you, O name that cannot be troubled, honored with the name of God and praised with the name of Father. To everyone and everything comes the fatherly kindness and affection and love, and any teaching there may be that is sweet and plain.

Translator's Notes

  1. §NHC VI.7: The Prayer of Thanksgiving is the only text found in both the Greek Magical Papyri and the Nag Hammadi library. Its inclusion in a Gnostic codex alongside Christian texts demonstrates that the boundaries between “pagan,” “Hermetic,” and “Christian” were far more porous in late antiquity than later heresiologists would admit.
