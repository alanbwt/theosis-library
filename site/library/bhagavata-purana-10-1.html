Introduction

Book 10 of the Bhagavata Purana narrates the birth and childhood of Krishna—the supreme deity born as a cowherd boy in Vrindavan. His divine play (līlā) reveals the nature of God as joy.

§10.1.1-4 · Bhagavata Purana
Manuscript
Greek

śrī śuka uvāca | prajāpatiḥ sa bhū-bhāraṃ

English

Shuka said: When the earth, overburdened by the armies of demons disguised as kings, approached Brahma for relief, Brahma prayed to Vishnu. The Lord consented to descend and was born as the son of Vasudeva and Devaki in the prison of the tyrant Kamsa.

§10.1.20-22 · Bhagavata Purana
Manuscript
Greek

vasudevaḥ sutaṃ ādāya | baddhaḥ mocanaṃ bandhanaṃ

English

At the moment of Krishna’s birth, the chains fell from Vasudeva’s hands, the prison doors opened of their own accord, and the guards fell into deep sleep. Vasudeva carried the infant across the Yamuna, which parted to let them pass.

Translator's Notes

  1. §10.1: The avatāra doctrine—God descending into human form—is structurally parallel to the Christian Incarnation but functionally different. In Hinduism, God descends repeatedly whenever dharma declines. In Christianity, the Incarnation is a singular, unrepeatable event.
