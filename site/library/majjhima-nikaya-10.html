§10.1-3 · Majjhima Nikāya
Manuscript
Greek

evaṃ me sutaṃ | ekāyanaṃ ayaṃ maggo

English

The Blessed One said: “Monks, this is the direct path for the purification of beings, for the overcoming of sorrow—the four foundations of mindfulness. Here, a monk abides contemplating the body as a body, ardent, clearly comprehending, and mindful.”

§10.40-42 · Majjhima Nikāya
Manuscript
Greek

yo hi koci bhikkhave ime cattāro satipaṭṭhāne satta vassāni bhāveyya

English

Whoever develops these four foundations of mindfulness for seven years may expect one of two results: either final knowledge here and now, or the state of non-returning. Let alone seven years—whoever develops them for seven days may expect one of these results.

Translator's Notes

  1. §10: The claim that seven days of proper mindfulness practice can lead to liberation is one of the most radical promises in Buddhist literature. It has been both inspiration and stumbling block: nearly all practitioners find it takes considerably longer.
