Introduction

Matthew 18:21–35 contains the parable of the unforgiving servant, Jesus’s most extended teaching on forgiveness. Peter’s question about limits provokes an answer that abolishes them. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§18:21–22 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

τοτε προσελθων ο πετρος ειπεν αυτω

English

Then Peter came up and said to him, “Lord, how often will my brother sin against me, and I forgive him? As many as seven times?” Jesus said to him, “I do not say to you seven times, but seventy times seven.”

§18:32–35 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

τοτε προσκαλεσαμενος αυτον ο κυριος αυτου

English

Then his master summoned him and said to him, “You wicked servant! I forgave you all that debt because you pleaded with me. Should you not also have had mercy on your fellow servant, as I had mercy on you?” So also my heavenly Father will do to every one of you, if you do not forgive your brother from your heart.

Translator's Notes

  1. §18:22: The phrase “seventy times seven” deliberately inverts Genesis 4:24, where Lamech boasts of seventy-sevenfold vengeance. Where the old dispensation multiplied retribution, the new multiplies forgiveness.
