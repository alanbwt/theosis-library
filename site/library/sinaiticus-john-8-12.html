Introduction

John 8:12–59 contains Jesus’ declaration “I am the light of the world” and the climactic claim “before Abraham was, I am.” This chapter represents the sharpest confrontation between Jesus and the religious authorities in John’s Gospel.

Greek

πάλιν οὖν αὐτοῖς ἐλάλησεν ὁ Ἰησοῦς λέγων · ἐγώ εἰμι τὸ φῶς τοῦ κόσμου

English

Again Jesus spoke to them, saying, ‘I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life.’

§8:58–59 · Codex Sinaiticus, John 8:58–59
Manuscript
Greek

εἶπεν αὐτοῖς Ἰησοῦς · ἀμὴν ἀμὴν λέγω ὑμῖν πρὶν Ἀβραὰμ γενέσθαι ἐγὼ εἰμί

English

Jesus said to them, ‘Truly, truly, I say to you, before Abraham was, I am.’ So they picked up stones to throw at him, but Jesus hid himself and went out of the temple.

Translator's Notes

  1. §8:58: The absolute “I am” (ἐγὼ εἰμί) without a predicate is the most direct divine self-identification in the Gospels, echoing Exodus 3:14. The audience’s response—picking up stones—shows they understood it as a claim to divinity.
