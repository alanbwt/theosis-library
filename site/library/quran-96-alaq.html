Introduction

Surah Al-Alaq ("The Clot") contains the first five verses revealed to the Prophet Muhammad in the Cave of Hira, making it the historical beginning of Quranic revelation. The command "Read!" inaugurates the entire prophetic mission.

§96:1-5 · Quran, Surah 96:1-5
Manuscript
Greek

اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ خَلَقَ الْإِنسَانَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ

English

Read! In the name of your Lord who created—created the human being from a clinging clot. Read! And your Lord is the Most Generous, who taught by the pen, taught the human being what he did not know.

Translator's Notes

  1. §96:1: The word iqra' can mean both "read" and "recite." Since the Prophet was traditionally understood to be unlettered (ummi), "recite" is the more common interpretation. The term 'alaq (clinging clot) refers to the early stage of embryonic development, grounding divine knowledge in the materiality of human creation.
