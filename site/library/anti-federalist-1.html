§Brutus No. 1 (Opening) · Brutus, Anti-Federalist No. 1 (1787)
Manuscript
Greek

[Original English text as published in the New-York Journal, 1787]

English

To the Citizens of the State of New-York: Perhaps this country never saw so critical a period as the present. We have felt the feebleness of the ties by which the states are held together under the Articles of Confederation. A new Constitution is proposed. It is to be examined with great care, for upon its adoption or rejection depends the happiness or misery of this generation and all those to follow.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Opening: Brutus (likely Robert Yates) warned prophetically that the “necessary and proper” clause and the supremacy clause would enable unlimited federal expansion. The Anti-Federalists lost the ratification battle but won the subsequent argument: the Bill of Rights adopted their core concerns about individual liberty against centralized power.
