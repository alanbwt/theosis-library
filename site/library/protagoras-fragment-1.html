Introduction

Protagoras of Abdera (c. 490–420 BCE) was the most celebrated of the Sophists. His “man-measure” doctrine made human perception the criterion of truth and became the founding statement of philosophical relativism.

§Fragment 1 · DK 80 B1 (Sextus Empiricus, Adv. Math. VII.60; Plato, Theaetetus 152a)
Manuscript
Greek

πάντων χρημάτων μέτρον ἐστὶν ἄνθρωπος, τῶν μὲν ὄντων ὡς ἔστιν, τῶν δὲ οὐκ ὄντων ὡς οὐκ ἔστιν

English

Of all things the measure is man: of those that are, that they are; and of those that are not, that they are not.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Fragment 1: Whether “man” (ἄνθρωπος) refers to the individual or to humanity collectively is the central interpretive question. Plato reads it as radical individual relativism in the Theaetetus; other readings take it as a statement about the human condition as such.
