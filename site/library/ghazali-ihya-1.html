§Book 1, Chapter 1 · Ihya Ulum al-Din, Book 1, Kitab al-Ilm
Manuscript
Greek

اعلم أن طلب العلم فريضة على كل مسلم

English

Know that the obligation to seek knowledge pertains to every Muslim. What is meant is the knowledge of one’s present state. No one is free from a state: if the time for prayer has arrived, he must learn how to pray. If the month of Ramadan comes upon him, he must learn the rules of fasting. Knowledge is the foundation upon which all acts of obedience stand, and the act of worship is only valid through knowledge of how to perform it.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Book 1: Ghazali's classification of knowledge into fard 'ayn (individually obligatory) and fard kifaya (communally obligatory) resolved a longstanding tension between the universal duty to learn and the reality that not everyone can master every science. This framework shaped Islamic education for nearly a millennium.
