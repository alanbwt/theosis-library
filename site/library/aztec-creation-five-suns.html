Introduction

The Aztec Legend of the Five Suns recounts the creation and destruction of four previous worlds before the current Fifth Sun. Each age was destroyed when its patron deity fell—the current age will end with earthquakes. The myth is preserved in the Leyenda de los Soles and the Sunstone.

§The Five Suns · Leyenda de los Soles (Nahuatl tradition)
Manuscript
Greek

[Nahuatl: In Tonatiuh nauhcampa momanaz]

English

In the beginning, the gods gathered at Teotihuacan and asked: who will bear the burden of being the sun? Nanahuatzin, the humble, pimpled god, threw himself into the great fire and became the Fifth Sun. But the sun would not move until the gods themselves shed their blood. Thus the current world was born from sacrifice—and sacrifice is what sustains it. This is the Sun of Movement, Nahui Ollin. It will be destroyed by earthquakes.

Translator's Notes

  1. §The Five Suns: The Aztec cosmology of successive world-ages destroyed and recreated resonates with Hindu yugas and Hesiod’s declining ages of gold, silver, bronze, and iron. The principle that the cosmos requires sacrifice to continue existing—that the gods themselves must bleed—distinguishes Mesoamerican theology from both Greek and biblical creation accounts, where creation is sustained by divine word or will rather than divine self-sacrifice.
