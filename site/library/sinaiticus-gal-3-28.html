Galatians 3:28: All One in Christ Jesus

Paul (attributed), c. 50-65 AD

Introduction

Galatians 3:28 is one of the most radical verses in the Pauline epistles. In a single sentence, Paul dissolves the three fundamental social distinctions of the ancient world—ethnic (Jew and Greek), legal (slave and free), and biological (male and female)—declaring that all are “one in Christ Jesus.” The Greek word for “one” is heis (εἷς), the masculine singular, not hen (ἕν), the neuter. The believers are not one thing; they are one person—unified in the single person of Christ.

For the theosis tradition, this verse addresses the question of what happens to individual identity in the process of divinization. The dissolution of distinctions does not mean the annihilation of persons but their incorporation into a larger unity. The Gnostic tradition read this verse as confirmation that the spiritual realm transcends all material categories. The orthodox tradition read it as a description of the eschatological community, in which the old divisions of the fallen world are overcome through participation in Christ’s resurrected body.

Translator's Notes

  1. §3:27: The phrase “put on Christ” (Χριστὸν ἐνεδύσασθε) uses the metaphor of clothing. Baptism is described as putting on a new garment—or rather, putting on a new person. The metaphor recurs in Romans 13:14 and Colossians 3:10. In the early Church, baptismal candidates literally stripped naked before immersion and were clothed in white garments afterward, ritually enacting the transformation that Paul describes.
  2. §3:28: The third pair—“male and female” (ἄρσεν καὶ θῆλυ)—uses different language from the first two. Instead of “neither... nor” (οὐκ... οὐδέ), it uses “male and female” (ἄρσεν καὶ θῆλυ), which echoes Genesis 1:27 in the Septuagint. Paul appears to be claiming that baptism into Christ reverses the division of humanity into male and female that was established at creation. The Gospel of Thomas, saying 22, makes a similar claim: one enters the kingdom “when you make the male and the female one and the same.”
