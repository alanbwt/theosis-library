Introduction

The Bhagavata Purana opens by declaring itself the essence of all Vedic wisdom, the “ripe fruit of the wish-fulfilling tree of Vedic literature.” It is the supreme text of Vaishnava devotion and the primary source for Krishna’s life and theology.

§1.1.1–3 · Bhagavata Purana 1.1.1–3
Manuscript
Greek

ओं नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

English

I meditate upon the Supreme Truth, the source from whom the creation, maintenance, and dissolution of the universe proceeds. That Absolute Truth, from whom even Brahma is bewildered, is known through the mature fruit of Vedic knowledge. This Bhagavata Purana is as brilliant as the sun and has arisen just after the departure of Krishna to his own abode.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1.1.1: The opening verse’s declaration that the Supreme Truth is the source of creation, maintenance, and dissolution establishes the Bhagavata’s theological framework: all reality is a manifestation of Vishnu/Krishna. The text’s emphasis on bhakti (devotion) over jnana (knowledge) influenced the entire devotional tradition of India.
