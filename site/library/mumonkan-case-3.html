§Case 3 · Wumenguan
Manuscript
Greek

俱胝和尚、凡有所問、唯挙一指

English

Whenever Master Juzhi was asked a question, he simply raised one finger. His boy attendant began imitating him. Hearing of this, Juzhi seized the boy and cut off his finger with a knife. As the boy ran away screaming, Juzhi called to him. The boy turned his head, and Juzhi raised his finger. The boy was suddenly enlightened.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Case 3: The violence of this koan resists comfortable interpretation. The cutting is both literal and metaphorical: the boy’s imitation was a conceptual finger pointing at the moon. When the real finger was removed, only the moon remained. Zen pedagogy operates by removing the crutch.
