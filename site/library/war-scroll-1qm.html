War Scroll (1QM): Sons of Light vs Sons of Darkness

Anonymous (Enochic), c. 3rd c. BCE

Introduction

The War Scroll (Milhamah, 1QM) describes the eschatological battle between the “Sons of Light”—the Qumran community and their angelic allies—and the “Sons of Darkness,” led by Belial and the nations of wickedness. The opening column (1.1–17) sets the stage: this is not merely a human conflict but a war in which the archangel Michael and the heavenly host fight alongside the elect. The final victory belongs to God, and it will result in the annihilation of evil and the eternal reign of light.

The War Scroll matters for the theosis tradition because it imagines human beings fighting alongside angels as co-participants in the divine plan. The boundary between the human and the heavenly is not absolute—the elect are already members of the angelic congregation. This “angelic fellowship” theology, found throughout the Qumran literature, represents one of the earliest Jewish frameworks for thinking about human participation in the divine realm.

למשכיל סרך המלחמה ראש משלחת יד בני אור בגורל בני חושך

English

For the Instructor: the rule of the war. The first attack of the Sons of Light shall be against the lot of the Sons of Darkness, against the army of Belial—against the troop of Edom, Moab, and the sons of Ammon, and against the army of the dwellers of Philistia, and against the troops of the Kittim of Asshur.

§1.4–7 · 1QM Column I, lines 4–7
Manuscript
Hebrew

ואחר המלחמה יעלו משם

English

And after the war they shall go up from there and encamp against the king of the Kittim in Egypt. In his time he shall go forth with great fury to wage war against the kings of the north, and his anger shall destroy and cut off the horn of their power. This shall be a time of salvation for the people of God, an appointed time of dominion for all the men of his lot, and of everlasting destruction for all the lot of Belial.

§1.8–11 · 1QM Column I, lines 8–11
Manuscript
Hebrew

והיה מהומה רבה ובני אור וגורל חושך ילחמו יחד

English

There shall be a great tumult, and the Sons of Light and the lot of Darkness shall fight together for the might of God, amid the roar of a great multitude and the war cry of gods and men, on the day of calamity. It shall be a time of great tribulation for the people whom God shall redeem. Of all their afflictions none shall be like this, from its beginning until its end in eternal redemption.

§1.10–12 · 1QM Column I, lines 10–12
Manuscript

English

On the day when the Kittim fall, there shall be battle and terrible carnage before the God of Israel, for that shall be the day appointed from ancient times for the battle of destruction of the Sons of Darkness. Translator's Notes

  1. §1.1–3: The “Kittim” is a cipher used across the Dead Sea Scrolls, most likely referring to the Romans, though some scholars have argued for the Seleucid Greeks. The identification of specific historical enemies with cosmic forces of darkness is characteristic of Qumran apocalypticism: the political and the metaphysical are fused.
  2. §1.10–12: The phrase “congregation of gods” (‘adat ’elim) refers to the angelic host fighting alongside the human elect. This is one of the clearest expressions of the Qumran belief that the community already participates in the heavenly assembly. The term ’elim (gods, divine beings) is the same word used in Psalm 82:1 (“God stands in the congregation of the gods”), a passage central to the theosis debate.
