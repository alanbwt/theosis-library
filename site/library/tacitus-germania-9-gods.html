Introduction

Chapter 9 of the Germania identifies the chief Germanic gods using Roman names (interpretatio Romana). Tacitus reports that the Germans worship Mercury (Woden/Odin) above all, and also honor Hercules (Thor) and Mars (Tyr).

Greek original and English translation.
§Germania 9 · Tacitus, Germania 9
Manuscript
Greek

Deorum maxime Mercurium colunt, cui certis diebus humanis quoque hostiis litare fas habent. Herculem ac Martem concessis animalibus placant. Pars Sueborum et Isidi sacrificat.

English

Among the gods, they worship Mercury most of all; they think it right to offer him on certain days even human sacrifices. Hercules and Mars they appease with lawful animals. Part of the Suebi sacrifice also to Isis. What the origin of this foreign cult is I have not been able to discover.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Germania 9: Tacitus’s identification of Mercury with the chief Germanic god (Woden/Odin) persists in our weekday names: Wednesday = Woden’s day, just as French mercredi = Mercury’s day. The mention of Isis-worship among the Suebi may refer to a Germanic goddess (possibly Nerthus or Freyja) interpreted through a Roman lens.
