Oedipus the King: Know Thyself

Oedipus the King: Know Thyself
Sophocles, c. 496-406 BCE
Mediceus Laurentianus 32.9

Introduction

Aristotle called Oedipus the King the perfect tragedy. The play dramatizes the most terrifying form of knowledge: the discovery that the truth you have been seeking is the truth about yourself. Oedipus, the great solver of riddles, cannot solve the riddle of his own identity until it is too late. The Delphic maxim 'Know Thyself' becomes not wisdom but catastrophe.

§1186-1196 · OT 1186-1196
Greek

ἰοὺ ἰού· τὰ πάντ᾿ ἂν ἐξήκοι σαφῆ. ὦ φῶς, τελευταῖόν σε προσβλέψαιμι νῦν, ὅστις πέφασμαι φύς τ᾿ ἀφ᾿ ὧν οὐ χρῆν, ξὺν οἷς τ᾿ οὐ χρῆν ὁμιλῶν, οὕς τέ μ᾿ οὐκ ἔδει κτανών.

English

Ah! Ah! It all comes clear now! O light, let me look upon you for the last time — I who have been revealed as born from those I should not have been born from, living with those I should not have lived with, and having killed those I should never have killed.

§1524-1530 · OT 1524-1530
Greek

ὥστε θνητὸν ὄντα κείνην τὴν τελευταίαν ἰδεῖν ἡμέραν ἐπισκοποῦντα μηδέν᾿ ὀλβίζειν, πρὶν ἂν τέρμα τοῦ βίου περάσῃ μηδὲν ἀλγεινὸν παθών.

English

So count no mortal happy until he has passed the final limit of his life without suffering grief.
