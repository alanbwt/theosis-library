Introduction

Ruth 1 narrates Naomi’s return from Moab and Ruth’s famous declaration of loyalty: “Where you go I will go.” The story of a Moabite woman entering Israel’s covenant community challenges ethnic exclusivism.

§1:16–17 · Septuagint, Ruth 1:16–17
Manuscript
Greek

εἶπεν δὲ Ρουθ μὴ ἀπαντήσαι ἐμοὶ τοῦ καταλιπεῖν σε

English

But Ruth said, ‘Do not urge me to leave you or to return from following you. For where you go I will go, and where you lodge I will lodge. Your people shall be my people, and your God my God. Where you die I will die, and there will I be buried.’

Translator's Notes

  1. §1:16: Ruth’s declaration is a voluntary covenant commitment from a foreigner—a Moabite, a people Israel’s law explicitly excluded. That Ruth becomes the great-grandmother of David and an ancestor of Jesus makes this the biblical tradition’s strongest statement against ethnic exclusivism.
