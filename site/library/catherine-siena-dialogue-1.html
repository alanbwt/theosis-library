Introduction

Catherine of Siena's Dialogue (1378) records a mystical conversation between the soul and God the Father. Dictated during ecstatic states, it addresses providence, obedience, and the bridge of Christ connecting earth to heaven.

Translation

§Chapter 1: The Soul's Petition · The Dialogue, Chapter 1

A soul rises up, restless with tremendous desire for God's honor and the salvation of souls. She has dwelt for some time in the cell of self-knowledge in order to know better God's goodness toward her. For knowledge of herself gives her the bitterness of humility, and knowledge of God gives her the fire of love. Then God, eternal Father, turns the eye of His mercy toward her and says: "O dearest daughter, I am He who is. You are she who is not. If you know these two things, you will be blessed."

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 1: The formula "I am He who is; you are she who is not" echoes Exodus 3:14 ("I am who I am") and recasts it as a principle of mystical ontology. Catherine, an unlettered laywoman who became one of the most influential theologians of the 14th century, was declared a Doctor of the Church in 1970—only the second woman to receive this title.
