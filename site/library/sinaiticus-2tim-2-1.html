Introduction

2 Timothy 2:1–13 exhorts Timothy to endure hardship and includes an early Christian hymn: “If we have died with him, we will also live with him.” The passage frames the Christian life as co-participation in Christ’s death and reign. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§2:1–3 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

συ ουν τεκνον μου ενδυναμου εν τη χαριτι τη εν χριστω ιησου

English

You then, my child, be strengthened by the grace that is in Christ Jesus, and what you have heard from me in the presence of many witnesses entrust to faithful men, who will be able to teach others also. Share in suffering as a good soldier of Christ Jesus.

§2:11–13 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

πιστος ο λογος ει γαρ συναπεθανομεν και συζησομεν

English

The saying is trustworthy, for: If we have died with him, we will also live with him; if we endure, we will also reign with him; if we deny him, he also will deny us; if we are faithless, he remains faithful—for he cannot deny himself.

Translator's Notes

  1. §2:11: The hymnic fragment uses four syn- (with) compounds: co-died, co-live, co-endure, co-reign. This “with Christ” language (συν-χριστος) is distinctly Pauline and expresses participation rather than mere imitation.
