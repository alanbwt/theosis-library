Theogony: The Birth of the Gods

Theogony: The Birth of the Gods
Hesiod, c. 700 BCE
P.Oxy. 2354; medieval MSS tradition

Introduction

The Theogony is the oldest systematic account of creation in Western literature. Hesiod tells how the cosmos emerged from Chaos, how Earth (Gaia) gave birth to Heaven (Ouranos), how the Titans ruled and fell, and how Zeus established the present order of the gods. This is not merely mythology: it is the first attempt to explain the origin and structure of reality through a narrative of cosmic generations.

§116-125 · Theog. 116-125
Greek

ἤτοι μὲν πρώτιστα Χάος γένετ᾿· αὐτὰρ ἔπειτα Γαῖ᾿ εὐρύστερνος, πάντων ἕδος ἀσφαλὲς αἰεί, ἠδ᾿ Ἔρος, ὃς κάλλιστος ἐν ἀθανάτοισι θεοῖσι.

English

In the beginning there came to be Chaos; and then broad-bosomed Earth, the ever-sure foundation of all, and Eros, the most beautiful among the immortal gods.

§126-138 · Theog. 126-138
Greek

Γαῖα δέ τοι πρῶτον μὲν ἐγείνατο ἶσον ἑαυτῇ Οὐρανὸν ἀστερόενθ᾿, ἵνα μιν περὶ πάντα καλύπτοι, ὄφρ᾿ εἴη μακάρεσσι θεοῖσ᾿ ἕδος ἀσφαλὲς αἰεί.

English

Earth first bore starry Heaven, equal to herself, to cover her on every side, to be a secure seat forever for the blessed gods.

§453-460 · Theog. 453-460
Greek

Ῥείη δὲ δμηθεῖσα Κρόνῳ τέκε φαίδιμα τέκνα... τοὺς μὲν κατέπινε μέγας Κρόνος.

English

Rhea, submitting to Kronos, bore him glorious children... but great Kronos swallowed them each as they came from the womb to her knees.
