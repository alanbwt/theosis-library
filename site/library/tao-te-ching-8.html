Introduction

Chapter 8 uses the metaphor of water to illustrate the Tao's virtue. Water benefits all things without competing, settles in the lowest places, and thereby comes closest to the Tao.

§Chapter 8 · Tao Te Ching, Chapter 8

Greek

上善若水 水善利万物而不争

English

The highest good is like water. Water benefits all things and does not compete with them. It dwells in the lowly places that all disdain—therefore it is close to the Tao. In dwelling, be close to the ground. In thought, be deep. In dealing with others, be gentle and kind. In speech, be true. In ruling, be just. In action, be timely.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 8: The water metaphor became central to Taoist ethics and strategy. The principle of wu zheng (non-contention) does not mean passivity but rather effectiveness through yielding. Water carves canyons not through force but through persistence and adaptability.
