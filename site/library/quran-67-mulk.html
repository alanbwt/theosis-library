Introduction

Surah Al-Mulk ("The Sovereignty") meditates on God's absolute dominion over creation, the perfection of the heavens, and the consequences of disbelief. Tradition holds that it intercedes for its reciter in the grave.

§67:1-5 · Quran, Surah 67:1-5
Manuscript
Greek

تَبَارَكَ الَّذِي بِيَدِهِ الْمُلْكُ وَهُوَ عَلَىـ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

English

Blessed is He in whose hand is the sovereignty, and He has power over all things. He who created death and life to test which of you is best in deed—and He is the Almighty, the Forgiving. He who created seven heavens in layers. You see no flaw in the creation of the Most Merciful. So look again: do you see any cracks? Then look again and again—your sight will return to you humbled and exhausted.

Translator's Notes

  1. §67:2: The statement that God "created death and life to test you" places death before life, reversing the expected order. Al-Zamakhshari explains that death is mentioned first because nonexistence preceded existence. The verse frames mortal existence as fundamentally probationary.
