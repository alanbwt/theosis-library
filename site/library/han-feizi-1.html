§Chapter 49: The Five Vermin · Han Feizi, Chapter 49
Manuscript
Greek

儒以文亂法，俠以武犯禁

English

The Confucians with their learning disturb the laws; the knights-errant with their martial prowess violate the prohibitions. In times of peace the ruler honors the Confucians; in times of crisis he employs warriors. Those he honors are not those he uses; those he uses are not those he honors. This is why the state falls into disorder.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Ch. 49: Han Fei’s critique of Confucian moralism anticipates Machiavelli by seventeen centuries: effective governance requires institutions, not virtuous individuals. The “five vermin” (scholars, speech-makers, swordsmen, draft-dodgers, merchants) represent groups whose private interests undermine state power.
