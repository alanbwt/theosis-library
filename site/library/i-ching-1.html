Introduction

Hexagram 1 of the I Ching (Qian/The Creative) represents pure yang energy, the creative power of heaven. It is the first and most fundamental hexagram, symbolizing initiative, strength, and the dynamic origin of all things.

Greek original and English translation.
§Hexagram 1: Qian (The Creative) · I Ching, Hexagram 1
Manuscript
Greek

乾。元亨利贞。天行健。君子以自强不息

English

The Creative works sublime success, furthering through perseverance. The movement of heaven is full of power. Thus the superior person makes himself strong and untiring. The dragon appears in the field. It furthers one to see the great man. The dragon flies in the heavens. It furthers one to see the great man.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Hexagram 1: The four qualities attributed to Qian—yuan (sublime), heng (success), li (furthering), zhen (perseverance)—became a foundational cosmological formula in Chinese philosophy. Neo-Confucian thinkers like Zhu Xi interpreted them as the four aspects of cosmic creativity itself.
