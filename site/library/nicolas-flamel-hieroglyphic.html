Introduction

The Hieroglyphic Figures attributed to Nicolas Flamel (c. 1399) describes his alleged discovery and interpretation of an alchemical manuscript. Whether or not Flamel authored it, the text became one of the most influential works in the Western alchemical tradition.

Translation

§The Discovery of the Book · Exposition of the Hieroglyphical Figures, Opening

I, Nicolas Flamel, scrivener, came upon a gilded book, very old and large, with a cover of wrought copper engraved with strange characters. It cost me two florins. The leaves were not of parchment but of the bark of young trees. On the first page was written in golden letters: "Abraham the Jew, Prince, Priest, Levite, Astrologer, and Philosopher, to the Nation of the Jews scattered by the Wrath of God in France: Greetings." The book contained the secret of the transmutation of metals, but written in figures and symbols I could not understand for twenty-one years.

Translator's Notes

  1. §The Discovery: The attribution to Flamel is almost certainly pseudepigraphic; the text was likely composed in the 17th century. Nevertheless, it encodes genuine alchemical philosophy: the twenty-one-year search for understanding represents the alchemist's long purification, and the Jewish-Christian synthesis reflects the Renaissance fascination with prisca theologia—the idea that all wisdom traditions share a common ancient source.
