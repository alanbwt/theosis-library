Introduction

Ibn Rushd's (Averroes') Tahafut al-Tahafut ("The Incoherence of the Incoherence") is a systematic defense of philosophy against al-Ghazali's famous attack. It remains the most rigorous argument for the compatibility of reason and revelation in Islamic intellectual history.

Translation

§Introduction · Tahafut al-Tahafut, Opening Section

The purpose of this book is to examine the arguments set forth by Abu Hamid al-Ghazali in his Tahafut al-Falasifa, to determine which are sound and which fall short. For if philosophy is condemned on account of unsound arguments against it, then the condemnation itself is unsound. Truth does not contradict truth; rather, it accords with it and bears witness to it.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Introduction: The principle that "truth does not contradict truth" (al-haqq la yudadd al-haqq) became Averroes' signature thesis. It implies that if a philosophical conclusion appears to contradict scripture, either the philosophy is wrong or the scripture requires allegorical interpretation. This stance was both celebrated and condemned across medieval Christendom and Islam.
