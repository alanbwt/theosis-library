Introduction

Mark 13:24–37 contains the climax of the Olivet Discourse, describing the coming of the Son of Man in clouds with great power and glory. The cosmic imagery draws on Daniel 7 and Isaiah 13. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§13:24–27 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

αλλα εν εκειναις ταις ημεραις μετα την θλιψιν εκεινην

English

But in those days, after that tribulation, the sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give its light, and the stars will be falling from heaven, and the powers in the heavens will be shaken. And then they will see the Son of Man coming in clouds with great power and glory. And then he will send out the angels and gather his elect from the four winds, from the ends of the earth to the ends of heaven.

§13:32–37 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

περι δε της ημερας εκεινης η της ωρας ουδεις οιδεν

English

But concerning that day or that hour, no one knows, not even the angels in heaven, nor the Son, but only the Father. Be on guard, keep awake. For you do not know when the time will come. Watch therefore—for you do not know when the master of the house will come.

Translator's Notes

  1. §13:26: The “Son of Man coming in clouds” directly alludes to Daniel 7:13–14, where the figure receives dominion from the Ancient of Days. Whether this refers to the destruction of Jerusalem (70 CE), a future cosmic event, or both, has been debated since the patristic era.
