Introduction

Al-Farabi's Ara Ahl al-Madina al-Fadila ("Opinions of the People of the Virtuous City") is the foundational text of Islamic political philosophy. It synthesizes Platonic political theory with Islamic theology to describe the ideal state.

Translation

§Chapter 1: The First Cause · Ara Ahl al-Madina al-Fadila, Chapter 1

The First Existent is the First Cause of the existence of all other existents. It is free from all matter, free from all deficiency, and it has no contrary. Its existence cannot be divided in any way. It is not a body, nor in a body. It is that from which everything else derives its existence. It is perfect, and its perfection consists in its being pure intellect, purely actual, with no potentiality whatsoever.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 1: Al-Farabi's description of the First Cause synthesizes Aristotle's Unmoved Mover with the Neoplatonic One and the Quranic God. The claim that the First Cause is "pure intellect" ('aql mahd) became the standard position of the Islamic Peripatetic tradition and was later critiqued by Ghazali as an unwarranted philosophical imposition on theology.
