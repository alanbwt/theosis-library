Introduction

The story of White Buffalo Calf Woman (Pte Ska Win) is the foundational sacred narrative of the Lakota people. She brought the sacred pipe (chanunpa) and the seven sacred rites that form the basis of Lakota spiritual life.

Translation

§The Coming of White Buffalo Calf Woman · Lakota Oral Tradition, as recorded by Joseph Epes Brown

Two young men were out hunting when they saw a beautiful woman approaching, dressed in white buckskin and carrying a bundle. One man had impure thoughts; a cloud covered him and when it lifted, only his bones remained. The other bowed his head in respect. The woman said: "Go tell your people to prepare a great lodge. I am coming with something holy." When the camp was ready, she entered and unwrapped the sacred pipe. She taught them to pray with it, saying: "With this pipe you will walk upon the earth, and the earth is your grandmother and your mother. Every step you take upon her should be as a prayer." She taught the seven rites, then walked away and turned into a white buffalo calf.

Translator's Notes

  1. §The Coming of White Buffalo Calf Woman: The sacred pipe (chanunpa) is not merely a ritual object but a living covenant between the Lakota people and the spiritual world. The statement that "every step should be as a prayer" establishes a theology in which the sacred is not confined to ceremony but pervades all of ordinary life—a principle that parallels the Sufi concept of ihsan (constant awareness of the divine presence).
