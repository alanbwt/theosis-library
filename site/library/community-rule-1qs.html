Community Rule (1QS) 3.13-4.26: The Two Spirits

Anonymous (Enochic), c. 3rd c. BCE

Introduction

The Community Rule (Serekh ha-Yahad, 1QS) is one of the foundational documents of the Qumran sect, likely composed in the second century BC. Columns 3.13–4.26 contain the Treatise on the Two Spirits, a dualistic theology that divides all humanity between the Spirit of Truth and the Spirit of Falsehood. God has appointed these two spirits to govern human conduct until the “time of visitation,” when evil will be destroyed forever. The elect walk in light; the wicked walk in darkness. There is no middle ground.

This passage is essential to the theosis question because it frames human transformation not as a philosophical ascent but as a cosmic war. Purification is not self-achieved—it is granted by God to those whom he has chosen for the lot of truth. The language of cleansing, renewal, and the “holy spirit” poured out upon the faithful anticipates Christian baptismal theology by at least a century. Whether the early Christians borrowed directly from Qumran or drew on a shared Second Temple vocabulary remains one of the great open questions of scholarship.

From the God of knowledge comes all that is and shall be. Before ever they existed he established their whole design, and when they come into being at their appointed time, they fulfil their task according to his glorious design, without changing anything.

He created humanity to rule the world, and he placed within him two spirits in which to walk until the time of his visitation: the spirits of truth and of falsehood.

§3.20–22 · 1QS Column III, lines 20–22
Manuscript

במעון אור תולדות האמת וממקור חושך תולדות העול

English

In a spring of light is the origin of truth, and from a well of darkness is the origin of falsehood. In the hand of the Prince of Lights is the dominion over all the sons of righteousness; in the ways of light they walk. And in the hand of the Angel of Darkness is all the dominion over the sons of falsehood; and in the ways of darkness they walk.

These are the counsels of the spirit to the sons of truth in the world: to enlighten the heart of man, to make straight before him all the ways of true righteousness, to make his heart fearful of the judgments of God; a spirit of humility, patience, abundant compassion, eternal goodness, insight, understanding, and mighty wisdom.

Translator's Notes

  1. §3.17–19: The “two spirits” doctrine has no direct parallel in the Hebrew Bible, though it resonates with the yetzer ha-tov (good inclination) and yetzer ha-ra (evil inclination) of later rabbinic theology. The key difference is that in the Community Rule, the two spirits are cosmic forces placed in humanity by God, not internal psychological tendencies. This is a predestinarian theology: God has determined the lot of every person before they were born.
  2. §4.20–23: The language of purification through a “holy spirit” (ruah qodesh) sprinkled like “purifying waters” closely parallels later Christian baptismal theology. Whether this passage influenced the early Church or reflects a shared Second Temple purification tradition is debated. The phrase “consume every spirit of falsehood from the midst of his flesh” anticipates the Pauline language of the “war between flesh and spirit” (Galatians 5:17).
