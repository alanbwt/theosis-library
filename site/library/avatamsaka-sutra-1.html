Introduction

The Avataṃsaka Sūtra describes a cosmos of infinite interpenetration: every atom contains every universe, every moment contains all of time. It is the philosophical summit of Mahayana Buddhism.

§1.1-3 · Avataṃsaka Sūtra
Manuscript
Greek

evaṃ mayā śrūtaṃ | ekasmin samaye bhagavān magadheṣu viharati

English

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, at the site of enlightenment, having just attained supreme, perfect awakening. The ground was solid and firm, made of diamond. Bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms in ten buddha-fields gathered from all directions.

§1.4-6 · Avataṃsaka Sūtra
Manuscript
Greek

sarva-kṣetra-paraṃāṇu-gateṣu tathāgata-kāyaṃ pradārśayati

English

In every atom of every land, the Buddha manifests his body. Each manifestation pervades all lands and all assemblies, teaching the dharma according to the needs of beings. This is called the inconceivable liberation of the Tathāgata.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1: The Avataṃsaka’s vision of mutual interpenetration influenced the Huayan school’s doctrine of “the net of Indra”—a cosmic web where every jewel reflects every other jewel. This is holographic ontology two millennia before the term existed.
