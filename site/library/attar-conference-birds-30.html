Introduction

The conclusion of The Conference of the Birds describes the thirty surviving birds arriving at the court of the Simurgh. What they find is the mystical climax of the entire Sufi allegorical tradition.

Translation

§The Arrival · Mantiq al-Tayr, Final Section

At last, after crossing the seven valleys—seeking, love, knowledge, detachment, unity, bewilderment, and annihilation—thirty birds arrived at the court of the Simurgh. They looked and saw the Simurgh was indeed there—but it was they themselves. When they gazed upon the Simurgh, they saw it was themselves; when they looked at themselves, they saw the Simurgh. The world dissolved into a sun of nothingness.

Translator's Notes

  1. §The Arrival: The seven valleys correspond to the Sufi stages (maqamat) of spiritual development. The final valley, annihilation (fana), is the state in which the distinction between seeker and sought collapses. The journey was necessary not to reach something external but to burn away the illusion of separateness.
