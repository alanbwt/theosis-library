Introduction

City of God Book 1 was written in response to the sack of Rome by the Visigoths in 410 CE, defending Christianity against the charge that abandoning the old gods caused Rome’s fall. It launches Augustine’s magisterial theology of history.

§1.1 · Augustine, City of God 1.1
Manuscript
Greek

Gloriosissimam civitatem Dei sive in hoc temporum cursu cum inter impios peregrinatur ex fide vivens

English

The glorious city of God, which in the present course of time is a pilgrim among the ungodly, living by faith, and which now awaits the steadfastness of the eternal seat that it patiently expects—this city I have undertaken to defend against those who prefer their own gods to the Founder of this city.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1.1: The two-cities framework—the City of God and the City of Man, intermingled in history but separated in destiny—became the dominant model of political theology in the Latin West. It shaped debates about church and state from Charlemagne through the Investiture Controversy.
