Heraclitus Fragment 53

Introduction

Fragment 53 presents war (polemos, πόλεμος) as the fundamental cosmic principle. For Heraclitus, strife is not destructive but generative—it is the father that produces all things and assigns each being its station.

§Fragment 53 · DK 22 B53 (Hippolytus, Refutatio IX.9.4)
Manuscript
Greek

πόλεμος πάντων μὲν πατήρ ἐστι πάντων δὲ βασιλεύς, καὶ τοὺς μὲν θεοὺς ἔδειξε τοὺς δὲ ἀνθρώπους, τοὺς μὲν δούλους ἐποίησε τοὺς δὲ ἐλευθέρους

English

War is the father of all and king of all; some he has shown as gods, others as men; some he has made slaves, others free.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Fragment 53: The Greek polemos (πόλεμος) means both armed conflict and cosmic strife. Heraclitus uses it in the latter sense: opposition and tension are the generative forces that produce all differentiation in the cosmos.
