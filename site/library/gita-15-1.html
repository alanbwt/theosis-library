§15:1-5 · Bhagavad Gita 15
Manuscript
Greek

śrī bhagavān uvāca | ūrdhva-mūlam adhaḥ-śākham aśvattham prāhur avyayam

English

The Blessed Lord said: They speak of an imperishable aśvattha tree with its roots above and branches below, whose leaves are the Vedic hymns. Its branches extend below and above, nourished by the guṇas. One must cut this firmly rooted tree with the strong axe of detachment.

Translator's Notes

  1. §15:1: The inverted tree image appears already in the Katha Upanishad (2.3.1). The roots-above symbolism suggests that the source of manifest reality is transcendent and invisible. The instruction to “cut” the tree is unusual—attachment to any manifest form, however sacred, must be severed.
