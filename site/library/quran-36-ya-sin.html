Introduction

Surah Ya-Sin is called "the heart of the Quran" in prophetic tradition. It addresses the core themes of revelation, resurrection, and divine signs in creation, and is traditionally recited for the dying and at funerals.

Greek

يس وَالْقُرْآنِ الْحَكِيمِ إِنَّكَ لَمِنَ الْمُرْسَلِينَ عَلَىـ صِرَاطٍ مُّسْتَقِيمٍ

English

Ya-Sin. By the wise Quran, you are indeed one of the messengers, on a straight path. A revelation sent down by the Almighty, the Most Merciful, that you may warn a people whose forefathers were not warned, and so they are heedless. The word has already been justified against most of them, so they will not believe. We have placed shackles on their necks up to their chins, so their heads are forced up. And We have placed before them a barrier and behind them a barrier and covered them, so they do not see.

Translator's Notes

  1. §36:1: The letters Ya-Sin belong to the huruf al-muqatta'at (disconnected letters) that open 29 surahs. Their meaning remains one of the great mysteries of Quranic interpretation. Some scholars read them as divine names; others as abbreviations; the Sufis treat them as symbols of esoteric knowledge accessible only through spiritual unveiling.
