Introduction

Psalm 46 declares God a refuge amid cosmic upheaval—mountains falling into the sea, nations raging. The command “Be still and know that I am God” punctuates the chaos.

§46:1-5 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ὁ θεὸς ἡμῶν καταφυγὴ καὶ δύναμις

English

God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble. Therefore we will not fear though the earth gives way, though the mountains be moved into the heart of the sea, though its waters roar and foam, though the mountains tremble at its swelling.

§46:10-11 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

σχολάσατε καὶ γνῶτε ὅτι ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ θεός

English

Be still, and know that I am God. I will be exalted among the nations, I will be exalted in the earth. The Lord of hosts is with us; the God of Jacob is our fortress.

Translator's Notes

  1. §46:10: The Greek σχολάσατε (“be at leisure,” “cease”) translates Hebrew הרפו (harpu, “relax, let go”). Luther’s “Ein feste Burg” hymn draws directly on this psalm’s imagery of God as fortress.
