Introduction

Mark 10:35–45 records James and John’s request to sit at Jesus’s right and left in glory. Jesus redefines greatness as service, culminating in the ransom saying. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§10:35–38 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και προσπορευονται αυτω ιακωβος και ιωαννης οι υιοι ζεβεδαιου

English

And James and John, the sons of Zebedee, came up to him and said to him, “Teacher, we want you to do for us whatever we ask of you.” And he said to them, “What do you want me to do for you?” And they said to him, “Grant us to sit, one at your right hand and one at your left, in your glory.” Jesus said to them, “You do not know what you are asking. Are you able to drink the cup that I drink?”

§10:43–45 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ουχ ουτως δε εστιν εν υμιν αλλ ος αν θελη μεγας γενεσθαι εν υμιν

English

But it shall not be so among you. Whoever would be great among you must be your servant, and whoever would be first among you must be slave of all. For even the Son of Man came not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many.

Translator's Notes

  1. §10:45: The word lytron (λύτρον), “ransom,” is the price paid to free a slave or prisoner of war. This verse became foundational for atonement theology: Origen interpreted it as a ransom paid to the devil, while Anselm later reframed it as satisfaction owed to God.
