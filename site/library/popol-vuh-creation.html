Popol Vuh: The Creation of Humanity

Popol Vuh: The Creation of Humanity
K'iche' Maya Scribes, c. 1554-1558 AD
Newberry Library MS; Ximenez transcription

Introduction

The Popol Vuh is the creation epic of the K'iche' Maya — the most complete surviving account of Mesoamerican cosmology. It tells of the gods' repeated failures to create beings capable of worship: first mud people (who dissolved), then wooden people (who were destroyed in a flood), and finally corn people — humanity as we know it. The narrative interweaves creation with the heroic saga of the Hero Twins in the underworld of Xibalba.

K'iche' Maya original and English translation.
§1.1 · PV Preamble
K'iche' Maya

Are u xe oher tzih waral K'iche' ub'i'. Waral xchiqatz'ib'aj wi, xchiqatikib'a' wi oher tzih.

English

This is the beginning of the ancient word, here in the place called K'iche'. Here we shall write, we shall begin the ancient stories.

§1.2 · PV §1
K'iche' Maya

Nab'e chi k'o wi ri kaj, k'o nay puch u wachulew. Xa u tukel k'o wi ri kaj. Xa katzininak, xa kasilanik.

English

First there was only sky, and there was also the surface of the earth. Nothing stirred. Nothing moved. It was silent and still.

§3.1 · PV §3
K'iche' Maya

Ta xkib'an winaq, ixim ub'aqil, ixim u kik'el.

English

Then they made people. Corn was their flesh, corn was their blood.
