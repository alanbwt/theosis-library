Reamker: The Cambodian Ramayana

Reamker: The Cambodian Ramayana
Khmer Court Poets, c. 7th-12th century AD
Angkor Wat bas-reliefs; palm-leaf manuscripts

Introduction

The Reamker (Glory of Rama) is Cambodia's adaptation of the Indian Ramayana, transformed through centuries of Khmer Buddhist culture. The story is carved in bas-relief across 50 meters of gallery at Angkor Wat — the largest religious monument ever built. The Khmer version emphasizes Buddhist themes of karma and compassion absent from the Hindu original, and its characters have become the archetypal figures of Cambodian classical dance.

Khmer

ព្រះរាមបានជាស្ដេចមួយពិសេស គឺជាព្រះនារាយណ៍ដែលចុះមកលើផែនដី។

English

Preah Ream was no ordinary king — he was Narayana who descended to earth.

Khmer

សោកសៅកើតឡើងនៅក្នុងព្រៃ។ ព្រះរាមធ្វើដំណើរ ដើម្បីរក្សាធម៌។

English

Sorrow arose in the forest. Preah Ream journeyed forth to uphold the dharma.
