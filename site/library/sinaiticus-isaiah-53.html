Sinaiticus Isaiah 53

Unknown,

Introduction

Isaiah 53 describes the suffering servant’s death and vindication—despised, rejected, led like a lamb to slaughter. The Ethiopian eunuch was reading this passage when Philip encountered him (Acts 8:32).

§53:1-3 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

κύριε τίς ἐπίστευσεν τῇ ἀκοῇ ἡμῶν

English

Who has believed what he has heard from us? And to whom has the arm of the Lord been revealed? For he grew up before him like a young plant, and like a root out of dry ground; he had no form or majesty that we should look at him. He was despised and rejected by men, a man of sorrows and acquainted with grief.

§53:7-9 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ αὐτὸς διὰ τὸ κεκακῶσθαι οὐκ ἀνοίγει τὸ στόμα

English

He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth; like a lamb that is led to the slaughter, and like a sheep that before its shearers is silent, so he opened not his mouth.

Translator's Notes

  1. §53:7: The lamb imagery is quoted directly in Acts 8:32-33. The association with Passover sacrifice predates Christianity; the Dead Sea Scrolls suggest messianic readings of this passage already existed in Second Temple Judaism.
