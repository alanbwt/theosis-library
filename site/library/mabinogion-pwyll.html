The Mabinogion: Pwyll Prince of Dyfed
Welsh Scribes, c. 11th-14th century AD
White Book of Rhydderch; Red Book of Hergest

Introduction

The Mabinogion preserves the oldest prose literature in Britain — tales of enchantment, sovereignty, and the Otherworld rooted in pre-Christian Celtic mythology. The First Branch follows Pwyll into Annwn (the Otherworld) and introduces Rhiannon, a figure widely identified with the horse-goddess Epona. These tales were composed orally centuries before their transcription.

Middle Welsh

English

Pwyll Prince of Dyfed was lord over the seven cantrefs of Dyfed.

§2 · Pwyll §6
Middle Welsh

Ac ual y byd yn eisted, llyma uorwyn yn dyuot ar uarch canwelw mawr.

And as he sat, behold a maiden on a great pale-white horse coming along the road.
