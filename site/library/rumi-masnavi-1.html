Masnavi: The Song of the Reed

Masnavi: The Song of the Reed
Jalal ad-Din Rumi, 1207-1273 AD
Konya manuscripts

Introduction

The Masnavi is Rumi's masterwork — six books of spiritual verse that he called 'the roots of the roots of the roots of religion.' Its opening image of the reed flute crying for the reed bed from which it was cut is the most famous metaphor in Sufi literature: the human soul's anguish at separation from the divine source. The poem was composed over the last years of Rumi's life and is considered second only to the Quran in the Persian-speaking world.

§1.5-8 · Masnavi I.5-8
Persian

هر کسی کو دور ماند از اصل خویش / باز جوید روزگار وصل خویش

Everyone who has been parted from their source seeks again the time of union.
