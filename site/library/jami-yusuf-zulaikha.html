Introduction

Jami’s Yusuf and Zulaikha (1483 CE) retells the Quranic story of Joseph and Potiphar’s wife as a Sufi romance. Zulaikha’s obsessive love for Joseph’s beauty is transformed through suffering into love for the divine Beauty of which Joseph is merely a reflection.

§Prologue · Jami, Yusuf and Zulaikha
Manuscript
Greek

عشق آمد و از من هرچه بود برد

English

Love came and swept away all trace of me. Nothing remained but the Beloved’s name. Whoever has tasted this wine knows that there is no sobriety more intoxicating than this drunkenness. When the mirror of the heart is polished clear, it reflects the beauty of the Friend. And the one who gazes long enough discovers that the gazer and the gazed-upon are one.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Prologue: Jami, the last great classical Persian poet, synthesizes the entire Sufi poetic tradition. His treatment of Zulaikha transforms her from a figure of illicit desire (as in Genesis 39) into a model of spiritual transformation: her human love, purified through suffering, becomes a vehicle for divine love. This redemptive reading of eros parallels the Christian mystical tradition’s reading of the Song of Songs.
