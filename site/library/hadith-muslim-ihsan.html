Hadith Muslim Ihsan

Unknown,

Introduction

Known as the Hadith of Gabriel, this is considered the most comprehensive single hadith in Islam. It defines the three dimensions of the religion—Islam (submission), Iman (faith), and Ihsan (spiritual excellence)—through a dialogue between the Prophet and Gabriel in human form.

§Hadith 8 · Sahih Muslim, Book 1, Hadith 8
Manuscript
Greek

أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللَّهَ كَأَنَّكَ تَرَاهُ فَإِنْ لَمْ تَكُنْ تَرَاهُ فَإِنَّهُ يَرَاكَ

English

Umar ibn al-Khattab said: One day while we were sitting with the Messenger of God, a man appeared with intensely white clothes and deeply black hair, showing no signs of travel, though none of us recognized him. He sat before the Prophet and said: "O Muhammad, tell me about Islam." He replied: "Islam is to testify that there is no god but God and that Muhammad is His Messenger, to establish prayer, give alms, fast Ramadan, and make pilgrimage if you are able." He said: "Tell me about faith." He replied: "To believe in God, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and divine decree." He said: "Tell me about spiritual excellence." He replied: "To worship God as though you see Him, for if you do not see Him, He surely sees you."

Translator's Notes

  1. §Hadith 8: The definition of ihsan—"to worship God as though you see Him"—became the foundational text of Sufism. The three-tiered structure (Islam, Iman, Ihsan) maps onto the Sufi stages of shari'a (law), tariqa (path), and haqiqa (reality).
