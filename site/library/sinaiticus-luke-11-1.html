Introduction

Luke 11:1–13 presents Jesus’ teaching on prayer, including Luke’s shorter version of the Lord’s Prayer. The Sinaiticus text preserves readings that differ from the longer Matthean form.

§11:1–4 · Codex Sinaiticus, Luke 11:1–4
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ἐγένετο ἐν τῷ εἶναι αὐτὸν ἐν τόπῳ τινὶ προσευχόμενον

English

He was praying in a certain place, and when he finished, one of his disciples said, ‘Lord, teach us to pray.’ And he said, ‘Father, hallowed be your name. Your kingdom come. Give us each day our daily bread, and forgive us our sins, for we ourselves forgive everyone who is indebted to us. And lead us not into temptation.’

Translator's Notes

  1. §11:2: Luke’s Lord’s Prayer is notably shorter than Matthew’s version, lacking “who art in heaven,” “thy will be done on earth as it is in heaven,” and “deliver us from evil.” Most scholars consider Luke’s shorter form closer to the original.
