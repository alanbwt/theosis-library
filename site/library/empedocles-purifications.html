Purifications: The Fallen Daimon

Purifications: The Fallen Daimon
Empedocles, c. 494-434 BCE
Diels-Kranz, Die Fragmente der Vorsokratiker

Introduction

Empedocles of Acragas was philosopher, physician, and mystic — a figure who claimed divine status and taught that the soul is a fallen daimon condemned to cycle through mortal incarnations until purified. His cosmology of four elements governed by Love (Philia) and Strife (Neikos) remained the standard model of physics until the scientific revolution.

§B112 · DK 31 B112
Greek

ὦ φίλοι, οἳ μέγα ἄστυ κατ᾿ ἀκρόπολιν Ἀκράγαντος ναίετ᾿ ἀν᾿ ἄκρα πόλεος, ἀγαθῶν μελεδήμονες ἔργων, χαίρετ᾿· ἐγὼ δ᾿ ὑμῖν θεὸς ἄμβροτος, οὐκέτι θνητός, πωλεῦμαι μετὰ πᾶσι τετιμένος.

English

Friends, who dwell in the great city on the heights of Acragas, devoted to good works: I greet you. I go among you as an immortal god, no longer mortal, honored among all.

§B115 · DK 31 B115
Greek

ἔστιν Ἀνάγκης χρῆμα, θεῶν ψήφισμα παλαιόν, ἀΐδιον, πλατέεσσι κατεσφρηγισμένον ὅρκοις· εὖτέ τις ἀμπλακίῃσι φόνῳ φίλα γυῖα μιήνῃ... τρίς μιν μυρίας ὧρας ἀπὸ μακάρων ἀλάλησθαι.

English

There is an oracle of Necessity, an ancient decree of the gods, eternal, sealed with broad oaths: whenever one of the divine spirits defiles his limbs with the sin of bloodshed... he must wander thrice ten thousand seasons away from the blessed ones.

§B17.7-8 · DK 31 B17
Greek

ἄλλοτε μὲν Φιλότητι συνερχόμεν᾿ εἰς ἓν ἅπαντα, ἄλλοτε δ᾿ αὖ δίχ᾿ ἕκαστα φορεύμενα Νείκεος ἔχθει.

English

At one time all things come together into one through Love; at another, each is carried apart by the hatred of Strife.
