Fragments: The Ship of State
Alcaeus, c. 620-580 BCE
P.Oxy. 2165; Heraclitus Allegoriae

Introduction

Alcaeus of Mytilene, contemporary of Sappho, invented the metaphor of the ship of state tossed in a storm — an image that has been used by every political tradition since, from Plato to Lincoln.

Greek

ἀσυννέτημμι τὼν ἀνέμων στάσιν· τὸ μὲν γὰρ ἔνθεν κῦμα κυλίνδεται, τὸ δ᾿ ἔνθεν.

English

I cannot understand the direction of the winds: one wave rolls from this side, another from that.
