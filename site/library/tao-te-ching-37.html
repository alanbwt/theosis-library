Chapter 37 closes the first half of the Tao Te Ching (the "Book of the Way") with a statement on wu wei—actionless action. The Tao does nothing, yet nothing is left undone.

Greek

道常无为而无不为

English

The Tao never acts, yet nothing is left undone. If kings and lords could hold to it, the ten thousand things would transform of themselves. If after transformation desire should arise, I would press it down with the weight of the nameless uncarved block. The nameless uncarved block is freedom from desire. Being free from desire, one is still, and the world settles of itself.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 37: The "nameless uncarved block" (pu) represents the state of original simplicity before distinctions and desires arise. It is both a cosmological principle (the Tao before differentiation) and a psychological ideal (the mind before conceptual elaboration). The political implication is that the best ruler governs by returning society to this primordial simplicity.
