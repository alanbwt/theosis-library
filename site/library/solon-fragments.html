Fragments: Justice and the City

Fragments: Justice and the City
Solon, c. 640-558 BCE
Aristotle, Athenian Constitution; Stobaeus

Introduction

Solon, elected archon of Athens in 594 BCE, wrote poetry to explain and justify his constitutional reforms. His fragments represent the earliest surviving political philosophy in the West — written not as abstract theory but as legislation backed by verse.

Greek

ἡμετέρη δὲ πόλις κατὰ μὲν Διὸς οὔποτ᾿ ὀλεῖται αἶσαν... ἀστοὶ δ᾿ αὐτοὶ ἀμηχανίῃσι λυγρῇσιν ὄλβον ἐκ μεγάλης ὕβριος ἠρέθισαν.

English

Our city will never be destroyed by the decree of Zeus... but the citizens themselves by their recklessness wish to ruin the great city, persuaded by wealth and the folly of their leaders.
