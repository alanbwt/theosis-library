Introduction

Ezekiel 37 is the vision of the valley of dry bones. God commands the prophet to prophesy over the bones; they reassemble, receive sinews and flesh, and the breath of life enters them.

§37:1-6 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ἐγένετο ἐπ᾽ ἐμὲ χεὶρ κυρίου

English

The hand of the Lord was upon me, and he brought me out in the Spirit of the Lord and set me down in the middle of the valley; it was full of bones. And he said to me, “Son of man, can these bones live?” And I answered, “O Lord God, you know.”

§37:9-14 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

προφήτευσον ἐπὶ τὸ πνεῦμα

English

Then he said to me, “Prophesy to the breath; prophesy, son of man, and say to the breath, Thus says the Lord God: Come from the four winds, O breath, and breathe upon these slain, that they may live.” So I prophesied as he commanded me, and the breath came into them, and they lived and stood on their feet, an exceedingly great army.

Translator's Notes

  1. §37:9: The Hebrew רוח (ruach) and Greek πνεῦμα both mean “wind,” “breath,” and “spirit” simultaneously. Ezekiel exploits all three senses: the wind comes, the corpses breathe, and the Spirit animates. Christian Pentecost theology draws directly on this triple meaning.
