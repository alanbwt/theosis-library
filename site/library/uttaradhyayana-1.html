Introduction

The Uttaradhyayana Sutra records the final teachings of Mahavira to his chief disciples before his liberation. The first lecture addresses the impermanence of worldly attachments and the urgency of the spiritual path.

Translation

§Lecture 1: On Discipline · Uttaradhyayana Sutra, Lecture 1

I say: thus has spoken the venerable ascetic Mahavira. Having given up all worldly ties, a wise person should wander about, treating all creatures as he himself would be treated. Knowing that all bonds are the result of attachment, and that attachment is the cause of rebirth, the monk should renounce possessions, comforts, and even the desire for liberation itself, dwelling in equanimity.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Lecture 1: The instruction to renounce even the desire for liberation is a sophisticated paradox: attachment to freedom is itself a form of bondage. This teaching parallels the Buddhist concept of non-attachment to nirvana found in the Mahayana tradition and the Taoist idea that seeking the Tao through deliberate effort is self-defeating.
