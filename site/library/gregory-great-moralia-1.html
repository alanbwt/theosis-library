Introduction

Gregory the Great’s Moralia in Job is a massive commentary blending literal, allegorical, and moral interpretation of the book of Job. It became the standard medieval handbook of moral theology and contemplative spirituality.

§Preface 1–3 · Gregory the Great, Moralia in Job, Preface
Manuscript
Greek

Beatus Iob venturi speculum nostrae vitae tenuit

English

Blessed Job holds before us a mirror of the present life. For what does the history of this one man suffering represent but the passion of Holy Church? We must examine his words on three levels: first historically, then allegorically, then morally—for Sacred Scripture transcends all other knowledge in its manner of expression.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Preface: Gregory’s threefold method of interpretation—historical, allegorical, moral—systematized patristic hermeneutics for the medieval period. His reading of Job as a figure of Christ and the Church transformed a wisdom text into a christological and ecclesiological treatise.
