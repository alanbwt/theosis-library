Introduction

The Dogon people of Mali preserve one of the most detailed creation narratives in African oral tradition. Their cosmogony describes the creator god Amma and the cosmic egg from which the universe emerged through a process of vibration and spiraling motion.

Translation

§Creation by Amma · Dogon Oral Tradition, as recorded by Marcel Griaule

In the beginning, Amma, the supreme god, was alone. He held within himself the plan of the world, coiled like a seed in the cosmic egg. The egg contained four elements and four directions. Amma began to spin and the egg vibrated. From the vibration came the first word, and from the word came the spiraling motion that created the stars. But the egg broke open too early, and the first being, the Pale Fox, emerged incomplete—bringing disorder into the world. To restore order, Amma sacrificed the Nommo, the first perfect beings, and from their dismembered body the world was organized.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Creation by Amma: The Dogon cosmogony's emphasis on vibration and spiraling motion as the mechanism of creation is striking. The sacrificial dismemberment of the Nommo to create cosmic order parallels the Vedic hymn of Purusha (Rigveda 10.90) and the Norse account of Ymir, suggesting either deep structural parallels in human mythological thinking or ancient connections yet to be fully understood.
