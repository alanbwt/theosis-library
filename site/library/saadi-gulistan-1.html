Introduction

Saadi’s Gulistan (Rose Garden, 1258 CE) is one of the most widely read works in Persian literature. It combines prose and verse to convey ethical wisdom through stories, aphorisms, and personal anecdotes. Its opening verse adorns the UN building in New York.

§Preface · Saadi, Gulistan
Manuscript
Greek

بنی آدم اعضای یکدیگرند / که در آفرینش ز یک گوهرند

English

The children of Adam are members of one body, for they were created from a single essence. When fate brings suffering to one member, the other members cannot remain at rest. You who are indifferent to the suffering of others do not deserve to be called human.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Preface: Saadi’s poem on human solidarity—“the children of Adam are members of one body”—derives from a hadith of the Prophet Muhammad and echoes the Pauline image of the body of Christ (1 Corinthians 12). It was inscribed at the entrance of the United Nations in 2005 and represents the universalist strand within Islamic ethics.
