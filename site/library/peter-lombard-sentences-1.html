Introduction

§Book 1, Distinction 1 · Peter Lombard, Sentences
Manuscript
Greek

[Latin: Omnis doctrina vel rerum est vel signorum]

English

All teaching concerns things or signs. Things are to be enjoyed or used. Those things which are to be enjoyed make us blessed; those which are to be used help us on our way to blessedness. The thing which is to be enjoyed is the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, the Trinity itself, which is the supreme and common good shared by all who enjoy it.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Distinction 1: The distinction between things to be “enjoyed” (frui) and things to be “used” (uti) comes directly from Augustine’s De Doctrina Christiana. Only God is to be enjoyed as an end; everything else is a means. This framework structures the entire four-book work and, through it, centuries of Scholastic theology.
