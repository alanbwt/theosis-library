Mundaka Upanishad: Two Birds on One Tree

Mundaka Upanishad: Two Birds on One Tree
Vedic Seers, c. 1200-1000 BCE
Atharvaveda tradition

Introduction

Two birds sit on one tree: one eats the fruit, the other watches — the self and the Self.

Sanskrit original and English translation.
§3.1 · MuU 3.1.1
Sanskrit

dvā suparṇā sayujā sakhāyā samānaṃ vṛkṣaṃ pariṣasvajāte | tayor anyaḥ pippalaṃ svādv atty anaśnann anyo abhicākaśīti

English

Two birds, companions and friends, cling to the same tree. One eats the sweet fruit; the other looks on without eating.
