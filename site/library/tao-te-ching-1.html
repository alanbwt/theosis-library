Tao Te Ching 1 + 25: The Tao That Cannot Be Spoken

        

        
Lao Tzu (attributed), c. 6th c. BCE

        

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
Chapter 1 of the Tao Te Ching is the most famous opening in Eastern philosophy. In just 59 Chinese characters, Laozi establishes the core paradox of Taoism: the ultimate reality (the Tao) cannot be put into words, yet it is the source of all things. The chapter has been translated thousands of times in dozens of languages, and no two translations agree perfectly — proof of its own thesis.

            §1.1-2 · DDJ 1
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Chinese

            
道可道，非常道。名可名，非常名。

          


          

            
English

            
The Tao that can be spoken is not the eternal Tao. The name that can be named is not the eternal name.

          

        

        
        


          

            §1.3-4 · DDJ 1
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Chinese

            
無名天地之始；有名萬物之母。

          


          

            
English

            
The nameless is the origin of heaven and earth. The named is the mother of the ten thousand things.

          

        

        
        


          

            §1.5-6 · DDJ 1
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Chinese

            
故常無欲，以觀其妙；常有欲，以觀其徼。

          


          

            
English

            
Therefore, ever desireless, one beholds the mystery. Ever desiring, one beholds the manifestations.

          

        

        
        


          

            §1.7 · DDJ 1
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Chinese

            
此兩者，同出而異名，同謂之玄。

          


          

            
English

            
These two come from the same source but have different names. Both may be called mysterious.

          

        

        
        


          

            §1.8 · DDJ 1
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Chinese

            
玄之又玄，眾妙之門。

          


          

            
English

            
Mystery upon mystery — the gateway to all wonders.
