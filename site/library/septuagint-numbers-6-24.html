Introduction

Numbers 6:24–26 contains the Priestly Blessing (Birkat Kohanim), the oldest biblical text confirmed by archaeology (found on the Ketef Hinnom silver scrolls, c. 600 BCE). Its threefold invocation of the divine name anticipates Trinitarian readings. From the Septuagint.

§6:24–26 · Septuagint
Manuscript
Greek

εὐλογήσαι σε κύριος καὶ φυλάξαι σε · ἐπιφάναι κύριος τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτοῦ ἐπὶ σὲ καὶ ἐλεήσαι σε · ἐπάραι κύριος τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτοῦ ἐπὶ σὲ καὶ δῴη σοι εἰρήνην

English

May the Lord bless you and keep you. May the Lord make his face shine upon you and be gracious to you. May the Lord lift up his countenance upon you and give you peace.

Translator's Notes

  1. §6:25: The image of God’s face shining (ἐπιφάναι πρόσωπον) connects to the theology of divine light developed in the Transfiguration narratives and in Palamite theology. The blessing asks not merely for protection but for direct encounter with the divine presence.
