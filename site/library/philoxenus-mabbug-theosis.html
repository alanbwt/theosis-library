Homily on 'He Who Sees Me Sees the Father' (First English Translation)

Philoxenus of Mabbug, c. 440-523 AD
First English Translation

Introduction

Philoxenus of Mabbug (c. 440–523 AD) was one of the most important Syriac theologians. This homily, never before translated into English, explores how seeing Christ is seeing the Father, and how this vision transforms the seer.

Translation

§1 · Syriac text

He who sees me sees the Father. Not because I am the Father, but because the Father dwells in me so completely that to see me is to see him. And if you have seen the Father in me, then know that the same indwelling is offered to you: that the Father may dwell in you as he dwells in me, and that you too may become transparent to the divine light.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1: This is the first English translation of this homily. Philoxenus develops a Syriac theology of theosis through divine indwelling that parallels but differs from Greek patristic formulations.
