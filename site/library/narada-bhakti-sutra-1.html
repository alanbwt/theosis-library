Introduction

The Narada Bhakti Sutra defines devotion (bhakti) as supreme love of God, surpassing knowledge, action, and renunciation as the path to liberation. It is the most concise and influential treatise on the philosophy of devotion in Hinduism.

अथातो भक्तिं व्याख्यास्यामः। सा त्वस्मिन् परमप्रेमरूपा

English

Now we shall expound the teaching of devotion. Devotion is supreme love of God. It is the nature of immortal bliss. Having attained it, a person becomes perfect, immortal, and satisfied. Having attained it, one desires nothing, grieves not, hates not, delights not in anything else, and makes no effort for selfish ends.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Sutra 2: Defining bhakti as “supreme love” (parama prema rupa) rather than ritual worship or intellectual assent places the emotional relationship with God at the center of spiritual life. This parallels the Christian mystical tradition’s emphasis on love over knowledge as the highest mode of union with the divine.
