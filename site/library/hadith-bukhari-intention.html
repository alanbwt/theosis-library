Introduction

This hadith on intention (niyyah) is the first entry in Sahih al-Bukhari, the most authoritative hadith collection in Sunni Islam. It establishes that the moral value of any action is determined entirely by the intention behind it.

§Hadith 1 · Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 1, Hadith 1
Manuscript
Greek

إِنَّمَا الأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ وَإِنَّمَا لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مَا نَوَى

English

The Commander of the Faithful, Umar ibn al-Khattab, said: I heard the Messenger of God say: "Actions are only by intentions, and every person shall have only what they intended. So whoever emigrated for the sake of God and His Messenger, then his emigration was for God and His Messenger. And whoever emigrated for worldly gain or to marry a woman, then his emigration was for what he emigrated for."

Translator's Notes

  1. §Hadith 1: Al-Bukhari's decision to open his collection with this hadith was a deliberate methodological statement: the validity of his own scholarly work depends on his intention being purely for God's sake. The hadith became the foundation of Islamic jurisprudence's doctrine that ritual acts require conscious intention to be valid.
