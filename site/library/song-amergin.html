The Song of Amergin

The Song of Amergin
Irish Scribes, c. 7th-12th century AD
Lebor Gabala Erenn; Book of Leinster

Introduction

The Song of Amergin is attributed to the first Gaelic poet to set foot on Irish soil. In it, the druid-poet claims identity with every force of nature — wind, wave, hawk, dewdrop, salmon, hill — in a litany that scholars compare to Hindu and Egyptian 'I am' declarations. It may preserve the oldest stratum of Celtic cosmological thought.

Old Irish original and English translation.
§1 · LGE
Old Irish

Am gaeth i m-muir. Am tond trethan. Am fuaim mara.

English

I am wind on sea. I am ocean wave. I am roar of sea.

§2 · LGE
Old Irish

Am dam secht ndirend. Am loch i m-maigh. Am bri danae.

English

I am a stag of seven tines. I am a lake on a plain. I am a hill of poetry.
