Introduction

Matthew 8:23–27 recounts the stilling of the storm on the Sea of Galilee. The disciples’ question—“What sort of man is this?”—frames the Christological inquiry of the entire Gospel. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§8:23–25 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και εμβαντι αυτω εις το πλοιον ηκολουθησαν αυτω οι μαθηται αυτου

English

And when he got into the boat, his disciples followed him. And behold, a great tempest arose on the sea, so that the boat was being swamped by the waves. But he was sleeping. And they came and woke him, saying, “Lord, save us! We are perishing.”

§8:26–27 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και λεγει αυτοις τι δειλοι εστε ολιγοπιστοι

English

And he said to them, “Why are you afraid, you of little faith?” Then he rose and rebuked the winds and the sea, and there was a great calm. And the men marvelled, saying, “What sort of man is this, that even the winds and the sea obey him?”

Translator's Notes

  1. §8:27: The question “What sort of man is this?” (potapos estin houtos) implies that Jesus transcends ordinary human categories. Command over wind and sea was an attribute of God alone in the Hebrew Bible (Psalm 107:29).
