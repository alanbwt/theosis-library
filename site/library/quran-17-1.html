Introduction

Surah Al-Isra (The Night Journey) opens with the miraculous night journey of the Prophet from Mecca to Jerusalem and thence to the heavens. This event (isra’ and mi’raj) is central to Islamic spirituality and the origin of the five daily prayers.

§17:1 · Quran, Surah 17:1
Manuscript
Greek

سُبْحَـنَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا

English

Exalted is He who took His servant by night from the Sacred Mosque to the Farthest Mosque, whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Seeing.

Translator's Notes

  1. §17:1: The “Farthest Mosque” (al-Masjid al-Aqsa) is traditionally identified with the Temple Mount in Jerusalem, making this verse the Quranic basis for Jerusalem’s sanctity in Islam. The mi’raj tradition elaborated in hadith literature became one of the richest sources of Islamic mystical imagery.
