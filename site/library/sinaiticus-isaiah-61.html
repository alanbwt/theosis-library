Sinaiticus Isaiah 61

Unknown,

Introduction

Isaiah 61 announces good news to the poor, liberty to captives, and the year of the Lord’s favor. Jesus reads this passage in the Nazareth synagogue and declares it fulfilled (Luke 4:18–21).

§61:1-3 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

πνεῦμα κυρίου ἐπ᾽ ἐμέ οὗ εἵνεκεν ἔχρισέν με

English

The Spirit of the Lord God is upon me, because the Lord has anointed me to bring good news to the poor; he has sent me to bind up the brokenhearted, to proclaim liberty to the captives, and the opening of the prison to those who are bound; to proclaim the year of the Lord’s favor.

§61:10-11 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

εὐφροσύνῃ εὐφρανθήσομαι ἐπὶ τῷ κυρίῳ

English

I will greatly rejoice in the Lord; my soul shall exult in my God, for he has clothed me with the garments of salvation; he has covered me with the robe of righteousness, as a bridegroom decks himself like a priest with a beautiful headdress.

Translator's Notes

  1. §61:1: The “anointing” connects directly to the title “Christ” (Χριστός, “anointed one”). Luke’s account of Jesus stopping mid-verse—omitting “the day of vengeance of our God”—has been read as a deliberate theological redaction emphasizing mercy over judgment.
