Introduction

Mark 5:1–20 recounts the exorcism of the Gerasene demoniac, the most dramatic encounter with unclean spirits in the Gospels. The demons’ recognition of Jesus as “Son of the Most High God” is a key Christological confession. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§5:1–5 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και ηλθον εις το περαν της θαλασσης εις την χωραν των γερασηνων

English

They came to the other side of the sea, to the country of the Gerasenes. And when Jesus had stepped out of the boat, immediately there met him out of the tombs a man with an unclean spirit. He lived among the tombs, and no one could bind him anymore, not even with a chain.

§5:7–9 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και κραξας φωνη μεγαλη λεγει τι εμοι και σοι ιησου υιε του θεου του υψιστου

English

And crying out with a loud voice, he said, “What have you to do with me, Jesus, Son of the Most High God? I adjure you by God, do not torment me.” For he was saying to him, “Come out of the man, you unclean spirit!” And Jesus asked him, “What is your name?” He replied, “My name is Legion, for we are many.”

Translator's Notes

  1. §5:9: The name “Legion” (Legeon, a Latin loanword in Greek) carries both military and political connotations. A Roman legion comprised roughly five thousand soldiers. The demons’ name evokes the occupying power, suggesting a link between spiritual and political oppression.
