Acaranga Sutra: The Book of Good Conduct
Jain Acharyas, c. 6th-3rd century BCE
Shvetambara Jain Agamas

Introduction

The Acaranga Sutra, dated to the 4th century BCE, is the oldest text in the Jain canon. It preserves the radical teachings of Mahavira on ahimsa — non-violence extended not just to humans and animals but to insects, plants, water, fire, earth, and air, all of which contain living souls. This extreme ethic of non-harm, which predates and influenced Buddhist compassion doctrine, makes the Jain tradition one of the most philosophically rigorous in the ancient world.

§1.1 · Acar. 1.1
Ardhamagadhi Prakrit

savvesim pāṇāṇam savvesim bhūyāṇam savvesim jīvāṇam na haṃtavvā

English

All breathing things, all existing things, all living beings should not be harmed.

§1.2 · Acar. 1.2
Ardhamagadhi Prakrit

je pāṇe vahe se appāṇam vahe

English

Whoever harms any living thing harms his own soul.
