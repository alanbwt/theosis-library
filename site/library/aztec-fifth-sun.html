Legend of the Fifth Sun: Nanahuatzin's Sacrifice

Legend of the Fifth Sun: Nanahuatzin's Sacrifice
Aztec Elders, c. 16th century AD
Leyenda de los Soles (Codex Chimalpopoca)

Introduction

The Aztec cosmos had been created and destroyed four times before our present age — the Fifth Sun. This narrative tells how the gods gathered at Teotihuacan and how the humble, pox-covered Nanahuatzin had the courage to leap into the sacred fire to become the sun, while the proud Tecuciztecatl hesitated. The myth establishes the Aztec theology of reciprocal sacrifice: the sun exists because a god died, and it must be sustained by human offering.

Nahuatl original and English translation.
§1 · LdlS §1
Nahuatl

In ic macuilli tonatiuh, in iuh quitoaya huehuetque.

English

Concerning the fifth sun, as the elders used to say.

§2 · LdlS §3
Nahuatl

Niman ye ic motlalitemoc Nanahuatzin. Niman ye onaci in tlecuilli, niman ye ic oncalac in tletl.

English

Then Nanahuatzin ran and threw himself into the fire. He reached the great hearth and cast himself into the flames.
