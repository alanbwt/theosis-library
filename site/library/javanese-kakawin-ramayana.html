Kakawin Ramayana: The Old Javanese Ramayana
Javanese Court Poets, c. 9th-14th century AD
Palm-leaf manuscripts; Prambanan reliefs

Introduction

The Kakawin Ramayana, composed around 870 AD in the Old Javanese court language of Kawi, is the oldest surviving literary work in the Javanese tradition. It adapts the Indian epic through the sophisticated lens of Javanese aesthetics, with elaborate descriptions of nature that have no parallel in the Sanskrit original. The poem was the literary companion to the monumental Prambanan temple complex, where scenes from the Ramayana are carved in stone.

§1.1 · KR 1.1
Old Javanese (Kawi)

Oṁ awighnam astu. Ikang Laṅkā nagara nikang rākṣasa.

English

Om, may there be no obstacle. Lanka was the city of the rakshasas.

§1.2 · KR 1.2
Old Javanese (Kawi)

Lwir ning Rāma sira pinakottama ning jagat.

English

Rama was like the highest being in the world.
