Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 11: The Vision of the Universal Form

        
Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 11: The Vision of the Universal Form

        
Attributed to Vyasa, legendary

        
Critical Edition (BORI)

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
Bhagavad Gita Chapter 11 contains the most overwhelming theophany in religious literature. Arjuna asks to see Krishna's true form, and Krishna grants him a divine eye. What Arjuna sees is the entire cosmos — every god, every being, all of time and space — gathered into one infinite body with countless faces, eyes, and arms. The vision is so terrifying that Arjuna begs Krishna to return to his familiar human form. The phrase 'I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds' (verse 32) was famously quoted by Robert Oppenheimer at the first atomic bomb test.

            §11.1-4 · BG 11.1-4
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
arjuna uvāca: mad-anugrahāya paramaṃ guhyam adhyātma-saṃjñitam | yat tvayoktaṃ vacas tena moho 'yaṃ vigato mama || bhavāpyayau hi bhūtānāṃ śrutau vistaraśo mayā | tvattaḥ kamala-patrākṣa māhātmyam api cāvyayam || evam etad yathāttha tvam ātmānaṃ parameśvara | draṣṭum icchāmi te rūpam aiśvaraṃ puruṣottama ||

          


          

            
English

            
Arjuna said: As a favor to me, you have spoken about the supreme secret known as the Self, and by your words my delusion has been removed. The origin and dissolution of beings I have heard from you in detail, O lotus-eyed Lord, and also of your imperishable greatness. Even so as you have described yourself, O Supreme Lord, I now wish to see your divine form, O Supreme Person.

          

        

        
        


          

            §11.5-8 · BG 11.5-8
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
śrī-bhagavān uvāca: paśya me pārtha rūpāṇi śataśo 'tha sahasraśaḥ | nānā-vidhāni divyāni nānā-varṇākṛtīni ca || paśyādityān vasūn rudrān aśvinau marutas tathā | bahūny adṛṣṭa-pūrvāṇi paśyāścaryāṇi bhārata || na tu māṃ śakyase draṣṭum anenaiva svacakṣuṣā | divyaṃ dadāmi te cakṣuḥ paśya me yogam aiśvaram ||

          


          

            
English

            
The Blessed Lord said: Behold, O Partha, my forms by hundreds and thousands, of various kinds, divine, of various colors and shapes. Behold the Adityas, the Vasus, the Rudras, the two Ashvins, and the Maruts; behold many wonders never seen before, O Bharata. Here, today, behold the entire universe — moving and unmoving — gathered together in this body of mine, and whatever else you wish to see. But you cannot see me with your own eye. I give you a divine eye. Behold my divine power.

          

        

        
        


          

            §11.10-13 · BG 11.10-13
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
aneka-vaktra-nayanam anekādbhuta-darśanam | aneka-divyābharaṇaṃ divyānekodyatāyudham || divya-mālyāmbara-dharaṃ divya-gandhānulepanam | sarvāścarya-mayaṃ devam anantaṃ viśvato-mukham ||

          


          

            
English

            
With many mouths and eyes, with many wondrous appearances, with many divine ornaments, with many divine weapons raised — wearing divine garlands and garments, anointed with divine fragrances, all-marvelous, infinite, with faces in every direction. There Arjuna saw the entire universe with its divisions, divided in many ways, all gathered together as one in the body of the God of gods.

          

        

        
        


          

            §11.12 · BG 11.12
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
divi sūrya-sahasrasya bhaved yugapad utthitā | yadi bhāḥ sadṛśī sā syād bhāsas tasya mahātmanaḥ ||

          


          

            
English

            
If the splendor of a thousand suns were to blaze all at once in the sky, that might resemble the splendor of that great Soul.

          

        

        
        


          

            §11.32 · BG 11.32
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
śrī-bhagavān uvāca: kālo 'smi loka-kṣaya-kṛt pravṛddho lokān samāhartum iha pravṛttaḥ | ṛte 'pi tvāṃ na bhaviṣyanti sarve ye 'vasthitāḥ pratyanīkeṣu yodhāḥ ||

          


          

            
English

            
The Blessed Lord said: I am Time, the great destroyer of worlds, and I have come here to destroy these people. Even without your action, all the warriors arrayed in the opposing armies shall not survive.

          

        

        
        


          

            §11.36-40 · BG 11.36-40
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
arjuna uvāca: sthāne hṛṣīkeśa tava prakīrtyā jagat prahṛṣyaty anurajyate ca | rakṣāṃsi bhītāni diśo dravanti sarve namasyanti ca siddha-saṅghāḥ ||

          


          

            
English

            
Arjuna said: O Hrishikesha, rightly the world rejoices and is delighted in your glorification. The demons fly away in fear in all directions, and the hosts of perfected beings bow before you. And why should they not bow, O Great One, who are greater even than Brahma, the original creator? O infinite, O Lord of the gods, O refuge of the universe — you are the imperishable, you are being and non-being and that which is beyond both. You are the primal God, the ancient Person; you are the supreme treasure of this universe. You are the knower and the known. By you the universe is pervaded, O you of infinite forms. I bow to you from in front, I bow from behind, from every side, O All! Infinite in power and immeasurable in might, you pervade all, therefore you are All.

          

        

        
        


          

            §11.51-55 · BG 11.51-55
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
arjuna uvāca: dṛṣṭvedaṃ mānuṣaṃ rūpaṃ tava saumyaṃ janārdana | idānīm asmi saṃvṛttaḥ sa-cetāḥ prakṛtiṃ gataḥ ||

          


          

            
English

            
Arjuna said: Having seen this human form of yours so gentle, O Janardana, now I am composed and have returned to my normal nature. The Blessed Lord said: This form of mine which you have seen is very difficult to behold. Even the gods always long to see it. Not by the Vedas, nor by austerity, nor by gifts, nor by sacrifice can I be seen as you have seen me. But by undivided devotion alone can I be known and seen and entered into, O Arjuna. He who does work for me, and considers me the supreme, who is devoted to me, free from attachment, without enmity to any being — he comes to me, O Pandava.
