Introduction

The so-called Mithras Liturgy (c. 3rd–4th century CE) is preserved in the Paris Magical Papyrus (PGM IV.475–829). Though its connection to institutional Mithraism is debated, it describes an ecstatic ascent through planetary spheres to a face-to-face encounter with the supreme god.

§Lines 475–510 · PGM IV.475–510
Manuscript
Greek

προνοία καὶ ψυχή, χαρίσασθέ μοι τὰ ἱερὰ ταῦτα γράφοντι· ἀρχὴ τῆς ἀρχῆς μου πρώτη, πνεῦμα πνεύματος, πῦρ πυρὸς ἐν ἐμοί

English

Be gracious to me, O Providence and Psyche, as I write these mysteries handed down not for gain but for instruction alone. First origin of my origin, first beginning of my beginning, spirit of spirit, fire of fire in me, first of the fire given to me for the mixture of the mixtures in me.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Lines 475–510: The formula “first origin of my origin” (ἀρχὴ τῆς ἀρχῆς μου) claims identity between the practitioner’s innermost essence and the divine source. This pattern of asserting ontological kinship with God recurs across Hermetic, Gnostic, and Neoplatonic traditions.
