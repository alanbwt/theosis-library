Quran, Sura 112: Al-Ikhlas

        
Attributed to Muhammad, 570-632 AD

        
Standard Uthmanic text

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
Sura 112, Al-Ikhlas ('Sincerity'), is the most concise statement of Islamic monotheism — only four short verses. The Prophet Muhammad said it is equivalent to one-third of the entire Quran. It defines God as One, eternal, neither begetting nor begotten, without equal — a direct rejection of Trinitarian and polytheistic theologies.
