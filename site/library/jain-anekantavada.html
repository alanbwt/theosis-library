Anekantavada: The Doctrine of Many-Sidedness

Anekantavada: The Doctrine of Many-Sidedness
Umasvati, c. 2nd century AD
Jain philosophical tradition

Introduction

The Jain epistemological principle: reality has infinite aspects, and no single perspective captures the whole truth.

§1 · jain-anekantavada §1

Reality is complex. What is true from one standpoint may be false from another. The wise person sees all sides.
