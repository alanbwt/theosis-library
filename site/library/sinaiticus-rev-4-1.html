Introduction

Revelation 4:1–11 describes John’s vision of the heavenly throne room, with the four living creatures and twenty-four elders in perpetual worship. The imagery synthesizes Ezekiel 1, Isaiah 6, and Daniel 7. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§4:1–3 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

μετα ταυτα ειδον και ιδου θυρα ηνεωγμενη εν τω ουρανω

English

After this I looked, and behold, a door standing open in heaven! And the first voice, which I had heard speaking to me like a trumpet, said, “Come up here, and I will show you what must take place after this.” At once I was in the Spirit, and behold, a throne stood in heaven, with one seated on the throne. And he who sat there had the appearance of jasper and carnelian, and around the throne was a rainbow that had the appearance of an emerald.

§4:8–11 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και τα τεσσαρα ζωα εν καθ εν αυτων εχων ανα πτερυγας εξ

English

And the four living creatures, each of them with six wings, are full of eyes all around and within, and day and night they never cease to say, “Holy, holy, holy, is the Lord God Almighty, who was and is and is to come!” And the twenty-four elders fall down before him who is seated on the throne and cast their crowns before the throne, saying, “Worthy are you, our Lord and God, to receive glory and honor and power, for you created all things.”

Translator's Notes

  1. §4:8: The Trisagion (“Holy, holy, holy”) echoes Isaiah 6:3 and became one of the most ancient liturgical acclamations in Christian worship. The addition of “who was and is and is to come” transforms the timeless seraphic hymn into a declaration about God’s relationship to history.
