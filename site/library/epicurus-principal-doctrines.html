Introduction

The Kyriai Doxai (Principal Doctrines) are forty authoritative maxims summarizing Epicurean philosophy. The first four—the “tetrapharmakos” or fourfold remedy—address the gods, death, pleasure, and pain, removing the four chief sources of human anxiety.

§Doctrines 1–4 · Diogenes Laertius X.139–142
Manuscript
Greek

τὸ μακάριον καὶ ἄφθαρτον οὔτε αὐτὸ πράγματα ἔχει οὔτε ἄλλῳ παρέχει

English

The blessed and imperishable being has no troubles itself nor does it cause trouble for others. A being so blessed is not affected by anger or favor, for all such things belong to weakness. Death is nothing to us; for what has been dissolved has no sensation, and what has no sensation is nothing to us. The limit of the magnitude of pleasures is the removal of all pain.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Doctrines 1–4: The tetrapharmakos was the practical core of Epicureanism: God is not to be feared, death is not to be worried about, what is good is easy to get, what is terrible is easy to endure. These four principles were meant to cure the soul of unnecessary suffering.
