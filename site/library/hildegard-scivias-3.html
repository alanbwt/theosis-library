Translation

§Book 3, Vision 13: The Symphony of the Blessed · Scivias, Book 3, Vision 13

Then I saw the lucent sky, in which I heard different kinds of music marvelously embodying all the meanings I had heard before. I heard the praises of the joyous citizens of heaven, steadfastly persevering in the ways of truth, and the lament of those called back to those praises. And the music of the virtues, exhorting one another to secure the salvation of the peoples ensnared by the devil. And the virtues destroyed his snares, so that the faithful at last passed through penitence from the darkness to the light.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Vision 13: Hildegard's visions are uniquely synesthetic: she sees sounds and hears colors. Her theology of music as a vehicle of divine truth led her to compose over 70 liturgical songs, making her the most prolific known composer of the Middle Ages. She argued that music was the primary means by which the human soul reconnects with its prelapsarian harmony.
