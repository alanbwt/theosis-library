Empedocles Fragment 17

Introduction

Fragment 17 presents Empedocles’s cosmology of Love and Strife, the two forces that alternately unite and separate the four roots (earth, water, air, fire). This cosmic cycle of harmony and dissolution governs all generation and destruction.

§Fragment 17 (lines 1–13) · DK 31 B17 (Simplicius, In Phys. 158.1–15)
Manuscript
Greek

δίπλ᾽ ἐρέω· τοτὲ μὲν γὰρ ἓν ἐξ πλεόνων μάθεν συμφύεσθαι, τοτὲ δ᾽ αὖ διέφυ πλέον᾽ ἐξ ἑνὸς εἶναι

English

I shall tell a double tale: at one time they grew to be one alone out of many, and at another they grew apart again to be many out of one. Double is the birth of mortal things and double their passing. For Love brings them all together into one, while Strife bears them apart again.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Fragment 17: Empedocles’s Love (Φιλία) and Strife (Νεῖκος) are not mere metaphors but cosmological forces. Aristotle classified them as efficient causes, and the Neoplatonists saw in Love a prefiguration of the emanative return to the One.
