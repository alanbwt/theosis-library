Introduction

Surah Az-Zilzal ("The Earthquake") is one of the shortest and most vivid descriptions of the Day of Judgment in the Quran. It declares that even an atom's weight of good or evil will be seen.

§99:1-8 · Quran, Surah 99:1-8
Manuscript
Greek

إِذَا زُلْزِلَتِ الْأَرْضُ زِلْزَالَهَا وَأَخْرَجَتِ الْأَرْضُ أَثْقَالَهَا

English

When the earth is shaken with its final earthquake, and the earth discharges its burdens, and the human being says, "What is wrong with it?"—on that Day it will report its news, because your Lord has inspired it. On that Day people will proceed in separate groups to be shown their deeds. So whoever does an atom’s weight of good will see it, and whoever does an atom’s weight of evil will see it.

Translator's Notes

  1. §99:7-8: The final two verses—"whoever does an atom’s weight (mithqala dharratin) of good will see it"—became one of the most quoted ethical maxims in Islamic civilization. The Prophet reportedly called these "the most comprehensive verses" in the Quran, establishing absolute moral accountability with no threshold below which deeds go unrecorded.
