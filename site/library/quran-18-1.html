§18:1-5 · Quran, Surah 18:1-5
Manuscript
Greek

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي أَنزَلَ عَلَىـ عَبْدِهِ الْكِتَابَ وَلَمْ يَجْعَل لَّهُ عِوَجًا

English

All praise belongs to God, who has sent down the Book to His servant and has placed in it no crookedness—upright, to warn of severe punishment from Him and to give good tidings to the believers who do righteous deeds that they will have a fine reward, abiding therein forever; and to warn those who say, "God has taken a son." They have no knowledge of it, nor did their forefathers. Grave is the word that comes out of their mouths; they speak nothing but a lie.

Translator's Notes

  1. §18:1: The Prophet Muhammad encouraged reciting the first and last ten verses of Al-Kahf as protection against the tribulation of the Dajjal (Antichrist). Friday recitation of the entire surah became a widespread devotional practice across the Islamic world.
