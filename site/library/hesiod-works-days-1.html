§Lines 106–126 · Hesiod, Works and Days 106–126
Manuscript
Greek

χρύσεον μὲν πρώτιστα γένος μερόπων ἀνθρώπων

English

First the immortal gods made a golden race of mortal men. They lived like gods, free from toil and grief. The earth bore fruit for them of its own accord. Then the gods made a silver race, far worse. And after that a bronze race, and then a race of heroes, and last of all, the iron race—our own—who never rest from labor and sorrow.

Translator's Notes

  1. §106: Hesiod’s Five Ages describe a decline from gold to iron, the inverse of modern progress narratives. The insertion of the Age of Heroes (the Trojan War generation) between bronze and iron breaks the metals schema, preserving the Homeric tradition within a pessimistic cosmology.
