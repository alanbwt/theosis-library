Introduction

Ogun is the Yoruba orisha of iron, war, and labor—the divine blacksmith who cleared the path between heaven and earth. This praise hymn (oriki) invokes his power and recounts his role as the pioneer among the gods.

Translation

§Oriki Ogun · Yoruba Oral Tradition, Oriki Ogun

Ogun, lord of the road, opener of paths! He who has water at home but bathes in blood. Owner of the fiery forge, first of the gods to descend to earth. When the gods could not pass through the primordial forest, it was Ogun who cut the way with his iron machete. Ogun, the one who fights with a hundred swords. He does not wait to be called before he comes to the aid of the righteous. Father of the blacksmith, protector of the warrior, guardian of the oath.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Oriki Ogun: The oriki (praise poetry) tradition is one of the most sophisticated literary forms in African oral culture. Each orisha has an extensive corpus of oriki that function simultaneously as prayer, historical record, and theological statement. Ogun's role as path-clearer makes him the patron of all who work with iron and technology—from blacksmiths to truck drivers to surgeons in contemporary Yoruba culture.
