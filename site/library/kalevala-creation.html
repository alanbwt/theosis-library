Kalevala: The Creation from the World Egg

Kalevala: The Creation from the World Egg
Elias Lonnrot, 1802-1884 AD
Finnish Literary Society (SKS); oral sources pre-1000 AD

Introduction

The Kalevala, compiled from Finnish oral poetry by Elias Lonnrot in 1835, preserves a cosmogony of extraordinary antiquity: the world is created from an egg that shatters on the knee of the water-mother Ilmatar. The lower shell becomes earth, the upper becomes sky, the yolk the sun, the white the moon. This 'cosmic egg' motif connects Finnish mythology to Indian (Hiranyagarbha), Egyptian, and Greek (Orphic) creation traditions, suggesting a common origin in deep prehistory.

§1.1-4 · Kal. 1.1-4
Finnish

Vaka vanha Väinämöinen, tietäjä iän-ikuinen.

English

Steadfast old Vainamoinen, the eternal sage.

§1.230-240 · Kal. 1.230-240
Finnish

Munat vierähti vetehen, meren aaltohon ajaikse. Munat murtuivat muruiksi. Munan alainen puolisko maaksi alle asettui, munan yläinen puolisko taivaaksi ylös kohosi.

English

The eggs rolled into the water, into the waves of the sea. The eggs broke into pieces. The lower half of the egg became the earth below; the upper half became the sky above.
