Introduction

Hexagram 11 (Tai/Peace) represents the harmonious interaction of heaven and earth, with the creative descending and the receptive ascending. It symbolizes a time of prosperity, cooperation, and cosmic balance.

§Hexagram 11: Tai (Peace) · I Ching, Hexagram 11
Manuscript
Greek

泰。小往大來。吉亨。天地交泰。後以財成天地之道

English

Peace. The small departs, the great approaches. Good fortune. Success. Heaven and earth unite: the image of Peace. Thus the ruler divides and completes the course of heaven and earth, furthering and regulating the gifts of heaven and earth, and so aids the people.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Hexagram 11: In Tai, heaven (below) moves upward while earth (above) moves downward—they meet and mingle. This counterintuitive arrangement (heaven below earth) represents the ideal: the strong serves the yielding, power descends to support, and the result is peace. Its opposite, hexagram 12 (Standstill), shows what happens when they separate.
