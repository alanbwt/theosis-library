Introduction

This hadith defines faith (iman) through its branches, establishing that Islamic belief is not a single declaration but a complex of attitudes and actions. It appears early in Bukhari's chapter on faith.

§Hadith 9 · Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 2, Hadith 9
Manuscript
Greek

الْإِيمَانُ بِضْعٌ وَسَبْعُونَ شُعْبَةً فَأَفْضَلُهَا قَوْلُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ

English

The Prophet said: "Faith has over seventy branches, the highest of which is the declaration that there is no god but God, and the lowest of which is removing something harmful from the road. And modesty is a branch of faith."

Translator's Notes

  1. §Hadith 9: The spectrum from theological declaration to picking up litter illustrates the Islamic integration of the sacred and mundane. Al-Bayhaqi wrote an entire treatise enumerating all seventy-plus branches. The inclusion of haya' (modesty) as a branch of faith connects inner moral character to the structure of belief.
