Introduction

Jan van Ruysbroeck's The Spiritual Espousals (c. 1350) is the masterpiece of Flemish mysticism. It describes the soul's journey through three stages—the active, interior, and contemplative lives—culminating in union with God.

Translation

§Book 3: The Contemplative Life · The Spiritual Espousals, Book 3

In the highest state of the contemplative life, the soul goes out beyond all that can be understood or named. It enters into a darkness in which the loving person is transformed and becomes one with God—not by losing its own nature but by being taken up into the divine embrace. Here there is neither this nor that, neither here nor there, neither time nor eternity, but only the one eternal Now in which God enjoys Himself and all things in the abyss of His simplicity. The soul rests in God above all activity, above all understanding, in the silence of the divine dark.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Book 3: Ruysbroeck carefully distinguishes his teaching from the heresy of the Free Spirit movement, which claimed that the soul could become so united with God as to be incapable of sin. He insists that the soul retains its created nature even in the highest union—a position that maintains the ontological distinction between Creator and creature while still affirming genuine mystical transformation.
