Introduction

The Navajo (Dine) creation narrative describes the ascent of the First People through a series of colored worlds—black, blue, yellow, and white—before emerging into the present Glittering World. It is recited as part of the Blessingway ceremony.

Translation

§The Emergence · Dine Bahane' (Navajo Creation Story)

In the beginning there was only the Black World, the first world. In it lived the Insect People. They quarreled and were driven upward into the Blue World, where the Swallow People lived. Again there was conflict, and they ascended into the Yellow World, where the Grasshopper People dwelled. Once more they could not live in peace, and they climbed through a reed into this, the Glittering World. First Man and First Woman emerged and looked around. The Holy People taught them how to build the first hogan, how to plant corn, and how to live in harmony—in hozho, the state of beauty, balance, and order.

Translator's Notes

  1. §The Emergence: The concept of hozho (beauty, balance, harmony) is the central value of Navajo philosophy and the goal of all Navajo ceremonial practice. Unlike Western cosmogonies that often present a single creative act, the Navajo emergence narrative is a process of progressive ascent through moral and spiritual refinement—each world left behind because its inhabitants failed to maintain balance.
