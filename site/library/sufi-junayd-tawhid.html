Introduction

Abu al-Qasim al-Junayd of Baghdad (d. 910) is called the "Master of the Circle" and the "Peacock of the Scholars." His doctrine of tawhid established the "sober" school of Sufism that remained within orthodox bounds.

Translation

§On Tawhid · Junayd, Risala fi al-Tawhid

Tawhid is the separation of the Eternal from that which was originated in time. It is to leave behind your customary ways and your habitual practices, and to leave behind what you know and what you do not know, and to let God be as He was before created things existed.

Translator's Notes

  1. §On Tawhid: Junayd's definition carefully avoids the ecstatic claims of "union" that led to the execution of his student al-Hallaj. By defining tawhid as "separation of the Eternal from the originated," he preserves the ontological distinction between God and creation while still pointing toward mystical experience that transcends ordinary consciousness.
