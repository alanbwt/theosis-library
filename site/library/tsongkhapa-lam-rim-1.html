Introduction

Tsongkhapa’s Lam Rim Chenmo is the masterwork of Tibetan scholastic Buddhism. It organizes the entire path from beginner to buddha in systematic stages.

§Lam Rim 1.1 · Lam Rim Chenmo
Manuscript
Greek

thugs rje chen po la phyag ’tshal lo | byang chub lam gyi rim pa bshad pa

English

Homage to the great compassion. This treatise explains the stages of the path to enlightenment. The teaching of the Blessed One, vast and deep, must be approached in stages. One who attempts the highest practices without a foundation is like one who builds a palace on sand.

§Lam Rim 1.2 · Lam Rim Chenmo
Manuscript
Greek

lam gyi gtso bo rnam gsum ni | nges ’byung byang chub sems | yang dag lta ba

English

The three principal aspects of the path are: renunciation, the mind of awakening (bodhicitta), and the correct view of emptiness. Without renunciation, there is no way to achieve liberation. Without bodhicitta, there is no way to achieve full awakening.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Lam Rim 1: Tsongkhapa’s genius is organizational rather than innovative. He synthesized Indian Buddhist sources into a comprehensive curriculum. The Gelug school he founded, which eventually produced the Dalai Lamas, is built entirely on this systematic approach.
