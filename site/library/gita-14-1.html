Introduction

Krishna explains the three guṇas of material nature: sattva (goodness), rajas (passion), and tamas (darkness). All beings are bound by these three strands.

§14:1-5 · Bhagavad Gita 14
Manuscript
Greek

śrī bhagavān uvāca | paraṃ bhūyaḥ pravakṣyāmi jñānānāṃ jñānam uttamam

English

The Blessed Lord said: I shall again declare that supreme knowledge. Material nature, called prakriti, is the womb into which I place the seed; from that the birth of all beings occurs. The three gunas—sattva, rajas, and tamas—born of prakriti, bind the imperishable embodied one in the body.

Translator's Notes

  1. §14:5: The guṇa theory is India’s most influential psychology. Every mental state, every personality type, every action can be classified as predominantly sattvik, rajasik, or tamasik. The goal is not to cultivate sattva but to transcend all three guṇas entirely.
