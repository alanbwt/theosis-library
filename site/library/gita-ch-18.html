Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 18: Liberation Through Renunciation

        
Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 18: Liberation Through Renunciation

        
Attributed to Vyasa, legendary

        
Critical Edition (BORI)

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
Chapter 18 is the climax and conclusion of the Bhagavad Gita. Krishna summarizes his teaching on renunciation, the three gunas, the four castes, devotion, and surrender. Verse 66 is the most quoted verse in the Gita: 'Abandoning all dharmas, take refuge in me alone. I will deliver you from all sins. Do not grieve.' At the end, Arjuna declares his delusion is destroyed — and the great battle begins.

            §18.1-6 · BG 18.1-6
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
arjuna uvāca: saṃnyāsasya mahā-bāho tattvam icchāmi veditum | tyāgasya ca hṛṣīkeśa pṛthak keśi-niṣūdana || śrī-bhagavān uvāca: kāmyānāṃ karmaṇāṃ nyāsaṃ saṃnyāsaṃ kavayo viduḥ | sarva-karma-phala-tyāgaṃ prāhus tyāgaṃ vicakṣaṇāḥ ||

          


          

            
English

            
Arjuna said: I wish to know the truth about renunciation, O mighty-armed one, and about relinquishment, O killer of the demon Keshi, separately, O Hrishikesha. The Blessed Lord said: The wise know renunciation as the giving up of works performed with desire. The discerning declare that the relinquishment of the fruits of all works is true relinquishment. Some philosophers say that all action should be relinquished as evil; others say that acts of sacrifice, gift, and austerity should not be given up. Hear my conclusion, O best of the Bharatas. Acts of sacrifice, gift, and austerity should not be given up — these purify even the wise.

          

        

        
        


          

            §18.20-22 · BG 18.20-22
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
sarva-bhūteṣu yenaikaṃ bhāvam avyayam īkṣate | avibhaktaṃ vibhakteṣu taj jñānaṃ viddhi sāttvikam || pṛthaktvena tu yaj jñānaṃ nānā-bhāvān pṛthag-vidhān | vetti sarveṣu bhūteṣu taj jñānaṃ viddhi rājasam ||

          


          

            
English

            
That knowledge by which one sees the one indestructible Being in all beings, undivided in the divided — know that knowledge to be sattvic (pure). That knowledge which sees in all beings various entities of distinct kinds, as different from one another — know that knowledge to be rajasic (passionate). And that which clings to one single effect as if it were the whole, without reason, without foundation in truth, and trivial — that is declared to be tamasic (dark).

          

        

        
        


          

            §18.41-44 · BG 18.41-44
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
brāhmaṇa-kṣatriya-viśāṃ śūdrāṇāṃ ca paraṃtapa | karmāṇi pravibhaktāni svabhāva-prabhavair guṇaiḥ ||

          


          

            
English

            
The duties of brahmins, kshatriyas, vaishyas, and shudras, O scorcher of foes, are distinguished according to the qualities born of their own nature. Serenity, self-restraint, austerity, purity, forbearance, uprightness, knowledge, wisdom, and faith in God are the duties of brahmins. Heroism, vigor, steadiness, resourcefulness, not fleeing in battle, generosity, and lordship are the duties of kshatriyas. Plowing, cattle-tending, and trade are the duties of vaishyas. Service is the duty of shudras.

          

        

        
        


          

            §18.55-58 · BG 18.55-58
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
bhaktyā mām abhijānāti yāvān yaś cāsmi tattvataḥ | tato māṃ tattvato jñātvā viśate tad-anantaram || sarva-karmāṇy api sadā kurvāṇo mad-vyapāśrayaḥ | mat-prasādād avāpnoti śāśvataṃ padam avyayam ||

          


          

            
English

            
By devotion he comes to know me — what I am in truth, who I am. Then, knowing me in truth, he enters into me immediately. Always performing all actions, taking refuge in me, by my grace he attains the eternal, undecaying abode. Mentally renouncing all actions to me, intent on me as the highest, taking refuge in the discipline of intelligence, fix your thought constantly on me. Fixing your thought on me, you shall by my grace cross over all difficulties. But if from self-conceit you do not listen, you shall perish.

          

        

        
        


          

            §18.65-66 · BG 18.65-66
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
man-manā bhava mad-bhakto mad-yājī māṃ namaskuru | mām evaiṣyasi satyaṃ te pratijāne priyo 'si me || sarva-dharmān parityajya mām ekaṃ śaraṇaṃ vraja | ahaṃ tvāṃ sarva-pāpebhyo mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ ||

          


          

            
English

            
Fix your mind on me, be devoted to me, sacrifice to me, prostrate yourself before me. You shall come to me alone. Truly do I promise you, for you are dear to me. Abandoning all dharmas, take refuge in me alone. I will deliver you from all sins. Do not grieve.

          

        

        
        


          

            §18.73 · BG 18.73
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
arjuna uvāca: naṣṭo mohaḥ smṛtir labdhā tvat-prasādān mayācyuta | sthito 'smi gata-sandehaḥ kariṣye vacanaṃ tava ||

          


          

            
English

            
Arjuna said: My delusion is destroyed and I have gained recognition through your grace, O Achyuta. I stand firm. My doubts have vanished. I will do your word.
