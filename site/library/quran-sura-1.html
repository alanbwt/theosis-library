Quran, Sura 1: Al-Fatiha

        
Attributed to Muhammad, 570-632 AD

        
Standard Uthmanic text

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
Al-Fatiha — 'The Opening' — is the first chapter of the Quran and the most recited prayer in Islam. Every Muslim recites it at least seventeen times a day in the five required prayers. It is called the 'Mother of the Book,' the 'Cure for All Ills,' and the 'Quran of the Quran.' The seven verses summarize the entire Islamic faith: praise for God, acknowledgment of God's mercy, recognition of judgment, and the prayer for guidance.
