Introduction

The Canticle of the Sun (c. 1225) by Francis of Assisi is the earliest major literary work in the Italian language. Composed near the end of Francis's life, while he was nearly blind and in great pain, it praises God through all creatures.

Translation

§The Canticle · Canticle of the Sun (Canticum Fratris Solis)

Most High, all-powerful, good Lord, Yours are the praises, the glory, and the honor and all blessing. To You alone, Most High, do they belong, and no human being is worthy to mention Your name. Praised be You, my Lord, with all Your creatures, especially Sir Brother Sun, who is the day, and through whom You give us light. He is beautiful and radiant with great splendor, and bears the likeness of You, Most High. Praised be You, my Lord, through Sister Moon and the Stars; in heaven You formed them, clear and precious and beautiful. Praised be You, my Lord, through our Sister Bodily Death, from whom no living person can escape.

Translator's Notes

  1. §The Canticle: The fraternal language—Brother Sun, Sister Moon, Sister Death—establishes a kinship between humans and all creation that goes beyond metaphor. For Francis, the natural world is not merely a symbol pointing to God but a family of beings sharing a common origin. This radical cosmic fraternity anticipates ecological theology by eight centuries.
