Introduction

Joel 2:28–32 prophesies the outpouring of God’s Spirit on all flesh—sons and daughters, old and young, even servants. Peter quotes this passage at Pentecost in Acts 2 to interpret the Spirit’s descent.

§2:28–32 · Septuagint, Joel 2:28–32 (3:1–5 LXX)
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ἔσται μετὰ ταῦτα καὶ ἐκχεῶ ἀπὸ τοῦ πνεύματός μου ἐπὶ πᾶσαν σάρκα

English

And it shall come to pass afterward, that I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh; your sons and your daughters shall prophesy, your old men shall dream dreams, and your young men shall see visions. Even on the male and female servants in those days I will pour out my Spirit. And everyone who calls on the name of the Lord shall be saved.

Translator's Notes

  1. §2:28: The democratization of the Spirit is radical: prophecy, previously restricted to designated prophets, will be given to all regardless of age, gender, or social status. This text became foundational for Pentecostal and charismatic movements.
