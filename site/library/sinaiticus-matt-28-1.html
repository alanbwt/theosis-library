§28:1-7 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ὀψὲ δὲ σαββάτων τῇ ἐπιφωσκούσῃ

English

Now after the Sabbath, toward the dawn of the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene and the other Mary went to see the tomb. And behold, there was a great earthquake, for an angel of the Lord descended from heaven and rolled back the stone. And he said to the women, “Do not be afraid, for I know that you seek Jesus who was crucified. He is not here, for he has risen, as he said.”

§28:18-20 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ προσελθὼν ὁ Ἰησοῦς ἐλάλησεν αὐτοῖς

English

And Jesus said to them, “All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you. And behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age.”

Translator's Notes

  1. §28:19: The Trinitarian baptismal formula appears only here in the NT. Some scholars question its originality since Acts consistently baptizes “in the name of Jesus” alone. Eusebius quotes the passage without the Trinitarian formula in his pre-Nicene writings.
