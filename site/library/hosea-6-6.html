Introduction

Hosea 6:6 is a prophetic declaration that God desires steadfast love (hesed) rather than sacrifice. Jesus quotes this verse twice in Matthew’s Gospel to defend his association with sinners.

§6:1–6 · Septuagint, Hosea 6:1–6
Manuscript
Greek

διότι ἔλεος θέλω καὶ οὐ θυσίαν καὶ ἐπίγνωσιν θεοῦ ἢ ὁλοκαυτώματα

English

Come, let us return to the Lord; for he has torn us, that he may heal us; he has struck us down, and he will bind us up. After two days he will revive us; on the third day he will raise us up, that we may live before him. For I desire steadfast love and not sacrifice, the knowledge of God rather than burnt offerings.

Translator's Notes

  1. §6:2: The phrase “on the third day he will raise us up” was read by early Christians as a prophecy of the resurrection. The original context refers to national restoration, but the language provided a scriptural framework for interpreting Jesus’ resurrection “in accordance with the Scriptures.”
