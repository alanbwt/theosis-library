Introduction

Hebrews 4:12–16 contains the famous declaration that the word of God is “living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword,” followed by the invitation to approach the throne of grace with confidence. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§4:12–13 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ζων γαρ ο λογος του θεου και ενεργης και τομωτερος υπερ πασαν μαχαιραν διστομον

English

For the word of God is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing to the division of soul and of spirit, of joints and of marrow, and discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart. And no creature is hidden from his sight, but all are naked and exposed to the eyes of him to whom we must give account.

§4:14–16 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

εχοντες ουν αρχιερεα μεγαν διεληλυθοτα τους ουρανους

English

Since then we have a great high priest who has passed through the heavens, Jesus, the Son of God, let us hold fast our confession. For we do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but one who in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin. Let us then with confidence draw near to the throne of grace.

Translator's Notes

  1. §4:12: The distinction between “soul and spirit” (ψυχης και πνευματος) became important for trichotomist anthropology in the patristic period. Origen and others distinguished body, soul, and spirit as three distinct components of the human person, with the spirit as the locus of divine contact.
