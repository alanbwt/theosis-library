Introduction

The Vassal Treaties of Esarhaddon (672 BCE) imposed loyalty oaths on Median chieftains, backed by divine curses. These treaties illuminate the covenant form that scholars argue influenced the structure of biblical covenants, particularly Deuteronomy.

[Akkadian cuneiform, Nimrud, 672 BCE]

English

Treaty of Esarhaddon, king of Assyria, with the city rulers of the Medes. You shall not alter, conceal, or destroy this tablet. You shall love Ashurbanipal as you love yourselves. If you transgress this treaty, may Shamash overturn your judgment; may Sin clothe you in leprosy; may all the gods named in this tablet make the ground as narrow as a brick under your feet.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Curses: The parallels between Esarhaddon’s treaty curses and Deuteronomy 28 are striking: both invoke disease, agricultural failure, and cosmic reversal. This suggests that biblical covenant theology adopted (and adapted) the diplomatic forms of the ancient Near Eastern suzerain-vassal treaty.
