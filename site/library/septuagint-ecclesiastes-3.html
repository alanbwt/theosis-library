Ecclesiastes 3:10-11: Eternity in the Heart

Ἐκκλησιαστής
Solomon (attributed), traditional
Septuagint (LXX)

Introduction

Ecclesiastes 3:10–11 contains one of the most enigmatic claims in the Hebrew Bible: God has placed eternity (αἰών, aion; Hebrew עולם, olam) into the human heart. If something eternal, something belonging to the divine order, resides within mortal humanity, then the boundary between temporal and eternal is not absolute. The verse does not promise theosis, but it asserts the precondition: that there is already something in human nature that reaches beyond the mortal toward the infinite.

Greek

English

I have seen the task that God has given to the sons of men to be occupied with. He has made everything beautiful in its time. Also, he has placed eternity (aion, αἰών) in their hearts, yet so that man cannot find out the work that God has done from the beginning to the end.

Translator's Notes

  1. §3:11: The Septuagint renders the Hebrew עולם (olam) as αἰών (aion). Both words carry a double meaning: eternity (timelessness) and age/world (a span of time). The claim that God placed aion in the human heart can mean that humans have an intuition of the eternal, or that the entire sweep of time is somehow implanted within mortal consciousness. For the theosis tradition, the verse asserts that human nature is not purely temporal: there is something in us that reaches toward the infinite. Gregory of Nyssa built his concept of epektasis (endless progress into God) partly on this foundation.
