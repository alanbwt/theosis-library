Introduction

Hymns to Serapis (c. 3rd century BCE–3rd century CE) honor the syncretic Graeco-Egyptian deity created under the Ptolemies to unite Greek and Egyptian worship. Serapis combined aspects of Osiris, Apis, Zeus, Hades, and Asclepius into a single universal god.

§P.Oxy. XI.1380, Lines 1–15 · Oxyrhynchus Papyrus XI.1380
Manuscript
Greek

Εἷς Ζεὺς Σέραπις. μακάριος εἶ καὶ μέγας, μέγιστε θεῶν. σὴ γαίης κράτος ἄφθιτον ἔχεις

English

One Zeus Serapis. You are blessed and great, greatest of the gods. Yours is the imperishable sovereignty of the cosmos. The earth, the sea, the boundless sky are your domain. When the winds blow, every living thing trembles before your power.

Translator's Notes

  1. §P.Oxy. XI.1380: The acclamation “One Zeus Serapis” (Εἷς Ζεὺς Σέραπις) is a henotheistic formula that proclaims Serapis as supreme without denying other gods. This “one god” language is strikingly close to Jewish and Christian monotheistic formulas and represents a late pagan convergence toward monotheism.
