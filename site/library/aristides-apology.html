Introduction

The Apology of Aristides (c. 125 CE) is one of the earliest surviving Christian apologetic texts, addressed to Emperor Hadrian. Aristides classifies humanity into four races—Barbarians, Greeks, Jews, and Christians—and argues that only Christians have found the truth about God.

§Chapter 15 · Aristides, Apology 15
Manuscript
Greek

Original preserved in Syriac and Armenian; Greek fragments in the Life of Barlaam and Josaphat.

English

The Christians trace the beginning of their religion from Jesus the Messiah. He is named the Son of God Most High. It is said that God came down from heaven and took flesh from a Hebrew virgin, and the Son of God lived in a daughter of man.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 15: Aristides’s Apology was lost for centuries until rediscovered in a Syriac translation in 1889. A Greek version was later found embedded in the Life of Barlaam and Josaphat. The four-race classification scheme is unique among early apologists and reflects a genuinely universal perspective.
