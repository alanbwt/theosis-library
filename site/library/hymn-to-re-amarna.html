Hymn To Re Amarna

Unknown,

Introduction

The Great Hymn to the Aten (c. 1345 BCE) from the tomb of Ay at Amarna is attributed to Pharaoh Akhenaten. It celebrates the sun-disc as the sole creator god and is remarkable for its parallels with Psalm 104.

English

How manifold are your works! They are hidden from the face of man. O sole god, like whom there is no other! You created the earth according to your desire, being alone: all peoples, herds, and flocks. All that is on earth, walking on legs, all that is above, flying with wings.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Central section: Compare Psalm 104:24: “O Lord, how manifold are your works! In wisdom you have made them all.” Whether the parallel represents direct literary borrowing, a shared cultural milieu, or independent expression of similar theology remains one of the most debated questions in biblical studies.
