Introduction

The Isis Aretalogy from Kyme (c. 1st–2nd century CE) is a first-person hymn in which the goddess declares her own powers and cosmic authority. It reveals Isis as a universal deity who transcends Egyptian boundaries.

§Lines 1–15 · Kyme Aretalogy, opening
Manuscript
Greek

Ἐγώ εἰμι Ἴσις ἡ τύραννος πάσης χώρας· ἐπαιδεύθην ὑπὸ Ἑρμοῦ, καὶ γράμματα εὗρον μετὰ Ἑρμοῦ τά τε ἱερὰ καὶ τὰ δημόσια

English

I am Isis, the mistress of every land. I was taught by Hermes, and with Hermes I devised letters, both sacred and common, that all things might not be written with the same. I gave laws to humankind, and ordained what no one can change.

§Lines 40–50 · Kyme Aretalogy, cosmic claims
Manuscript
Greek

Ἐγὼ γῆν ἀπ’ οὐρανοῦ ἐχώρισα· ἐγὼ ἄστρων ὁδοὺς ἔδειξα· ἐγὼ ἡλίου καὶ σελήνης πορείαν ἐταξάμην

English

I separated the earth from the heaven. I showed the paths of the stars. I ordered the course of the sun and the moon. I devised business in the sea. I made strong the right. I brought together woman and man.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Lines 1–15: The Isis aretalogies use the “I am” (Ἐγώ εἰμι) formula that later appears in John’s Gospel. Whether the Johannine “I am” sayings draw on the aretalogy tradition or share a common ancient Near Eastern root remains debated.
