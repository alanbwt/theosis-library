Introduction

§11:9-13 · Bhagavad Gita 11
Manuscript
Greek

evam uktvā tato rājan mahā-yogeśvaro hariḥ | darśayām āsa pārthāya paramaṃ rūpam aiśvaram

English

Sanjaya said: Having spoken thus, the great Lord of Yoga showed Arjuna his supreme divine form—with many mouths and eyes, many wondrous sights, many divine ornaments, many divine weapons uplifted; wearing divine garlands and garments, all-wonderful, resplendent, infinite, facing every direction.

§11:32 · Bhagavad Gita 11
Manuscript
Greek

kālo ’smi loka-kṣaya-kṛt pravṛddho lokān samāhartum iha pravṛttaḥ

English

The Supreme Lord said: I am Time, the great destroyer of worlds, and I have come here to engage all people. Even without your participation, all the warriors standing arrayed in the opposing armies shall cease to exist.

Translator's Notes

  1. §11:32: Oppenheimer famously quoted this verse upon witnessing the first nuclear detonation. The original context is not destruction for its own sake but the revelation that time itself is the divine agent of cosmic dissolution.
