Introduction

Colossians 3:1–17 exhorts believers to “seek the things that are above” and to put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge after the image of its creator. The passage grounds ethics in ontological transformation. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§3:1–4 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ει ουν συνηγερθητε τω χριστω τα ανω ζητειτε

English

If then you have been raised with Christ, seek the things that are above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God. Set your minds on things that are above, not on things that are on earth. For you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God. When Christ who is your life appears, then you also will appear with him in glory.

§3:9–11 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

μη ψευδεσθε εις αλληλους απεκδυσαμενοι τον παλαιον ανθρωπον

English

Do not lie to one another, seeing that you have put off the old self with its practices and have put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge after the image of its creator. Here there is not Greek and Jew, circumcised and uncircumcised, barbarian, Scythian, slave, free; but Christ is all, and in all.

Translator's Notes

  1. §3:10: The new self is being “renewed in knowledge after the image of its creator” (ανακαινουμενον εις επιγνωσιν κατ εικονα του κτισαντος). This echoes Genesis 1:26–27: humanity is being restored to the divine image that was distorted by sin.
