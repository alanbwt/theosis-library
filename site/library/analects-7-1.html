Introduction

Analects 7:1 captures Confucius's self-understanding as a transmitter of ancient wisdom rather than an innovator. This stance of humble transmission became the model for the Confucian scholar's relationship to tradition.

§7.1 · Analects, Book 7, Chapter 1
Manuscript
Greek

子曰：述而不作，信而好古，窃比于我老彭

English

The Master said: "I transmit but do not innovate. I am truthful in what I say and devoted to antiquity. I venture to compare myself to our Old Peng."

Translator's Notes

  1. §7.1: "Old Peng" is traditionally identified as a minister of the Shang dynasty known for his love of ancient lore. Confucius's claim to be merely a transmitter is both genuine and strategically modest: by presenting his radical ethical vision as the recovery of ancient wisdom, he gave it the authority of antiquity while shielding it from the charge of dangerous novelty.
