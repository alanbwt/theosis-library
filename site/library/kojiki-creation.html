Kojiki: The Creation of Japan
O no Yasumaro, d. 723 AD
Shinpukuji-bon manuscript

Introduction

The Kojiki (Record of Ancient Matters), compiled in 712 AD, is the oldest surviving Japanese text and the foundation of Shinto theology. It recounts the creation of the Japanese islands by the divine couple Izanagi and Izanami, Izanagi's descent to the land of the dead, and the birth of Amaterasu, the sun goddess from whom the imperial line claims descent.

Japanese original and English translation.
§1.1 · Kojiki §1
Japanese

天地初發之時、於高天原成神名、天之御中主神。

English

When heaven and earth first appeared, there came into being in the Plain of High Heaven a deity named the Lord of the August Center of Heaven.

§1.4 · Kojiki §4
Japanese

伊邪那岐命、伊邪那美命。是二柱神、立天浮橋而、指下其沼矛以畫者。

English

Izanagi and Izanami, these two deities, standing upon the Floating Bridge of Heaven, thrust down the jeweled spear and stirred.
