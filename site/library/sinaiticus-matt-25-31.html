Introduction

The parable of the sheep and goats is the climactic judgment scene in Matthew. The criterion is not belief or ritual but action: “As you did it to one of the least of these, you did it to me.”

§25:31-40 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ὅταν δὲ ἔλθῃ ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου ἐν τῇ δόξῃ αὐτοῦ

English

When the Son of Man comes in his glory, and all the angels with him, then he will sit on his glorious throne. Before him will be gathered all the nations. Then the King will say to those on his right, “Come, you who are blessed by my Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world. For I was hungry and you gave me food, I was thirsty and you gave me drink, I was a stranger and you welcomed me.”

§25:41-46 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

τότε ἐρεῖ καὶ τοῖς ἐξ εὐωνύμων

English

Then he will say to those on his left, “Depart from me, you cursed, into the eternal fire prepared for the devil and his angels. For I was hungry and you gave me no food.” And they will go away into eternal punishment, but the righteous into eternal life.

Translator's Notes

  1. §25:40: The identification of Christ with “the least of these” is theologically radical. It locates the divine not in temples or heavens but in the hungry, the naked, and the imprisoned. Liberation theology builds its entire program on this verse.
