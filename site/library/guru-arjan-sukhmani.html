Introduction

The Sukhmani Sahib ("Psalm of Peace") by Guru Arjan Dev is the longest single composition in the Guru Granth Sahib. Its 24 sections (ashtpadis) systematically explore meditation on the divine Name as the source of all peace and liberation.

Translation

§Ashtpadi 1 · Guru Granth Sahib, Sukhmani Sahib, Page 262

Remember, remember God; by remembering Him, you shall find peace. Let the worry and anguish of your mind be dispelled. Remember the Name of the one Lord, who pervades all places and interspaces. His praises are sung by thousands, but His limits are never found. The Creator Lord is immanent in all creation; He is everywhere, fully pervading all. The Name is the support of all His devotees. The Name is the honor of the dishonored.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Ashtpadi 1: The practice of naam simran (remembrance of the divine Name) is the central devotional practice of Sikhism. The Sukhmani does not treat the Name as a mere word but as the creative vibration (shabad) underlying all reality. This concept parallels the Logos theology of John's Gospel and the Hindu understanding of Om as the primordial sound.
