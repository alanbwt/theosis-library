Bardo Thodol: Liberation Through Hearing in the Intermediate State

Bardo Thodol: Liberation Through Hearing in the Intermediate State
Attributed to Padmasambhava, 8th century AD
Tibetan terma tradition

Introduction

Known in the West as the Tibetan Book of the Dead, the Bardo Thodol is a guide for the consciousness of the recently deceased as it navigates the intermediate states (bardos) between death and rebirth. Attributed to the 8th-century master Padmasambhava and hidden as a terma (treasure text), it was rediscovered centuries later. The text describes encounters with peaceful and wrathful deities that are, in truth, projections of the mind itself.

§1.1 · Bardo §1
Tibetan

ཀྱེ་མ། ད་ཆོས་ཉིད་བར་དོ་ང་ལ་ཤར། སྣང་བ་ཐམས་ཅད་ཆོས་སྐུ་ཡིན་པར་ཤེས།

English

Now when the bardo of reality dawns upon me, I shall recognize all appearances as the dharmakaya.

§1.2 · Bardo §2
Tibetan

འོད་གསལ་རང་བྱུང་ཆེན་པོ་འདི། མ་རིག་པའི་སྒྲིབ་པས་མ་བསྒྲིབས།

English

This great self-arising luminosity has never been obscured by the darkness of ignorance.
