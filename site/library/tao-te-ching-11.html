Introduction

Chapter 11 presents the paradox of usefulness through emptiness. The value of a vessel, a room, or a wheel lies not in its material substance but in the void it contains.

§Chapter 11 · Tao Te Ching, Chapter 11
Manuscript
Greek

三十辐共一毂 当其无 有车之用

English

Thirty spokes share the wheel's hub; it is the center hole that makes it useful. Shape clay into a vessel; it is the space within that makes it useful. Cut doors and windows for a room; it is the holes that make it useful. Therefore benefit comes from what is there; usefulness from what is not there.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 11: This chapter articulates the Taoist metaphysics of wu (non-being, emptiness) as prior to and more fundamental than you (being, substance). The examples are deliberately mundane—wheels, pots, rooms—to show that this principle operates at every level of daily experience, not just in abstract cosmology.
