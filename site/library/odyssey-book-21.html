            §21.1 · Od. 21
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Greek

            
Τῇ δ᾿ ἄρ᾿ ἐπὶ φρεσὶ θῆκε θεὰ γλαυκῶπις Ἀθήνη.

          


          

            
English

            
Athena put it in Penelope's mind to set the contest of the bow. Whoever could string Odysseus' great bow and shoot an arrow through twelve axe heads would win her hand. One by one the suitors tried and failed. Odysseus asked to try. Despite their protests, he took the bow, strung it easily, and shot the arrow through all twelve axes.
