Sinaiticus Isaiah 42

Introduction

Isaiah 42 introduces the first Servant Song. The servant brings justice to the nations not through violence but through quiet faithfulness—a bruised reed he will not break.

§42:1-4 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ἰδοὺ ὁ παῖς μου ἀντιλήμψομαι αὐτοῦ

English

Behold my servant, whom I uphold, my chosen, in whom my soul delights; I have put my Spirit upon him; he will bring forth justice to the nations. He will not cry aloud or lift up his voice in the street; a bruised reed he will not break, and a faintly burning wick he will not quench.

§42:5-9 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

οὕτως λέγει κύριος ὁ θεός

English

Thus says God, the Lord, who created the heavens and stretched them out: “I am the Lord; I have called you in righteousness; I will take you by the hand and keep you; I will give you as a covenant for the people, a light for the nations.”

Translator's Notes

  1. §42:1: Matthew 12:18-21 quotes this passage to explain Jesus’ command to silence after healings. The servant’s quiet manner becomes a christological template: messiahship expressed not through triumphalism but through restraint.
