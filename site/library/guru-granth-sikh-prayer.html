Introduction

The Ardas is the formal Sikh prayer recited at the conclusion of every worship service and before any significant undertaking. It invokes the ten Gurus, commemorates Sikh martyrs, and petitions for universal welfare.

Translation

§Ardas: Opening · Sikh Ardas, Traditional Text

Having first remembered God, turn your thoughts to Guru Nanak. Then to Guru Angad, Guru Amar Das, and Guru Ram Das—may they grant us help. Remember Guru Arjan, Guru Hargobind, and Guru Har Rai. Remember Guru Har Krishan, by whose sight all suffering vanishes. Remember Guru Tegh Bahadur, and the nine treasures will come hastening to your home. May the tenth king, Guru Gobind Singh, grant us help everywhere. The light of the ten kings shines in the Guru Granth Sahib. Turn your thoughts to its teachings and call out: Waheguru!

Translator's Notes

  1. §Opening: The Ardas is the only major Sikh prayer not found in the Guru Granth Sahib; it is a living, evolving text that has been modified over the centuries to include commemorations of new martyrs and historical events. The exclamation Waheguru ("Wonderful Lord") functions as both a divine name and a mantra in Sikh devotional practice.
