Cambridge MS; Bodleian MS

Introduction

Omar Khayyam was a mathematician, astronomer, and philosopher whose short quatrains (rubaiyat) express a radical skepticism about the afterlife and a fierce insistence on living fully in the present. His hedonism is philosophical, not trivial — the wine he celebrates is both literal and a metaphor for intoxication with the mystery of existence. Edward FitzGerald's 1859 English translation made the Rubaiyat one of the most quoted poems in the English language.

§1 · Rub. §1
Persian

اوقات خوش آن بود که با دوست به سر شد / باقی همه بی‌حاصلی و بی‌خبری بود

English

The happy times were those spent with a friend — the rest was all fruitlessness and ignorance.

§7 · Rub. §7
Persian

این قافلهٔ عمر عجب می‌گذرد / دریاب دمی که با طرب می‌گذرد

English

This caravan of life passes wondrous quickly — seize the moment that passes with joy.
