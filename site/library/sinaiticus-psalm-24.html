Introduction

Psalm 24 is a liturgical entrance hymn. The question “who shall ascend the hill of the Lord?” frames moral purity as the condition for approaching divine presence.

§24:1-4 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

τοῦ κυρίου ἡ γῆ καὶ τὸ πλήρωμα αὐτῆς

English

The earth is the Lord’s and the fullness thereof, the world and those who dwell therein. For he has founded it upon the seas. Who shall ascend the hill of the Lord? And who shall stand in his holy place? He who has clean hands and a pure heart.

§24:7-10 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ἄρατε πύλας οἱ ἄρχοντες ὑμῶν

English

Lift up your heads, O gates! And be lifted up, O ancient doors, that the King of glory may come in. Who is this King of glory? The Lord, strong and mighty, the Lord, mighty in battle. The Lord of hosts, he is the King of glory.

Translator's Notes

  1. §24:1: Paul quotes this verse in 1 Corinthians 10:26 to settle the question of whether Christians may eat meat offered to idols. If the earth and its fullness belong to the Lord, then no food is inherently impure.
