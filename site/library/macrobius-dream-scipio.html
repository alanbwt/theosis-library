Introduction

Macrobius’s Commentary on the Dream of Scipio (c. 400 CE) takes Cicero’s brief vision of cosmic order and expands it into a comprehensive Neoplatonic cosmology. The soul descends through the planetary spheres, acquiring qualities at each level, and must reascend to its celestial origin.

§I.12.1–8 · Macrobius, Commentarii in Somnium Scipionis I.12.1–8 (Willis, Teubner)
Manuscript
Greek

In Saturni ratiocinationem et intellegentiam, in Iovis vim agendi, in Martis animositatis ardorem, in Solis sentiendi opinandique naturam.

English

The soul, descending from the junction of the zodiac and the Milky Way into the successive spheres below, is clothed in each sphere with an ethereal envelope. In Saturn it receives reasoning and understanding; in Jupiter, the power to act; in Mars, spirited energy; in the Sun, the capacity for sensation and imagination; in Venus, desire; in Mercury, the ability to speak and interpret; in the Moon, the capacity for generation. Entering the body, it forgets its divine origin.

Translator's Notes

  1. §I.12.1–8: The doctrine of the soul’s acquisition of qualities during planetary descent became standard in late antique cosmology. It blends Platonic psychology with astrological theory and provided the conceptual framework for medieval and Renaissance accounts of the soul’s cosmic journey.
