Introduction

This hadith from Bukhari's Book of Knowledge establishes the religious obligation to seek and transmit learning. It became a foundational text for the Islamic scholarly tradition and its vast networks of knowledge transmission.

§Hadith 67 · Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 3, Hadith 67
Manuscript
Greek

بَلِّغُوا عَنِّي وَلَوْ آيَةً وَحَدِّثُوا عَنْ بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ وَلَا حَرَجَ

English

The Prophet said: "Convey from me, even if it be a single verse. And narrate from the Children of Israel, and there is no sin in that. And whoever deliberately lies about me, let him take his seat in the Fire."

Translator's Notes

  1. §Hadith 67: The permission to narrate from the Israelite tradition (Isra'iliyyat) opened the door for Jewish and Christian narratives to enter Islamic exegesis, while the severe warning against fabrication established the rigorous authentication methodology (isnad criticism) that became the hallmark of hadith science.
