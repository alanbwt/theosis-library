Histories III.80-82: The Debate on Government

Histories III.80-82: The Debate on Government
Herodotus, c. 484-425 BCE
Codex Laurentianus 70.3

Introduction

Herodotus records a debate among three Persian conspirators — Otanes, Megabyzus, and Darius — about the best form of government. This is the earliest known systematic comparison of democracy, oligarchy, and monarchy in Western literature, predating Plato's Republic and Aristotle's Politics by a century. Each speaker identifies the fatal flaw in the others' preferred system. The passage shaped all subsequent Western political thought.

§3.80 · Hdt. 3.80
Greek

Ὀτάνης μὲν ἐκέλευε ἐς μέσον Πέρσῃσι καταθεῖναι τὰ πρήγματα... πλῆθος δὲ ἄρχον πρῶτα μὲν οὔνομα πάντων κάλλιστον ἔχει, ἰσονομίην.

English

Otanes urged them to put the government in the hands of the whole Persian people... Rule by the many has first the fairest name of all: equality before the law.

§3.82 · Hdt. 3.82
Greek

ἑνὸς τοῦ ἀρίστου ἀνδρὸς οὐδὲν ἄμεινον ἂν φανείη· γνώμῃ γὰρ τοιαύτῃ χρεώμενος ἐπιτροπεύοι ἂν ἀμωμήτως τοῦ πλήθεος.

English

Nothing better can appear than the rule of the one best man. With such judgment he would govern the people blamelessly.
