Introduction

Utterance 213 of the Pyramid Texts (c. 2350 BCE) is inscribed in the pyramid of Unas at Saqqara. It is a resurrection spell declaring that the dead king rises as a star and joins the gods, asserting the royal dead’s identity with Osiris.

Greek

j n(j) km.t(j) m(w)t.t(j), km.t(j) ḥnq.t(j). ḥms ḥr ns.t Wsjr, ḥḏā.t=k m ḏr.t=k, wḏ-md.w n ānḫ.w

English

O King, you have not departed dead; you have departed living. Sit upon the throne of Osiris, your scepter in your hand, that you may give commands to the living. Your lotus-scepter is in your hand; give commands to those of the secret places.

Translator's Notes

  1. §PT 213: The assertion “you have not departed dead; you have departed living” is the earliest surviving statement of what would later become the Egyptian doctrine of justified immortality. The king does not merely survive death—he transcends it, becoming one with Osiris.
