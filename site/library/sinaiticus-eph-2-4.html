Ephesians 2:4-10: Seated in the Heavenly Places

Paul (attributed), c. 50-65 AD

Introduction

Ephesians 2:4–10 describes the most audacious spatial claim in Pauline theology: that believers have already been raised with Christ and seated with him “in the heavenly places.” The passage moves from death to life in three verses. Humanity was dead in trespasses; God, rich in mercy, made us alive together with Christ (synezōopoiēsen, συνεζωοποίησεν), raised us together (synēgeiren, συνήγειρεν), and seated us together (synekathisen, συνεκάθισεν) in the heavenlies. The three syn- compounds hammer home the co-participation: everything that happened to Christ happens to those who are in him.

The passage concludes with one of Paul’s most carefully balanced theological statements: salvation is by grace through faith, not from works, yet believers are God’s “workmanship” (poiēma, ποίημα)—his poem, his artifact—created in Christ Jesus for good works. The human being is simultaneously the recipient of grace and the product of divine craftsmanship, a new creation shaped by the hands of God.

Greek

ὁ δὲ θεὸς πλούσιος ὢν ἐν ἐλέει · διὰ τὴν πολλὴν ἀγάπην αὐτοῦ ἣν ἠγάπησεν ἡμᾶς · καὶ ὄντας ἡμᾶς νεκροὺς τοῖς παραπτώμασιν · συνεζωοποίησεν τῷ Χριστῷ · χάριτί ἐστε σεσωσμένοι · καὶ συνήγειρεν καὶ συνεκάθισεν ἐν τοῖς ἐπουρανίοις ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ · ἵνα ἐνδείξηται ἐν τοῖς αἰῶσιν τοῖς ἐπερχομένοις τὸ ὑπερβάλλον πλοῦτος τῆς χάριτος αὐτοῦ ἐν χρηστότητι ἐφʼ ἡμᾶς ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ ·

English

But God, being rich in mercy, because of the great love with which he loved us, even when we were dead in our trespasses, made us alive together with Christ—by grace you have been saved—and raised us up together and seated us together in the heavenly places in Christ Jesus, so that in the coming ages he might show the surpassing riches of his grace in kindness toward us in Christ Jesus.

§2:8–10 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

τῇ γὰρ χάριτί ἐστε σεσωσμένοι διὰ πίστεως · καὶ τοῦτο οὐκ ἐξ ὑμῶν · θεοῦ τὸ δῶρον · οὐκ ἐξ ἔργων · ἵνα μή τις καυχήσηται · αὐτοῦ γάρ ἐσμεν ποίημα · κτισθέντες ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ ἐπὶ ἔργοις ἀγαθοῖς · οἷς προητοίμασεν ὁ θεὸς ἵνα ἐν αὐτοῖς περιπατήσωμεν ·

English

For by grace you have been saved through faith, and this is not from yourselves—it is the gift of God—not from works, so that no one may boast. For we are his workmanship (poiēma, ποίημα), created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand so that we would walk in them.

Translator's Notes

  1. §2:5–6: The three syn- compound verbs—συνεζωοποίησεν (“made alive together with”), συνήγειρεν (“raised together with”), συνεκάθισεν (“seated together with”)—are all in the aorist tense, presenting these cosmic events as already accomplished. The believer is not waiting to be raised and exalted; in Christ, it has already happened. This “realized eschatology” is characteristic of Ephesians and provides the temporal framework for the Eastern doctrine of theosis as present reality rather than future hope.
  2. §2:10: The word poiēma (ποίημα) is the root of the English word “poem.” It means something made, a work of art, a crafted product. The human being saved by grace is not merely forgiven but remade—a new artifact of divine craftsmanship. The word appears only here and in Romans 1:20 (where it refers to God’s creation of the world), drawing an implicit parallel between the original creation and the new creation in Christ.
