Introduction

Matthew 24:36–44 addresses the unknown timing of the Son of Man’s return. Jesus’s admission that even the Son does not know the hour is one of the most theologically challenging statements in the Gospels. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§24:36 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

περι δε της ημερας εκεινης και ωρας ουδεις οιδεν ουδε οι αγγελοι των ουρανων ουδε ο υιος

English

But concerning that day and hour no one knows, not even the angels of heaven, nor the Son, but the Father only.

§24:42–44 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

γρηγορειτε ουν οτι ουκ οιδατε ποια ημερα ο κυριος υμων ερχεται

English

Therefore, stay awake, for you do not know on what day your Lord is coming. Therefore you also must be ready, for the Son of Man is coming at an hour you do not expect.

Translator's Notes

  1. §24:36: The phrase “nor the Son” is present in Sinaiticus but was omitted in some later manuscripts, likely because it seemed to limit Christ’s omniscience. The Arian controversy made this verse a battleground.
