Introduction

Libanius’s Pro Templis (Oration 30, 386 CE) is a passionate defense of pagan temples addressed to the Christian emperor Theodosius. The Antiochene rhetorician argues that the temples are the soul of the countryside and their destruction brings ruin to communities.

§Oration 30.8–9 · Libanius, Oration 30.8–9
Manuscript
Greek

τὰ ἱερὰ ψυχὴ τῆς χώρας· ταῦτα πρῶτα τῶν ἐν τοῖς ἀγροῖς οἰκήσεων ἐγένετο

English

The temples are the soul of the countryside. They were the first buildings in the settlements, and through many generations people have come to them. In them the farming folk have placed their hopes for their families, their crops, their cattle. A countryside from which temples have been torn has lost its soul.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Oration 30.8–9: Libanius strategically avoids theological argument and instead frames temple destruction as an economic and social catastrophe. His appeal to Theodosius is pragmatic: even if Christianity is true, destroying temples harms the empire. This rhetorical strategy reflects the weakened position of paganism by the 380s.
