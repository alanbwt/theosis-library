Introduction

John 5:19–47 contains Jesus’ discourse on his relationship with the Father, asserting that the Son does nothing independently but only what he sees the Father doing. It is a foundational text for Trinitarian theology.

§5:19–21 · Codex Sinaiticus, John 5:19–21
Manuscript
Greek

ἀπεκρίνατο οὖν ὁ Ἰησοῦς καὶ ἔλεγεν αὐτοῖς · ἀμὴν ἀμὴν λέγω ὑμῖν οὐ δύναται ὁ υἱὸς ποιεῖν ἀφ’ ἑαυτοῦ οὐδέν

English

Jesus said to them, ‘Truly, truly, I say to you, the Son can do nothing of his own accord, but only what he sees the Father doing. For whatever the Father does, that the Son does likewise. For the Father loves the Son and shows him all that he himself is doing.’

Translator's Notes

  1. §5:19: This verse was pivotal in the Arian controversy. Arius cited the Son’s inability to act independently as evidence of subordination, while the orthodox read it as describing perfect unity of will between Father and Son.
