Iliad, Book I: The Wrath of Achilles

Iliad, Book I: The Wrath of Achilles
Homer, c. 8th century BCE
Venetus A (Marcianus Graecus 454)

Introduction

The Iliad opens with the word mēnin — wrath — and the whole of Western literature follows from it. Homer's epic of the Trojan War is not primarily about war but about the consequences of unchecked anger, the tragedy of mortality, and the complex relationship between gods and humans. Book I establishes the central conflict: Achilles withdrawing from battle after Agamemnon seizes his war-prize, setting in motion the chain of suffering that fulfills the will of Zeus.

§1.1-7 · Il. 1.1-7
Greek

Μῆνιν ἄειδε, θεά, Πηληϊάδεω Ἀχιλῆος οὐλομένην, ἣ μυρί᾿ Ἀχαιοῖς ἄλγε᾿ ἔθηκε, πολλὰς δ᾿ ἰφθίμους ψυχὰς Ἄϊδι προΐαψεν ἡρώων, αὐτοὺς δὲ ἑλώρια τεῦχε κύνεσσιν οἰωνοῖσί τε πᾶσι — Διὸς δ᾿ ἐτελείετο βουλή — ἐξ οὗ δὴ τὰ πρῶτα διαστήτην ἐρίσαντε Ἀτρεΐδης τε ἄναξ ἀνδρῶν καὶ δῖος Ἀχιλλεύς.

English

Sing, goddess, the wrath of Achilles, son of Peleus — the accursed wrath that brought countless sorrows upon the Achaeans and hurled many mighty souls of heroes down to Hades, making them prey for dogs and all the birds — and the will of Zeus was being accomplished — from the moment when first they quarreled and parted, Atreus' son, lord of men, and godlike Achilles.

§1.8-16 · Il. 1.8-16
Greek

τίς τ᾿ ἄρ σφωε θεῶν ἔριδι ξυνέηκε μάχεσθαι; Λητοῦς καὶ Διὸς υἱός. ὁ γὰρ βασιλῆϊ χολωθεὶς νοῦσον ἀνὰ στρατὸν ὦρσε κακήν, ὀλέκοντο δὲ λαοί.

English

Which of the gods set them to quarrel and fight? The son of Leto and Zeus. For angered at the king, he sent a plague upon the army — an evil sickness — and the people were dying.

§1.188-194 · Il. 1.188-194
Greek

ἕλκετο δ᾿ ἐκ κολεοῖο μέγα ξίφος· ἦλθε δ᾿ Ἀθήνη οὐρανόθεν... στῆ δ᾿ ὄπιθεν, ξανθῆς δὲ κόμης ἕλε Πηλεΐωνα.

English

He was drawing his great sword from its sheath when Athena came from heaven... She stood behind him and seized the son of Peleus by his golden hair.
