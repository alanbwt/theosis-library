Introduction

Chapter 76 uses the contrast between the soft and the hard, the living and the dead, to argue that flexibility is the way of life and rigidity the way of death.

§Chapter 76 · Tao Te Ching, Chapter 76
Manuscript
Greek

人之生也柔弱 其死也坚强

English

A man is born gentle and weak. At his death he is hard and stiff. Green plants are tender and filled with sap. At their death they are withered and dry. Therefore the stiff and unbending is the disciple of death. The soft and yielding is the disciple of life. The hard and strong will fall. The soft and weak will overcome.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 76: This chapter's principle was adopted as a strategic maxim in Chinese military and political thought. The association of softness with life and hardness with death reverses the common assumption that strength is superior. It influenced martial arts philosophy, particularly the internal arts like taijiquan.
