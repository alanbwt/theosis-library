Introduction

Utterance 219 describes the dead king’s ascent to the sky on a ladder. The sky-goddess Nut is invoked to receive him, and the king is declared to be a god among gods.

§PT 219 (§167–170) · Pyramid of Unas, Antechamber
Manuscript
Greek

Nw.t pss.t ḥr sā=t Wnis pn. sāw sw r ḫ.t nb.t ḏw.t. Nw.t āḫ.n=t, wsḫ.n=t, nḫt.n=t

English

O Nut, spread yourself over your son, this King Unas. Protect him from all things evil. O Nut, you became a spirit, you became mighty, you became powerful. O King, you are the greatest of the gods.

Translator's Notes

  1. §PT 219: The invocation of Nut to “spread herself” over the dead king is both cosmic and maternal. The sky-goddess literally envelops the king as the sky covers the earth. This image persists in Egyptian coffin decoration for over two millennia, with Nut painted on the interior lids of sarcophagi.
