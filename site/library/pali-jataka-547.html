Jataka Tales: The Monkey King (Mahakapi Jataka)

        
Jataka Tales: The Monkey King (Mahakapi Jataka)

        
Gautama Buddha, c. 563-483 BCE

        
Pali Canon, Khuddaka Nikaya

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
The Buddha in a previous life as a monkey king stretches his body as a bridge so his troop can escape across a river. A parable of self-sacrifice carved at every major Buddhist temple in Southeast Asia.

Translation

        
        

          §1 · pali-jataka-547
          
The monkey king saw his people trapped between the hunters and the river. He stretched his body from one bank to the other and said: 'Walk across me.' The monkeys crossed to safety. But the king's back was broken. The king of the humans wept and said: 'You have given your life for your people. You are a greater king than I.'
