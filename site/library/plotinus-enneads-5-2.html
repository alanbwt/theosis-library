Introduction

Ennead 5.2, “The Origin and Order of the Beings Following the First,” describes how all reality emanates from the One in a cascading series: the One produces Intellect, Intellect produces Soul, Soul produces the material cosmos. This is the foundational text of emanation metaphysics.

§5.2.1 · Plotinus, Enneads
Manuscript
Greek

τὸ ἓν πάντα καὶ οὐδὲ ἓν

English

The One is all things and not a single one of them. It is the source of all things, not itself all things, but their transcendent origin. They are all, as it were, flowing back to it, or rather, they have not yet flowed away; they are still there. Everything that exists aspires to the Good and reaches toward it, for all things owe their being to it.

Translator's Notes

  1. §5.2.1: The paradox that the One is “all things and not a single one of them” defines apophatic theology: the ultimate principle transcends all categories, including the categories of being and non-being. This Plotinian formulation was adopted by Pseudo-Dionysius and through him entered the mainstream of Christian mystical theology.
