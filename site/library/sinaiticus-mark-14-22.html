Introduction

The Last Supper in Mark is the institution of the Eucharist. Jesus takes bread and wine and identifies them with his body and blood—“the blood of the covenant, poured out for many.”

§14:22-25 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ἐσθιόντων αὐτῶν λαβὼν ἄρτον

English

And as they were eating, he took bread, and after blessing it broke it and gave it to them, and said, “Take; this is my body.” And he took a cup, and when he had given thanks he gave it to them, and they all drank of it. And he said to them, “This is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many.”

§14:32-36 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ἔρχονται εἰς χωρίον οὗ τὸ ὄνομα Γεθσημανεί

English

And they went to a place called Gethsemane. And he said to his disciples, “Sit here while I pray.” And he began to be greatly distressed. And he said, “Abba, Father, all things are possible for you. Remove this cup from me. Yet not what I will, but what you will.”

Translator's Notes

  1. §14:24: Sinaiticus reads “blood of the covenant” without the word “new,” which later manuscripts add under the influence of Luke 22:20 and 1 Corinthians 11:25. The shorter reading connects directly to Exodus 24:8, where Moses sprinkles “the blood of the covenant” on the people.
