The Baal Cycle: Baal Against Death

The Baal Cycle: Baal Against Death
Ugaritic Scribes, c. 1400-1200 BCE
KTU 1.5-1.6; Ras Shamra tablets

Introduction

In the second part of the Baal Cycle, the storm god descends to the underworld and is swallowed by Mot (Death). His sister Anat searches for him, defeats Death, and Baal is restored to life. This pattern of divine death and resurrection — predating the Christian narrative by over a millennium — reflects the agricultural cycle of the ancient Near East and belongs to a widespread mythological type that includes Osiris, Tammuz, and Persephone.
