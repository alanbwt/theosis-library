Mencius 1A.7: The Ox Mountain Parable

Mencius 1A.7: The Ox Mountain Parable
Mencius, c. 372-289 BCE
Shisan Jing Zhushu

Introduction

A mountain stripped bare still has shoots of goodness — like human nature, degraded by circumstance but never destroyed.

Translation

§1 · mencius-1a7 §1

The trees of Ox Mountain were once beautiful. But axes came morning after morning. Can the mountain remain beautiful?
