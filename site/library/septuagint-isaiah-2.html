Introduction

Isaiah 2:1–4 presents the eschatological vision of all nations streaming to the mountain of the Lord, where God will judge between peoples and they shall “beat their swords into plowshares.” This passage is duplicated in Micah 4:1–3. From the Septuagint.

§2:2–4 · Septuagint
Manuscript
Greek

ὅτι ἔσται ἐν ταῖς ἐσχάταις ἡμέραις ἐμφανὲς τὸ ὄρος κυρίου

English

In the last days the mountain of the house of the Lord shall be established as the highest of the mountains, and shall be lifted up above the hills; and all the nations shall flow to it. And many peoples shall come and say, “Come, let us go up to the mountain of the Lord, to the house of the God of Jacob, that he may teach us his ways.” And he shall judge between the nations and shall decide disputes for many peoples; and they shall beat their swords into plowshares, and their spears into pruning hooks; nation shall not lift up sword against nation, neither shall they learn war anymore.

Translator's Notes

  1. §2:4: The image of swords beaten into plowshares (μαχαίρας εἰς ἄροτρα) envisions a transformation of instruments of death into instruments of life. Joel 3:10 deliberately reverses this image, commanding “beat your plowshares into swords.” The tension between the two prophetic visions reflects the biblical tradition’s honest debate about the relationship between peace and justice.
