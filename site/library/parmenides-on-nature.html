On Nature: The Way of Truth

On Nature: The Way of Truth
Parmenides, c. 515-450 BCE
Diels-Kranz, Die Fragmente der Vorsokratiker

Introduction

Parmenides of Elea composed the first sustained philosophical argument in Western history: a hexameter poem in which a goddess reveals that true reality is eternal, unchanging, and indivisible. His distinction between the 'Way of Truth' and the 'Way of Opinion' established the framework for all subsequent metaphysics, from Plato's Theory of Forms to modern ontology.

§B2 · DK 28 B2
Greek

ἡ μὲν ὅπως ἔστιν τε καὶ ὡς οὐκ ἔστι μὴ εἶναι, Πειθοῦς ἐστι κέλευθος — Ἀληθείῃ γὰρ ὀπηδεῖ —, ἡ δ᾿ ὡς οὐκ ἔστιν τε καὶ ὡς χρεών ἐστι μὴ εἶναι, τὴν δή τοι φράζω παναπευθέα ἔμμεν ἀταρπόν.

English

The one way, that it is and that it is not possible for it not to be, is the path of Persuasion — for Truth attends it. The other, that it is not and that it is necessary for it not to be — this I tell you is a path wholly unknowable.

§B3 · DK 28 B3
Greek

τὸ γὰρ αὐτὸ νοεῖν ἐστίν τε καὶ εἶναι.

English

For thinking and being are the same.

§B8.1-6 · DK 28 B8
Greek

μόνος δ᾿ ἔτι μῦθος ὁδοῖο λείπεται ὡς ἔστιν· ταύτῃ δ᾿ ἐπὶ σήματ᾿ ἔασι πολλὰ μάλ᾿, ὡς ἀγένητον ἐὸν καὶ ἀνώλεθρόν ἐστιν, ἐστι γὰρ οὐλομελές τε καὶ ἀτρεμὲς ἠδ᾿ ἀτέλεστον· οὐδέ ποτ᾿ ἦν οὐδ᾿ ἔσται, ἐπεὶ νῦν ἔστιν ὁμοῦ πᾶν, ἕν, συνεχές.

English

Only one account of the way remains: that it is. And on this way there are signs in abundance — that Being is ungenerated and imperishable, whole, of one kind, unshaken, and complete. It never was nor will be, since it is now, all together, one, continuous.
