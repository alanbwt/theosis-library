Introduction

Mark 7:1–23 contains the dispute over purity laws, where Jesus declares all foods clean and relocates defilement from external contact to the human heart. From Codex Sinaiticus, fourth century.

§7:5–8 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και επερωτωσιν αυτον οι φαρισαιοι και οι γραμματεις

English

And the Pharisees and the scribes asked him, “Why do your disciples not walk according to the tradition of the elders, but eat with defiled hands?” And he said to them, “Well did Isaiah prophesy of you hypocrites, as it is written: This people honors me with their lips, but their heart is far from me.”

§7:14–15 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και προσκαλεσαμενος παλιν τον οχλον ελεγεν αυτοις

English

And he called the people to him again and said to them, “Hear me, all of you, and understand: There is nothing outside a person that by going into him can defile him, but the things that come out of a person are what defile him.”

Translator's Notes

  1. §7:15: This teaching effectively abolishes the Levitical distinction between clean and unclean. Mark’s editorial comment (“Thus he declared all foods clean,” 7:19) makes explicit what the saying implies. The internalization of purity would have enormous consequences for Gentile inclusion.
