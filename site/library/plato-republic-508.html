Introduction

At Republic 508a–509b, Socrates presents the analogy of the Sun: as the sun provides light and the power of sight in the visible world, so the Good provides truth and the power of knowing in the intelligible world. The Good is “beyond being.”

§508b–509b · Plato, Republic 508b8–509b9 (Burnet, OCT)
Manuscript
Greek

τοῦτο τοίνυν τὸ τὴν ἀλήθειαν παρέχον τοῖς γιγνωσκομένοις καὶ τῷ γιγνώσκοντι τὴν δύναμιν ἀποδιδόν

English

What the Good is in the intelligible realm in relation to intelligence and the things known, the sun is in the visible realm in relation to sight and the things seen. The Good gives to the objects of knowledge their truth and to the knower his power of knowing. And you must say that the Good provides not only their being known but also their very being and essence, though the Good itself is not being but is beyond being in dignity and power.

Translator's Notes

  1. §508b–509b: The phrase “beyond being” (ἐπέκεινα τῆς οὐσίας) became the single most important formula in Neoplatonic metaphysics. Plotinus identified the Good with the One, the first principle that transcends all determination, including being itself.
