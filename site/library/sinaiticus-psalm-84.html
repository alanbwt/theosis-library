Introduction

Psalm 84 is a pilgrimage psalm expressing yearning for the temple courts. Its imagery of the soul longing for the “living God” and finding strength in the journey has made it a beloved text in liturgical tradition.

§84:1—4, 10–12 · Codex Sinaiticus, Psalm 84
Manuscript
Greek

ὡς ἀγαπητὰ τὰ σκηνώματά σου Κύριε τῶν δυνάμεων

English

How lovely is your dwelling place, O Lord of hosts! My soul longs, yes, faints for the courts of the Lord; my heart and flesh sing for joy to the living God. For a day in your courts is better than a thousand elsewhere. I would rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God than dwell in the tents of wickedness.

Translator's Notes

  1. §84:2: The phrase “living God” (θεὸν ζῶντα) distinguishes Israel’s God from the idols of surrounding nations. The psalmist’s response is not intellectual but visceral—heart and flesh cry out, indicating that the whole person, not just the mind, participates in worship.
