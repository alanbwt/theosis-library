Introduction

1 Peter 1:3–12 opens with a blessing of God for the “living hope” of the resurrection, then describes faith as more precious than gold refined by fire. The letter addresses Christians facing persecution. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§1:3–5 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ευλογητος ο θεος και πατηρ του κυριου ημων ιησου χριστου

English

Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! According to his great mercy, he has caused us to be born again to a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, to an inheritance that is imperishable, undefiled, and unfading, kept in heaven for you, who by God’s power are being guarded through faith for a salvation ready to be revealed in the last time.

§1:6–9 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

εν ω αγαλλιασθε ολιγον αρτι ει δεον λυπηθεντες

English

In this you rejoice, though now for a little while, if necessary, you have been grieved by various trials, so that the tested genuineness of your faith—more precious than gold that perishes though it is tested by fire—may be found to result in praise and glory and honor at the revelation of Jesus Christ. Though you have not seen him, you love him. Though you do not now see him, you believe in him and rejoice with joy that is inexpressible and filled with glory, obtaining the outcome of your faith, the salvation of your souls.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1:7: The metallurgical image of faith tested by fire recurs throughout early Christian literature. The refining process does not create the gold but purifies it. The analogy implies that trials do not produce faith but reveal and purify faith already present.
