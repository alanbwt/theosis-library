Introduction

Judges 6 introduces Gideon, called by an angel of the Lord while threshing wheat in a winepress to hide it from the Midianites. Gideon’s reluctance and demand for signs make him a paradigm of the unlikely deliverer. From the Septuagint.

§6:12–15 · Septuagint
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ὤφθη αὐτῷ ἄγγελος κυρίου καὶ εἶπεν πρὸς αὐτόν

English

And the angel of the Lord appeared to him and said to him, “The Lord is with you, mighty man of valor.” And Gideon said to him, “Please, my lord, if the Lord is with us, why then has all this happened to us? And where are all his wonders that our fathers told us about? But now the Lord has forsaken us and given us into the hand of Midian.”

§6:36–40 · Septuagint
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ εἶπεν Γεδεων πρὸς τὸν θεόν

English

And Gideon said to God, “If you will save Israel by my hand, as you have said, behold, I am laying a fleece of wool on the threshing floor. If there is dew on the fleece alone, and it is dry on all the ground, then I shall know that you will save Israel by my hand, as you have said.” And it was so.

Translator's Notes

  1. §6:13: Gideon’s protest—“if the Lord is with us, why has all this happened?”—is one of the most honest expressions of the theodicy question in Scripture. The angel does not answer the question directly but instead commissions Gideon as the instrument of deliverance, transforming the questioner into the answer.
