Shahnameh: The Creation of the World and Gayomart
Ferdowsi, 940-1020 AD
British Library Or. 12688; Florence Magl. III.24

Introduction

The Shahnameh (Book of Kings) is the longest poem ever written by a single author — 50,000 couplets composed over 30 years. Ferdowsi's masterpiece preserves the mythological and historical memory of the Iranian world from the creation through the Arab conquest. It opens with the creation of the world, the first man Gayomart (cognate with the Avestan Gayomaretan), and establishes the cosmic struggle between the forces of light and darkness that structures all of Iranian civilization.

§2 · Shahnameh §2
Persian

نخست آفرینش خرد را شناس / نگهبان جان است و آن سه پاس

English

Know that the first creation was Wisdom — the guardian of the soul and its threefold watch.
