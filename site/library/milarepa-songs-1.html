The Hundred Thousand Songs: Song of Realization

The Hundred Thousand Songs: Song of Realization
Milarepa, 1052-1135 AD
Tsangnyon Heruka biography (1488)

Introduction

Milarepa (1052-1135) is Tibet's most famous saint — a murderer who through extreme asceticism and devotion to his teacher Marpa achieved complete enlightenment in a single lifetime. His songs, spontaneously composed in mountain caves, express the direct experience of emptiness and luminosity with an immediacy that has made them beloved across all Tibetan Buddhist traditions.

§1 · Mila §1
Tibetan

ང་ནི་རྗེ་བཙུན་མི་ལ་རས་པ་ཡིན། ངས་སྐུ་ལུས་འདི་ཡི་ཆོས་ཉིད་རྟོགས།

English

I am the cotton-clad Milarepa. In this very body I have realized the nature of reality.

§2 · Mila §2
Tibetan

ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་བརྗོད་པ། སྟོང་ཉིད་ཀྱི་དོན་ལ་བསམ་གཏན་བྱས།

English

I meditated on the meaning of emptiness, the perfection of wisdom beyond expression.
