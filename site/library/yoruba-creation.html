Ifa Corpus: The Creation of the World

Ifa Corpus: The Creation of the World
Yoruba Oral Tradition, pre-modern
Oral tradition; Abimbola transcription

Introduction

The Ifa divination corpus preserves the cosmology of the Yoruba people — one of the most sophisticated theological systems in Africa. The creation narrative tells how the supreme deity Olodumare delegated the creation of the physical world to lesser orishas, and how the divination god Orunmila brought the system of Ifa to guide humanity. This oral tradition, transmitted through thousands of memorized verses, was inscribed on the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage list.

Yoruba original and English translation.
§1 · Odu Eji Ogbe
Yoruba

Olodumare ni o ran Obatala lati da ile aye. O fun un ni igba iyanrin, adiye kan ati igi ogbo.

English

Olodumare sent Obatala to create the earth. He gave him a calabash of sand, a hen, and a palm nut.

§2 · Odu Eji Ogbe
Yoruba

Obatala da ile si ori omi. Adiye na si ke iyanrin ka. Nibi ti iyanrin ba de, ile ni o di.

English

Obatala poured the sand upon the water. The hen scratched the sand outward. Wherever the sand reached, it became dry land.
