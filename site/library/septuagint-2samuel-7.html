Introduction

2 Samuel 7 records the Davidic covenant, where God promises David an eternal dynasty: “Your house and your kingdom shall be established forever.” This oracle became the foundation of Jewish messianic expectation. From the Septuagint.

§7:12–16 · Septuagint
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ἔσται ἐὰν πληρωθῶσιν αἱ ἡμέραι σου

English

When your days are fulfilled and you lie down with your fathers, I will raise up your offspring after you, who shall come from your body, and I will establish his kingdom. He shall build a house for my name, and I will establish the throne of his kingdom forever. I will be to him a father, and he shall be to me a son. Your house and your kingdom shall be made sure forever before me. Your throne shall be established forever.

Translator's Notes

  1. §7:14: The declaration “I will be to him a father, and he shall be to me a son” became a key Christological text. Hebrews 1:5 quotes it directly as applying to Jesus. The father-son language establishes a relationship of intimacy and inheritance that the theosis tradition extends to all believers through adoption.
