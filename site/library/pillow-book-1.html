The Pillow Book: In Spring, the Dawn

The Pillow Book: In Spring, the Dawn
Sei Shonagon, c. 966-1025 AD
Maeda manuscript; Noin-bon

Introduction

Written around 1002 AD by a lady-in-waiting at the Heian court, the Pillow Book is the world's first great collection of personal essays — a genre Sei Shonagon essentially invented. Her opening passage on the beauties of the seasons remains the most famous prose in the Japanese language. The work's aesthetic sensibility, its attention to ephemeral beauty, helped define the Japanese concept of mono no aware.

§1 · Makura §1
Japanese

春はあけぼの。やうやう白くなりゆく山際、少し明かりて、紫だちたる雲の細くたなびきたる。

English

In spring, the dawn. The gradually whitening mountain rim, brightened slightly, and thin wisps of purplish cloud trailing across it.

§2 · Makura §2
Japanese

夏は夜。月のころはさらなり。闇もなほ、蛍の多く飛びちがひたる。

English

In summer, the nights. Not just when the moon is out, but even in the dark — the fireflies, crossing and recrossing each other's paths.
