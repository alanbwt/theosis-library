Introduction

Chapter 16 distinguishes divine from demonic natures. The divine nature leads to liberation; the demonic—characterized by hypocrisy, arrogance, and lust—leads to bondage.

§16:1-5 · Bhagavad Gita 16
Manuscript
Greek

śrī bhagavān uvāca | abhayaṃ sattva-saṃśuddhir jñāna-yoga-vyavasthitiḥ

English

The Blessed Lord said: Fearlessness, purity of heart, steadfastness in knowledge and yoga, charity, self-control, sacrifice, study of the scriptures, austerity, straightforwardness—these belong to one born with divine nature. Hypocrisy, arrogance, pride, anger, harshness, and ignorance belong to one born with demonic nature.

Translator's Notes

  1. §16:3: The daivī/āsurī distinction is not about cosmological beings but about psychological orientations. Everyone contains both tendencies. The chapter’s practical force is diagnostic: it invites the reader to recognize which qualities dominate their own character.
