The Death of Cuchulainn
Irish Mythographers, c. 9th-12th century AD
Book of Leinster

Introduction

The greatest Irish hero, bound by magical obligations, ties himself to a standing stone so he can die on his feet.

Translation

§1 · aided-cuchulinn §1

He bound himself to the pillar stone so that he might die standing, for he would not sit nor lie.
