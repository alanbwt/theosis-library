Heart Sutra: Form Is Emptiness

        

        
Nagarjuna (attributed), c. 150-250 AD

        

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
The Heart Sutra is the shortest and most important text in Mahayana Buddhism — a single page that distills the entire Perfection of Wisdom literature into its essence. It is recited daily in temples across China, Japan, Korea, Vietnam, and Tibet. The sutra teaches that emptiness is form, form is emptiness — and ends with a mantra in untranslated Sanskrit.

            §1 · Heart Sutra (Conze)
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
आर्यावलोकितेश्वरो बोधिसत्त्वो गम्भीरां प्रज्ञापारमिताचर्यां चरमाणो व्यवलोकयति स्म

          


          

            
English

            
The Bodhisattva Avalokitesvara, while practicing the deep practice of the Perfection of Wisdom, looked down.

          

        

        
        


          

            §2 · Heart Sutra
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
पञ्च स्कन्धास्तांश्च स्वभावशून्यान् पश्यति स्म

          


          

            
English

            
He saw that the five aggregates were empty of self-nature.

          

        

        
        


          

            §3 · Heart Sutra
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
इह शारिपुत्र रूपं शून्यता शून्यतैव रूपम्

          


          

            
English

            
Here, O Shariputra, form is emptiness, emptiness is form. Form is not different from emptiness, emptiness is not different from form. Whatever is form, that is emptiness. Whatever is emptiness, that is form. The same is true of feelings, perceptions, mental formations, and consciousness.

          

        

        
        


          

            §4 · Heart Sutra
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
इह शारिपुत्र सर्वधर्माः शून्यतालक्षणा अनुत्पन्ना अनिरुद्धा

          


          

            
English

            
Here, O Shariputra, all dharmas are marked with emptiness. They are neither produced nor destroyed, neither defiled nor undefiled, neither deficient nor complete.

          

        

        
        


          

            §5 · Heart Sutra
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
तस्माच्छारिपुत्र शून्यतायां न रूपं न वेदना न संज्ञा न संस्काराः

          


          

            
English

            
Therefore, O Shariputra, in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, no perception, no mental formations, no consciousness. There is no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind. No color, sound, smell, taste, touch, object of mind. No realm of sight, no realm of mind-consciousness. There is no ignorance and no extinction of ignorance. There is no old age and death and no extinction of old age and death. There is no suffering, no origin, no cessation, no path. There is no knowledge and no attainment, because there is nothing to attain.

          

        

        
        


          

            §6 · Heart Sutra
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
बोधिसत्त्वस्य प्रज्ञापारमितामाश्रित्य विहरति चित्तावरणः

          


          

            
English

            
The bodhisattva, depending on the Perfection of Wisdom, dwells with no obstructions of mind. Without obstructions, he has no fear. Far beyond all illusion, he attains complete nirvana.

          

        

        
        


          

            §7 (Mantra) · Heart Sutra
            

              
              
              
            

          


          

          

            
Sanskrit

            
गते गते पारगते पारसंगते बोधि स्वाहा

          


          

            
English

            
Therefore know the Perfection of Wisdom is the great mantra, the unequaled mantra, the mantra that calms all suffering. This is true, not false. So set forth this mantra: gate gate paragate parasamgate bodhi svaha — gone, gone, gone beyond, gone utterly beyond, awakening, hail!
