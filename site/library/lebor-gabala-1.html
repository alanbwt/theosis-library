Lebor Gabala Erenn: The Book of Invasions

Lebor Gabala Erenn: The Book of Invasions
Irish Scribes, c. 7th-12th century AD
Book of Leinster; Book of Ballymote

Introduction

The Lebor Gabala Erenn (Book of Invasions) is Ireland's national creation myth — a pseudo-historical account of the successive peoples who settled the island, from the antediluvian Cessair through the Fomorians, the Fir Bolg, and the divine Tuatha De Danann, to the mortal Gaels. It is the framework within which all Irish mythology is organized.

Old Irish original and English translation.
§1 · LGE §1
Old Irish

In principio fecit Deus celum et terram... Ériu, immorro, iar ndílinn, secht mbliadna fichet bái fás.

English

In the beginning God made heaven and earth... Ireland, moreover, after the flood, was waste for twenty-seven years.

§2 · LGE §7
Old Irish

Tuatha Dé Danann i n-insib tuaiscirt an domhain, oc foglaim druidechta ocus fessa ocus ammainsechta.

English

The Tuatha De Danann were in the northern islands of the world, learning druidry and knowledge and prophecy.
