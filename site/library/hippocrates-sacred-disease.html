Introduction

On the Sacred Disease is the Hippocratic treatise arguing that epilepsy has natural rather than divine causes. It is the founding document of rational medicine, rejecting supernatural explanations of illness in favor of empirical observation.

§Chapter 1 · Hippocratic Corpus, On the Sacred Disease 1
Manuscript
Greek

Περὶ μὲν τῆς ἱερῆς νούσου καλεομένης ὥδε ἔχει

English

I am about to discuss the disease called ‘sacred.’ It is not, in my opinion, any more divine or sacred than other diseases, but has a natural cause. Men regard its nature and cause as divine from ignorance and wonder, because it is not at all like other diseases. Those who first called it sacred were the sort of people we now call magicians and charlatans.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Ch. 1: This treatise represents a watershed in intellectual history: the explicit rejection of supernatural causation in favor of naturalistic explanation. The author does not deny the gods but insists that all diseases, including epilepsy, operate through natural mechanisms accessible to rational inquiry.
