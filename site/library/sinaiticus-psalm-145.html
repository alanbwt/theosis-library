Sinaiticus Psalm 145

Introduction

Psalm 145 is an acrostic hymn of praise declaring that God’s kingdom endures through all generations. It is the only psalm with the superscription “praise” (tehillah).

§145:1-7 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

ὑψώσω σε ὁ θεός μου ὁ βασιλεύς

English

I will extol you, my God and King, and bless your name forever and ever. Every day I will bless you and praise your name forever and ever. Great is the Lord, and greatly to be praised, and his greatness is unsearchable.

§145:8-13 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

οἰκτίρμων καὶ ἐλεήμων ὁ κύριος

English

The Lord is gracious and merciful, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love. The Lord is good to all, and his mercy is over all that he has made. Your kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and your dominion endures throughout all generations.

Translator's Notes

  1. §145:13: The everlasting kingdom anticipates Daniel’s vision of the eternal kingdom given to the “son of man.” Jewish and Christian eschatology both draw on this language of divine sovereignty without end.
