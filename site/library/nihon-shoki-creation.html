Introduction

The Nihon Shoki (Chronicles of Japan), completed in 720 AD, is the first official history of Japan. Its opening chapters preserve multiple variant accounts of creation — unique among world mythologies — presenting alternative versions side by side without choosing among them. The text establishes the theological foundation for imperial sovereignty through descent from Amaterasu.

Japanese original and English translation.
§1.1 · NS §1
Japanese

古天地未剖、陰陽不分。渾沌如鶏子、溟涬而含牙。

English

Of old, when heaven and earth were not yet separated, and yin and yang not yet divided, chaos was like a hen's egg, dim and dark, containing a germ.

§1.7 · NS §7
Japanese

天照大神者、光華明彩、照徹六合。

English

Amaterasu, the Great Deity Who Illuminates Heaven, was radiant in her brightness, filling the six quarters of the world with light.
