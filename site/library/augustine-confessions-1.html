§1.1.1 · Augustine, Confessions 1.1.1
Manuscript
Greek

Magnus es, domine, et laudabilis valde. Magna virtus tua et sapientiae tuae non est numerus

English

Great are you, O Lord, and greatly to be praised; great is your power, and your wisdom is beyond measure. And man, a small portion of your creation, desires to praise you—man, who bears about him his mortality, the witness of his sin. Yet man, this small portion of your creation, desires to praise you. You move us to delight in praising you, for you have made us for yourself, and our heart is restless until it rests in you.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1.1.1: The cor inquietum (restless heart) became one of the most influential metaphors in Western theology. Augustine locates the human problem not in ignorance (as the Greeks did) but in disordered desire—the heart seeks its rest in creatures rather than in the Creator.
