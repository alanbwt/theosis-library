Introduction

The Edict of Thessalonica (380 CE), issued by emperors Theodosius I, Gratian, and Valentinian II, established Nicene Christianity as the official state religion of the Roman Empire. It is the legal instrument that ended Rome’s centuries of religious pluralism.

§Cunctos populos · Codex Theodosianus XVI.1.2
Manuscript
Greek

Cunctos populos, quos clementiae nostrae regit temperamentum, in tali volumus religione versari, quam divinum Petrum apostolum tradidisse Romanis religio usque ad nunc ab ipso insinuata declarat.

English

It is our desire that all the peoples who are ruled by the administration of our clemency shall practice that religion which the divine Peter the Apostle transmitted to the Romans. We shall believe in the single deity of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, under the concept of equal majesty and of the Holy Trinity.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Cunctos populos: This edict is remarkable for its specificity: it names Nicene Trinitarianism as the only legal religion and brands all others as “demented and insane.” It effectively criminalizes both paganism and non-Nicene Christianity (Arianism), transforming theological orthodoxy into a legal category.
