Introduction

A hadith qudsi (sacred hadith) is a divine utterance conveyed through the Prophet but not part of the Quran. This celebrated example describes God's progressive intimacy with the devoted servant, culminating in a union of perception.

§Hadith Qudsi · Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 81, Hadith 38
Manuscript
Greek

كُنْتُ سَمْعَهُ الَّذِي يَسْمَعُ بِهِ وَبَصَرَهُ الَّذِي يُبْصِرُ بِهِ

English

God Most High said: "Whoever shows hostility to a friend of Mine, I declare war upon him. My servant draws near to Me through nothing more beloved to Me than what I have made obligatory upon him. And My servant continues to draw near to Me through voluntary acts until I love him. And when I love him, I become the hearing with which he hears, the sight with which he sees, the hand with which he grasps, and the foot with which he walks."

Translator's Notes

  1. §Hadith Qudsi: This hadith is the single most important text in Sufi theology. The phrase "I become his hearing and his sight" describes what the Sufis call fana (annihilation of the self in God) while remaining within orthodox hadith literature. Ibn Arabi built much of his metaphysics upon it. Opponents of Sufism insist the language is metaphorical, not ontological.
