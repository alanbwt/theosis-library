§15.24 · Analects, Book 15, Chapter 24
Manuscript
Greek

子贡问曰：有一言而可以终身行之者乎？子曰：其恕乎！己所不欲，勿施于人

English

Zigong asked: "Is there a single word that can serve as a guide for one's entire life?" The Master said: "Is it not shu—reciprocity? Do not impose on others what you yourself do not wish."

Translator's Notes

  1. §15.24: The negative formulation—"do not impose"—is sometimes contrasted with the positive Christian version ("do unto others"). Some scholars argue the negative form is more ethically cautious, as it prevents the imposition of one's own preferences on others. The passage is the clearest statement of Confucian ethics as fundamentally relational rather than rule-based.
