The Tel Dan Stele: 'House of David' in Stone

        
The Tel Dan Stele: 'House of David' in Stone

        
Mesha, King of Moab, c. 840 BCE

        
Israel Museum, Jerusalem

        
      


      

        
Introduction

        
The Tel Dan Stele, discovered in 1993 during excavations in northern Israel, sent shockwaves through the world of biblical archaeology. An Aramaic inscription on a basalt fragment contained the phrase bytdwd — 'House of David' — the first reference to King David found outside the Bible.
Before this discovery, some scholars argued that David was a mythological figure invented by later writers. The Tel Dan inscription, dated to the 9th century BCE (roughly 100-150 years after David's reign), proved that the Davidic dynasty was recognized by Israel's neighbors as a historical reality within living memory of David's descendants.

Translation

        
        

          §1 · tel-dan-stele
          
I killed Joram son of Ahab, king of Israel, and I killed Ahaziahu son of Joram, king of the House of David. And I set their towns into desolation. The fragment preserves the phrase BYTDWD — 'House of David' — the first and earliest extra-biblical reference to the Davidic dynasty.
