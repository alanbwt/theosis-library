Introduction

In Enneads IV.8.1, Plotinus describes the soul’s descent from the intelligible realm into the body. He begins with a famous autobiographical passage about being “roused from the body” into direct contact with the divine, then asks how the soul ever came to be embodied at all.

§IV.8.1 · Plotinus, Enneads IV.8.1 (Henry–Schwyzer, OCT)
Manuscript
Greek

πολλάκις ἐγειρόμενος εἰς ἐμαυτὸν ἐκ τοῦ σώματος

English

Many times it has happened: lifted out of the body into myself, becoming external to all other things, I have seen a beauty wonderfully great. I was certain then that I possessed the best of lives, having attained identity with the divine. Then, after that sojourn in the divine, falling back from intellect to reasoning, I ask myself how it is that I have now descended, and how the soul ever came to be within the body.

Translator's Notes

  1. §IV.8.1: This passage is the most explicitly autobiographical in the Enneads and describes what scholars term a mystical experience of henosis (union with the One). Porphyry reports that Plotinus achieved this state four times during the years of their association.
