Introduction

Revelation 19:11–21 depicts the rider on the white horse, called “Faithful and True” and “the Word of God,” who defeats the beast and the false prophet. The passage is the climactic battle of the Apocalypse. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§19:11–13 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και ειδον τον ουρανον ηνεωγμενον και ιδου ιππος λευκος

English

Then I saw heaven opened, and behold, a white horse! The one sitting on it is called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he judges and makes war. His eyes are like a flame of fire, and on his head are many diadems, and he has a name written that no one knows but himself. He is clothed in a robe dipped in blood, and the name by which he is called is The Word of God.

§19:15–16 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

και εκ του στοματος αυτου εκπορευεται ρομφαια οξεια

English

From his mouth comes a sharp sword with which to strike down the nations, and he will rule them with a rod of iron. He will tread the winepress of the fury of the wrath of God the Almighty. On his robe and on his thigh he has a name written, King of kings and Lord of lords.

Translator's Notes

  1. §19:13: The title “The Word of God” (ο λογος του θεου) connects this warrior figure to the Johannine Prologue (John 1:1). Yet the robe “dipped in blood” (from Isaiah 63:1–3) transforms the Logos from a cosmic principle into a figure of judgment. The blood may be his own—the Lamb’s sacrificial blood—rather than his enemies’.
