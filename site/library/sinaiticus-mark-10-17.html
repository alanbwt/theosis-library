§10:17-22 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ἐκπορευομένου αὐτοῦ εἰς ὁδόν

English

And as he was setting out on his journey, a man ran up and knelt before him and asked him, “Good Teacher, what must I do to inherit eternal life?” And Jesus said, “Why do you call me good? No one is good except God alone.” And Jesus, looking at him, loved him, and said, “You lack one thing: go, sell all that you have and give to the poor.” Disheartened by the saying, he went away sorrowful, for he had great possessions.

§10:23-27 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ περιβλεψάμενος ὁ Ἰησοῦς

English

And Jesus said to his disciples, “How difficult it will be for those who have wealth to enter the kingdom of God!” It is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for a rich person to enter the kingdom of God. They said, “Then who can be saved?” Jesus said, “With man it is impossible, but not with God.”

Translator's Notes

  1. §10:18: Jesus’ deflection—“Why do you call me good? No one is good except God alone”—has generated enormous christological debate. Arians cited it as proof Jesus denied his divinity. Orthodox interpreters read it as a pedagogical challenge forcing the man to consider what “good” truly means.
