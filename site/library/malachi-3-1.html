Introduction

Malachi 3:1–4 prophesies a messenger who will prepare the way before the Lord, followed by the Lord’s sudden arrival at his temple. The Gospels identify John the Baptist as this messenger.

Manuscript
Greek

ἰδοὺ ἐγὼ ἐξαποστέλλω τὸν ἄγγελόν μου καὶ ἐπιβλέψεται ὁδὸν πρὸ προσώπου μου

English

Behold, I send my messenger, and he will prepare the way before me. And the Lord whom you seek will suddenly come to his temple; and the messenger of the covenant in whom you delight, behold, he is coming. But who can endure the day of his coming? For he is like a refiner’s fire and like fullers’ soap.

Translator's Notes

  1. §3:1: Malachi (“my messenger”) is the last book of the prophets in the Christian Old Testament. Mark’s Gospel opens by combining this verse with Isaiah 40:3 to frame John the Baptist as the prophesied forerunner, creating a bridge between the testaments.
