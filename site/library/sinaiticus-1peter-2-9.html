Introduction

1 Peter 2:9–12 declares believers to be “a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation,” applying to the church the titles formerly reserved for Israel. The passage grounds Christian identity in election and vocation. From Codex Sinaiticus.

§2:9–10 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

υμεις δε γενος εκλεκτον βασιλειον ιερατευμα εθνος αγιον

English

But you are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own possession, that you may proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness into his marvelous light. Once you were not a people, but now you are God’s people; once you had not received mercy, but now you have received mercy.

§2:11–12 · Codex Sinaiticus
Manuscript
Greek

αγαπητοι παρακαλω ως παροικους και παρεπιδημους

English

Beloved, I urge you as sojourners and exiles to abstain from the passions of the flesh, which wage war against your soul. Keep your conduct among the Gentiles honorable, so that when they speak against you as evildoers, they may see your good deeds and glorify God on the day of visitation.

Translator's Notes

  1. §2:9: The phrase “royal priesthood” (βασιλειον ιερατευμα) quotes Exodus 19:6 from the Septuagint. It became the basis for the Protestant doctrine of the “priesthood of all believers”—the claim that all Christians, not only ordained clergy, have direct access to God.
