Introduction

Johannes Tauler (c. 1300-1361) was a German Dominican mystic and one of the great Rhineland preachers. His sermons on the birth of God in the soul extended Meister Eckhart's theology while remaining within orthodox bounds.

Translation

§Sermon on the Inner Birth · Tauler, Sermon 1

The eternal Word is spoken in every soul that is prepared to hear it. The Father speaks His Word in the ground of the soul, and there the Son is born. This birth does not happen once and then cease; it happens continually in every moment. If you could empty yourself perfectly of all created things and even of yourself, then this birth would be yours in its fullness. The whole of the spiritual life consists in making room for this birth. Strip away everything that is not God, and what remains is the place where God is born.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Sermon 1: Tauler's doctrine of the "birth of the Word in the soul" derives from Eckhart but is expressed in more pastorally accessible language. Where Eckhart could sound speculative and abstract, Tauler emphasizes the practical requirements: detachment, inner silence, and radical self-emptying. Martin Luther praised Tauler's sermons highly, finding in them a model of experiential theology.
