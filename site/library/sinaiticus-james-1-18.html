James 1:17-18: Brought Forth by the Word of Truth

John (attributed), c. 90–110 AD

Introduction

James 1:17–18 contains a striking claim about divine generation: God, of his own will, “brought us forth” (apekuēsen, ἀπεκύησεν) by the word of truth, that believers should be “a kind of firstfruits of his creatures.” The verb apokueo is a birth word—it means to give birth to, to bring forth from the womb. James applies it to God without hesitation: the Father of lights begets believers as his offspring through the instrumentality of truth.

The passage frames this divine birth within a theology of gifts. Every good gift descends from the Father of lights, in whom there is “no variation or shadow of turning.” God’s generative act is not arbitrary but flows from an unchanging divine nature that pours forth goodness. The designation of believers as “firstfruits” (aparchē, ἀπαρχή) implies that they are the first portion of a larger harvest—the initial installment of a redeemed creation, consecrated to God as the first sheaf was consecrated at the temple.

English

Of his own will he brought us forth (apekuēsen, ἀπεκύησεν) by the word of truth, that we should be a kind of firstfruits (aparchē, ἀπαρχή) of his creatures.

Translator's Notes

  1. §1:17: The phrase “Father of lights” (πατρὸς τῶν φώτων) is unique in the New Testament. The “lights” most likely refer to the celestial bodies—sun, moon, and stars—making God the father of the luminaries. The astronomical language of “variation” (παραλλαγή) and “shadow of turning” (τροπῆς ἀποσκίασμα) contrasts God’s constancy with the changeable heavens: the stars shift and the sun casts shadows, but the God who made them does not change.
  2. §1:18: The verb apekuēsen (ἀπεκύησεν) is used in verse 15 of sin “giving birth to” death. Here, by deliberate contrast, it is God who gives birth—not to death but to new life. The same biological process that produces death in the realm of sin produces life in the realm of grace. This is James’s version of the new birth theology found in John 3 and 1 Peter 1:23.
