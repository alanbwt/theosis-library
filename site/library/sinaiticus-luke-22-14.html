§22:14–20 · Codex Sinaiticus, Luke 22:14–20
Manuscript
Greek

καὶ ὅτε ἐγένετο ἡ ὥρα ἀνέπεσεν καὶ οἱ ἀπόστολοι σὺν αὐτῷ

English

And when the hour came, he reclined at table, and the apostles with him. And he said, ‘I have earnestly desired to eat this Passover with you before I suffer. For I tell you I will not eat it until it is fulfilled in the kingdom of God.’ And he took bread, and gave thanks and broke it and gave it to them, saying, ‘This is my body, which is given for you.’

Translator's Notes

  1. §22:19–20: The longer text of Luke’s institution narrative (including “which is given for you” and the cup saying) is present in Sinaiticus but absent from some Western manuscripts. This is one of the most significant textual disputes in the Gospels.
