Introduction

The Anabasis of Xenophon (c. 370 BCE) recounts the march of ten thousand Greek mercenaries deep into Persia and their harrowing retreat to the Black Sea. It is one of the great adventure narratives of antiquity and a primary source for Greek military practice.

§1.1.1–2 · Xenophon, Anabasis
Manuscript
Greek

Δαρείου καὶ Παρυσάτιδος γίγνονται παῖδες δύο

English

Of Darius and Parysatis were born two sons, the elder Artaxerxes and the younger Cyrus. When Darius fell sick and suspected the end of his life was near, he wished both his sons to be present.

§4.7.24–25 · Xenophon, Anabasis
Manuscript
Greek

θάλαττα θάλαττα

English

And then they came to the mountain. When the first men reached the summit and caught sight of the sea, a great cry went up. Xenophon and the rearguard, hearing it, thought that enemies were attacking. But when the shout grew louder and nearer, and those who kept coming up ran toward those who were shouting, and the cry became much louder as more and more men reached the top, it became clear that something extraordinary was happening. So Xenophon mounted his horse and rode forward, and presently they could hear the soldiers shouting: “The sea! The sea!”

Translator's Notes

  1. §4.7.24: The cry Thalatta! Thalatta! (Θάλαττα! Θάλαττα!) when the exhausted soldiers sight the Black Sea is one of the most famous moments in ancient literature. It represents the recognition that survival is possible—a homecoming not yet complete but suddenly imaginable.
