Introduction

Tertullian’s Apologeticum (197 CE) is the most forceful early Christian defense addressed to Roman magistrates. Writing from Carthage, Tertullian attacks the legal basis of persecution and argues that Christian growth under suffering proves divine backing.

§Chapter 50.13 · Tertullian, Apologeticum 50.13
Manuscript
Greek

Semen est sanguis Christianorum. Laudatis enim eos qui apud vos dolorem mortisque contemptum toleraverunt. Nostram vero obstinationem furorem vocatis.

English

The blood of the martyrs is the seed of the Church. You praise those among yourselves who endure pain and death. Yet our obstinacy in suffering you call madness. The very fact that we endure such things proves that we are not without the aid of God.

Translator's Notes

  1. §Chapter 50.13: The phrase “the blood of the martyrs is the seed of the Church” (semen est sanguis Christianorum) is Tertullian’s most famous line, though the common English rendering is a slight paraphrase. His original says “the blood of Christians is seed,” with the agricultural metaphor implying that persecution causes growth.
