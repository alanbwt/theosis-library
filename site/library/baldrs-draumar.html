Baldrs Draumar

Unknown,

Introduction

The Baldrs Draumar (“Baldr’s Dreams”) narrates Odin’s journey to Hel to learn the meaning of his son Baldr’s ominous dreams. By necromancy he raises a dead seeress who reveals that Baldr will be killed by his blind brother Hǫðr.

§Stanzas 7–9 · Poetic Edda, Baldrs Draumar 7–9
Manuscript
Greek

Óðinn reið fyr austan dúrr, þar er hann vissi vǫlvu leiði; nam hann vittugri valgaldr kveða, unz nauðig reis, nás orð of kvað.

English

Odin rode on to the eastern gate, where he knew the seeress’s grave to be. He began to speak a corpse-raising spell for the wise woman, until unwillingly she rose and spoke the words of the dead: “What man is this, unknown to me, who makes me travel the troublesome road?”

Translator's Notes

  1. §Stanzas 7–9: Odin’s necromancy (valgaldr, “corpse-spell”) demonstrates that even the chief god must resort to forbidden arts to obtain knowledge. The dead vǫlva’s resentment at being raised is a recurring motif—the dead possess knowledge the living need but do not wish to share it.
